Quick reference for the top 10 trade show cities in the US. Bookmark this page for your next trip.

Las Vegas, NV

Convention CenterLas Vegas Convention Center
Nearest AirportLAS (Harry Reid Intl)
Airport to Venue10 min / 3 miles
Hotel Range$80 - $300/night
Pro tip: Stay on the Strip near the Monorail for easy convention center access. Avoid taxi lines by using rideshare pickup areas. Book hotels early as rates spike during major shows like CES.

Orlando, FL

Convention CenterOrange County Convention Center
Nearest AirportMCO (Orlando Intl)
Airport to Venue15 min / 12 miles
Hotel Range$100 - $250/night
Pro tip: International Drive hotels are walking distance to OCCC. Rent a car if you want to explore beyond the convention area. Florida humidity means light fabrics are essential.

Chicago, IL

Convention CenterMcCormick Place
Nearest AirportORD (O'Hare) / MDW (Midway)
Airport to Venue30-45 min / 18 miles
Hotel Range$120 - $350/night
Pro tip: McCormick Place is massive. Wear comfortable shoes and allow extra time between halls. The Metra train stops right at the venue. Book South Loop hotels for shortest commute.

New York, NY

Convention CenterJacob K. Javits Center
Nearest AirportJFK / LGA / EWR
Airport to Venue45-75 min
Hotel Range$200 - $500/night
Pro tip: Stay in Hell's Kitchen or Midtown West for walking distance to Javits. Use the 7 train (Hudson Yards stop) or the NYC Ferry. Budget extra for dining and transport.

Atlanta, GA

Convention CenterGeorgia World Congress Center
Nearest AirportATL (Hartsfield-Jackson)
Airport to Venue20 min / 10 miles
Hotel Range$100 - $280/night
Pro tip: MARTA rail goes directly from the airport to the venue area. Downtown hotels along Centennial Olympic Park are most convenient. Great food scene in Midtown.

Dallas, TX

Convention CenterKay Bailey Hutchison Convention Center
Nearest AirportDFW / DAL (Love Field)
Airport to Venue25-35 min / 18 miles
Hotel Range$100 - $275/night
Pro tip: DART light rail connects the airport to downtown. Stay in the West End or downtown for walkable access. Texas heat in summer means lightweight clothing is a must.

San Francisco, CA

Convention CenterMoscone Center
Nearest AirportSFO (San Francisco Intl)
Airport to Venue30 min / 13 miles
Hotel Range$180 - $450/night
Pro tip: BART from SFO to Powell Station puts you blocks from Moscone. Hotels in SoMa are closest. Layer up as SF microclimates mean wide temperature swings throughout the day.

Houston, TX

Convention CenterGeorge R. Brown Convention Center
Nearest AirportIAH (George Bush) / HOU (Hobby)
Airport to Venue30-40 min / 22 miles
Hotel Range$90 - $250/night
Pro tip: METRORail runs from downtown to the convention center. The Houston Tunnel System connects downtown hotels and restaurants underground. Great for avoiding summer heat.

Denver, CO

Convention CenterColorado Convention Center
Nearest AirportDEN (Denver Intl)
Airport to Venue35-45 min / 24 miles
Hotel Range$110 - $300/night
Pro tip: The A Line train runs from DEN to Union Station in downtown, a short walk to the convention center. Altitude (5,280 ft) affects many visitors. Drink extra water and pace yourself.

Miami, FL

Convention CenterMiami Beach Convention Center
Nearest AirportMIA (Miami Intl)
Airport to Venue25-40 min / 12 miles
Hotel Range$150 - $400/night
Pro tip: Stay on Miami Beach near the convention center. Traffic to/from the mainland can be brutal during rush hour. Rideshare is generally easier than renting a car in the beach area.

Follow this timeline to ensure nothing falls through the cracks before your next trade show trip.

4 Weeks Before

  • Confirm booth registration and exhibitor badge
  • Book flights and hotel (book early for best rates)
  • Reserve rental car or arrange ground transportation
  • Order any remaining booth supplies or materials
  • Confirm freight shipping dates and carrier
  • Begin pre-show marketing outreach to prospects
  • Schedule meetings with key prospects and partners

2 Weeks Before

  • Finalize booth staff schedule and roles
  • Conduct booth staff training session
  • Print brochures, sell sheets, and business cards
  • Test all technology (laptops, tablets, presentations)
  • Confirm all reservations (hotel, flights, car)
  • Send pre-show emails to meeting confirmations
  • Pack promotional items and giveaways

1 Week Before

  • Ship booth materials to venue (confirm tracking)
  • Download exhibitor app and floor maps
  • Review show schedule for must-attend sessions
  • Prepare elevator pitch and key talking points
  • Set up lead capture system (test Scannly QR code)
  • Pack personal items using the checklist above
  • Charge all batteries and devices

Day Before Show

  • Arrive at venue for booth setup
  • Verify all shipped materials arrived safely
  • Set up display, lighting, and electronics
  • Test power, WiFi, and all connections
  • Walk the floor to scout competitor booths
  • Brief the team on goals and expectations
  • Get a good night's sleep

Show Day

  • Arrive 30-60 minutes before doors open
  • Final booth walkthrough and cleanliness check
  • Turn on all displays, demos, and lighting
  • Position team members in assigned roles
  • Actively engage attendees from the aisle
  • Capture and qualify leads consistently
  • Debrief with team at end of each day
  • Send same-day follow-up to hot leads
