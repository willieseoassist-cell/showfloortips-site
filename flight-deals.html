Money-Saving Tips for Trade Show Travel

Proven strategies to reduce your travel costs without sacrificing convenience or comfort.

1

Book 6-8 Weeks Early

Average savings of 20-30% on flights when booked 45-60 days before the show. Last-minute bookings during convention weeks face inflated prices across the board.

2

Tuesday/Wednesday Flights

Flying mid-week can save $50-150 per ticket compared to Monday or Friday departures. If your show schedule allows, shift travel days to capture savings.

3

Consider Nearby Airports

Chicago Midway vs O'Hare, Oakland vs SFO, Fort Lauderdale vs Miami. Secondary airports often have cheaper fares and shorter security lines.

4

Hotel Alternatives

Convention center hotel blocks vs a property 2 miles away can differ by $100+/night. A short rideshare adds up to far less than the premium rate.

5

Group Rates

Teams of 5+ should always negotiate group rates with hotels. Call the sales desk directly rather than booking online individually. Savings of 15-25% are typical.

6

Loyalty Programs

Use airline and hotel loyalty programs to accumulate points from business travel. A few shows per year can easily earn free flights or hotel nights for future trips.

7

Expense Before Year End

Maximize tax deductions by booking Q4 trade shows before December 31. Prepaying hotel and flight costs accelerates your business travel deductions.
