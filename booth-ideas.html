Design Principles

  • 1 Open the entrance. Remove barriers between the aisle and your booth. Attendees are more likely to enter an open space than step over a threshold.
  • 2 Lead with one message. Your primary value proposition should be readable from 20 feet away. Save secondary messaging for conversations.
  • 3 Use vertical space. Hanging signs and tall backwalls create visibility above the crowd, especially in dense show floors.
  • 4 Light it well. Good lighting directs attention to products and graphics. Spotlights, backlighting, and ambient LEDs work together.
  • 5 Plan the flow. Guide visitors through a natural path: attract, engage, inform, capture. Each zone should serve a purpose.
  • 6 Include storage. Every booth needs a place for personal items, extra literature, and supplies. A hidden closet keeps the space clean.

Common Mistakes

  • 1 Too much text on graphics. Large paragraphs on booth walls go unread. Use short headlines and let your team deliver the details.
  • 2 Blocking the entrance. Tables stretched across the front create a wall between you and attendees. Move tables to the side or replace with counters.
  • 3 Ignoring lighting. Convention hall lighting is flat and unflattering. Without your own lighting, your booth will look washed out.
  • 4 No lead capture plan. Collecting business cards in a fishbowl is not a strategy. Use digital lead capture to qualify and follow up fast.
  • 5 Overspending on giveaways. Expensive swag that has nothing to do with your product attracts the wrong crowd. Spend on targeted follow-up instead.
  • 6 Staff sitting behind the table. Your team should be standing at the front of the booth, engaging passersby, not seated behind a barrier.
