How to Choose a Lead Capture App

The right lead capture app depends on your team size, trade show frequency, existing tech stack, and budget. Here is a framework for making the decision.

1. Start with Your CRM

The most important factor is whether the app integrates with your existing CRM. Leads that sit in a spreadsheet instead of flowing directly into Salesforce or HubSpot lose value quickly. Prioritize apps with native integrations over those that rely solely on CSV export.

2. Consider Your Trade Show Volume

If you exhibit at 1-3 shows per year, per-show pricing may be fine. If you exhibit at 10+ shows annually, a monthly subscription model like Scannly or QuickTapSurvey is far more economical. Calculate your annual cost under each pricing model before committing.

3. Test Offline Mode

Convention center Wi-Fi is notoriously unreliable. Any lead capture app you choose must work fully offline and sync data when connectivity returns. Test this before the show -- download the app, turn off Wi-Fi, and try scanning. Apps that require a connection will cost you leads at the worst possible moment.

4. Evaluate Scanning Speed

During peak booth traffic, you need to capture a lead in under 10 seconds. The best apps scan a badge and populate the contact record almost instantly. If an app requires multiple steps or manual data entry for each lead, your team will fall behind during busy periods.

5. Look for Qualification Features

Scanning a badge captures contact information, but qualification turns a contact into an actionable lead. Look for apps that let you add notes, tags, lead scores, and custom fields immediately after scanning. This context is invaluable when your sales team follows up after the show.

  • Hot/warm/cold lead scoring
  • Custom tags (product interest, budget, timeline)
  • Voice or text notes per lead
  • Photo capture for business cards or product configurations
