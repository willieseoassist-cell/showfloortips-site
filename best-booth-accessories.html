Detailed Reviews

1

LED Booth Lighting Kits

$30 - $200

Proper lighting is one of the most impactful and affordable upgrades you can make. LED spotlights and strip lights highlight products, illuminate signage, and create a welcoming atmosphere that draws attendees in. Battery-powered options eliminate the need for costly electrical hookups.

  • Adjustable brightness and color temperature
  • Battery or plug-in options available
  • Clip-on, magnetic, and track mount styles
  • Lightweight and portable
  • Energy efficient LED technology
  • Easy setup without tools
2

Trade Show Flooring & Mats

$50 - $400

Standing on concrete for 8+ hours takes a toll. Interlocking foam tiles or rollable carpet provide comfort for your team and create a defined, professional space. Custom-printed floor graphics can reinforce your brand from every angle.

  • Anti-fatigue foam construction
  • Interlocking tiles for easy assembly
  • Custom branding options available
  • Beveled edges prevent tripping
  • Lightweight and reusable
  • Multiple color and finish options

Literature Racks & Brochure Holders

$25 - $150

Keep brochures, sell sheets, and business cards organized and accessible. Portable literature racks fold flat for shipping and set up in seconds. Multi-pocket designs let you display different materials for different audience segments.

  • Collapsible for easy transport
  • Multiple pocket configurations
  • Acrylic, metal, and fabric options
  • Countertop and floor-standing models
  • Adjustable pocket widths
  • Lightweight aluminum frames

iPad & Tablet Stands

$30 - $180

Tablets are versatile booth tools for product demos, lead capture forms, surveys, and interactive presentations. A secure, professional stand keeps them at the right height and angle while deterring theft. Locking enclosures add security at busy shows.

Power Strips & Extension Cords

$15 - $60

Convention center outlets are never where you need them. Heavy-duty power strips with surge protection keep your monitors, laptops, lighting, and charging stations running safely. Always bring more outlets than you think you'll need.

  • Surge protection rated

  • Flat plug design for tight spaces
  • 15-25 foot cord lengths
  • Heavy-duty 12-gauge wire
  • Multiple outlet configurations

  • Reusable for multiple shows

Buying Guide: What to Prioritize

If you're building your booth accessory kit from scratch, prioritize these items in order of impact:

  • Lighting first. Nothing transforms a booth more dramatically per dollar spent. Even a $40 set of LED spotlights makes a noticeable difference in how your booth looks from the aisle.
  • Power and cables second. You can't run your booth without reliable power, and loose cables are both unprofessional and a liability. Budget $50-75 for power strips, extensions, and cable covers.
  • Flooring third. Anti-fatigue mats keep your team energized through long show days. Your staff's comfort directly impacts their performance and engagement with attendees.
  • Organization last. Literature racks, tablet stands, and storage solutions round out a professional setup. These are the details that separate experienced exhibitors from first-timers.

Pro Tips from Experienced Exhibitors

  • Pack a "show day" box with tape, zip ties, scissors, markers, pain relievers, breath mints, phone chargers, and snacks. You'll need it.
  • Label everything with your company name and booth number. Items get misplaced during setup and teardown constantly.
  • Bring 50% more supplies than you think you need. You'll use them or a neighboring exhibitor will thank you.
  • Test everything before you ship. Plug in every light, check every cable, and verify every piece of equipment works.
  • Invest in quality cases. A good shipping case pays for itself after 2-3 shows by protecting your equipment and making setup faster.
