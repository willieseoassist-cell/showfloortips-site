LEAP 2026 vs GITEX Global 2026: Middle East Mega Tech Events Compared

ShowFloorTips · February 11, 2026

The Middle East has become one of the fastest-growing technology hubs on the planet, and two events sit at the very top of the region's conference calendar: LEAP in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, and GITEX Global in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. Both attract more than 200,000 attendees. Both draw the world's largest technology companies. And both promise exhibitors and visitors a front-row seat to the deals, announcements, and partnerships shaping the future of technology across the Gulf Cooperation Council, Africa, and South Asia.

But despite the surface similarities, LEAP 2026 and GITEX Global 2026 are fundamentally different events with different histories, different strategic emphases, and different value propositions. If you are an exhibitor deciding where to allocate your budget, a startup founder choosing where to pitch, or a technology executive planning your event calendar, this head-to-head comparison will give you the data and context you need to make the right call.

LEAP 2026: The Profile

LEAP takes place April 13 through 16, 2026, in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. Now in its fifth year, LEAP has grown at a pace that few events in any industry can match. What began as an ambitious bet by the Saudi government to create a world-class technology showcase has become one of the three largest tech events on Earth, sitting alongside CES and Mobile World Congress in terms of sheer scale.

LEAP 2026 is expected to attract more than 200,000 attendees, feature 1,800 global tech brands on the exhibition floor, and host 600 startups in its dedicated DeepTech startup zone. The event spans four days across the Riyadh Exhibition and Convention Center, one of the newest and most modern venue complexes in the Middle East. The show floor is organized into thematic zones covering artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, fintech, smart cities, health tech, sustainability, and enterprise computing.

1,800+
Global tech brands exhibiting at LEAP 2026 across AI, cybersecurity, fintech, and more

LEAP's rapid rise is inseparable from Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 initiative. The Saudi government has committed hundreds of billions of dollars to technology investment, digital transformation, and startup ecosystem development. LEAP is the flagship platform where that investment is on display. Government agencies like the Communications, Space & Technology Commission and the Saudi Data & AI Authority are not just sponsors but active participants, making announcements, signing deals, and recruiting talent on the show floor.

The attendee profile at LEAP skews toward decision-makers. Saudi Arabia's position as the largest economy in the GCC means that buyers attending LEAP often have significant procurement budgets. Enterprise deals, government contracts, and investment commitments are regularly announced during the event. The 2025 edition generated over $14 billion in announced deals and investments, a figure that organizers expect to exceed in 2026.

Startup Ecosystem at LEAP

LEAP's startup component is among the most compelling reasons to attend. With 600 startups represented, the DeepTech zone features companies from across the Middle East, North Africa, and South Asia, alongside international startups looking to enter the Saudi market. LEAP offers dedicated pitch competitions, investor matchmaking sessions, and accelerator showcases. For early-stage founders, the combination of high-net-worth investors, government-backed venture funds, and corporate venture arms makes LEAP one of the highest-ROI startup events in the world.

GITEX Global 2026: The Profile

GITEX Global has been the anchor technology event for the Middle East for over four decades. What started in 1981 as the Gulf Information Technology Exhibition has evolved into one of the world's largest and most internationally diverse tech events. GITEX Global 2026 marks a major milestone: the event is moving to Expo City Dubai, the legacy site of Expo 2020, which offers state-of-the-art infrastructure across a sprawling campus designed for exactly this kind of mega-event.

GITEX Global 2026 is expected to attract more than 200,000 attendees from over 180 countries, making it one of the most internationally diverse technology gatherings anywhere. The event runs across multiple days at Expo City Dubai and encompasses several co-located shows, including GITEX Impact (focused on sustainability and social impact technology), Expand North Star (one of the world's largest startup events), GITEX Digi Health, and Global DevSlam (for developers and open-source communities).

180+
Countries represented at GITEX Global, making it one of the most internationally diverse tech events on Earth

The move to Expo City Dubai is significant. The venue offers more than 600,000 square meters of event-ready space, climate-controlled pavilions, integrated transportation including a direct metro line, and the architectural grandeur that defined Expo 2020. For exhibitors, the upgraded venue means better logistics, more modern booth infrastructure, and a visitor experience that matches the premium positioning of the event.

GITEX Global's strength lies in its breadth. While LEAP is tightly focused on enterprise technology and government-driven digital transformation, GITEX covers the full technology spectrum: consumer electronics, telecommunications, enterprise software, cybersecurity, AI, blockchain, fintech, gaming, and more. The event attracts a wider range of attendees, from C-suite executives and government officials to developers, students, and tech enthusiasts.

Expand North Star: The Startup Engine

GITEX Global's startup component, Expand North Star, has grown into one of the largest startup events in the world in its own right. Previously held as a separate event at Dubai Harbour, Expand North Star is now co-located with GITEX Global at Expo City Dubai. The startup event features thousands of startups, hundreds of investors, pitch competitions with significant prize money, and dedicated pavilions organized by country and sector. For startups targeting international markets beyond the GCC, Expand North Star offers unmatched geographic diversity among its investor and partner base.

Location and Venue: Riyadh vs Dubai

The venue difference between these two events is about to become much more interesting. LEAP takes place at the Riyadh Exhibition and Convention Center, a purpose-built facility that has expanded rapidly to accommodate the show's growth. Riyadh itself is undergoing a historic transformation, with mega-projects like NEOM, The Line, and the Riyadh Metro reshaping the city's infrastructure and international profile. Attending LEAP means experiencing a city in the middle of the most ambitious urban transformation on Earth.

GITEX Global's move to Expo City Dubai represents a major upgrade from its long-time home at the Dubai World Trade Centre. Expo City offers a campus-style experience with multiple interconnected pavilions, outdoor event spaces, direct metro access, and the kind of architectural statement-making that Dubai does better than almost any city. Dubai itself needs no introduction: it is one of the world's most accessible cities, with visa-free or visa-on-arrival access for visitors from most countries, world-class hotels at every price point, and a logistics infrastructure built for exactly this kind of international event.

"The Middle East is no longer a secondary market for technology. It is where the next decade of digital infrastructure will be built, and both LEAP and GITEX are the front doors to that opportunity."

-- Regional technology investment analyst

For international visitors, Dubai generally offers an easier travel experience. Dubai International Airport is one of the busiest in the world, with direct flights from virtually every major city. Riyadh is catching up quickly, with the new King Salman International Airport under construction and Saudi Arabia's liberalized visa regime making entry significantly easier than it was even two years ago. However, Dubai still holds the edge in terms of hotel inventory, nightlife, dining options, and the general ease of doing business as a visiting international professional.

Attendance and Audience Quality

Both events claim 200,000 or more attendees, but the composition of those audiences differs meaningfully. LEAP's audience is more concentrated in enterprise technology and government procurement. Saudi Arabia is the largest economy in the Middle East, and the Saudi government is the largest technology buyer in the region. Attending LEAP puts you in front of the people who control those budgets. The attendee mix includes CTOs, CIOs, and procurement directors from Saudi ministries, state-owned enterprises, and the country's rapidly growing private sector.

GITEX Global's audience is broader and more internationally diverse. With 180 or more countries represented, GITEX draws a wider range of personas: enterprise buyers, yes, but also telecom operators from Africa, fintech founders from South Asia, gaming companies from Southeast Asia, and developer communities from everywhere. This diversity is a strength if your product or service has global applicability. It can be a weakness if you are looking for highly targeted, high-value conversations with a specific buyer profile.

Key TakeawayLEAP delivers a more concentrated, procurement-heavy audience with direct ties to Saudi government spending. GITEX delivers greater international diversity and a broader cross-section of the global technology industry. Your ideal audience should determine your choice.

Cost Comparison: What Will You Actually Spend?

The total cost of exhibiting at either event extends far beyond the booth rental. Booth costs at LEAP range from approximately $15,000 for a basic shell scheme in a secondary zone to $80,000 or more for a premium island booth in a primary hall. GITEX Global's booth pricing is similar in range, starting around $12,000 and climbing well above $100,000 for large, premium-positioned stands. Both events offer startup packages at significantly lower price points, typically in the $3,000 to $8,000 range for a small stand with basic furnishings.

The real cost differential lies in travel and accommodation. Dubai hotel rates during GITEX week spike significantly, but the city's enormous hotel inventory means you can still find options across a wide price range. Riyadh's hotel inventory is growing but remains more constrained during LEAP, which can push nightly rates to $400 or more for a mid-range business hotel within reasonable distance of the venue. Airfare to both cities from major hubs in Europe, Asia, and North America is generally comparable.

Hidden Costs to Consider

  • Booth construction and logistics: Both cities have mature exhibition services ecosystems, but custom booth builds in Riyadh can be more expensive due to higher labor costs and a newer supplier market.
  • Staff travel: If your team is based in Europe, Dubai is typically a shorter and cheaper flight. If your team is based in South or Southeast Asia, both cities are similarly accessible.
  • Entertainment and hospitality: Client dinners and hospitality events are a critical part of trade show ROI. Dubai offers more options for client entertainment, while Riyadh's dining and hospitality scene is improving rapidly but remains more conservative.
  • Visa costs and processing time: Saudi Arabia's eVisa is now available to visitors from many countries, but processing can still be slower than the UAE's seamless visa-on-arrival system for most nationalities.

Industry Focus: Where Each Event Excels

LEAP's thematic zones are organized around the pillars of Saudi Arabia's digital transformation agenda. Artificial intelligence gets enormous floor space, reflecting the kingdom's multi-billion-dollar investments in AI infrastructure, including the recently announced $40 billion AI investment fund. Cybersecurity is another major focus, driven by the need to secure the vast new digital infrastructure being built across the country. Fintech, health tech, and smart city technology round out the primary zones, all directly tied to active government procurement programs.

GITEX Global covers a wider technology landscape. In addition to the enterprise and government technology that dominates LEAP, GITEX features significant representation from telecommunications operators, consumer electronics brands, gaming and esports companies, and blockchain and Web3 projects. The co-located events within GITEX expand the scope even further: GITEX Impact covers sustainability technology, Global DevSlam draws developer communities, and GITEX Digi Health focuses on healthcare innovation.

$14B+
In deals and investments announced at LEAP 2025, with expectations to exceed that figure at LEAP 2026

For exhibitors with a narrowly defined technology offering, LEAP's focused zones make it easier to reach the right audience. For exhibitors with a platform or service that cuts across multiple industries, GITEX's breadth provides more diverse lead generation opportunities.

Networking and Dealmaking Dynamics

The networking culture at the two events reflects the broader business cultures of their host countries. LEAP's networking is heavily structured around government-facilitated programs. The Saudi government actively connects international technology providers with domestic buyers through curated matchmaking sessions, ministerial roundtables, and invitation-only dinners. If you are selling to the Saudi government or its vast ecosystem of state-owned enterprises, LEAP's networking infrastructure is purpose-built to facilitate those connections.

GITEX Global's networking is more organic and market-driven. The event's size and diversity mean that serendipitous connections are common, but finding your ideal buyer in a crowd of 200,000 requires careful pre-event planning. GITEX offers various networking tools, including an AI-powered matchmaking platform, hosted buyer programs, and VIP lounges, but the sheer volume of attendees can make targeted networking more challenging than at LEAP.

"At LEAP, you know exactly who you are meeting and why. At GITEX, the unexpected conversations often turn out to be the most valuable. Both approaches work, but they require different preparation strategies."

-- Enterprise sales director, global SaaS company

Side Events and After-Hours

Both events have robust side-event ecosystems, but they differ in character. LEAP's side events tend to be more formal: government-hosted receptions, embassy dinners, and invitation-only investor gatherings. GITEX's side events span a wider range, from corporate-sponsored parties and product launches to developer meetups, hackathons, and informal networking at Dubai's restaurants and hotels. For teams that do their best relationship-building in less formal settings, GITEX's after-hours ecosystem is significantly more developed.

Startup and Innovation Ecosystem

Both events are serious contenders for startups, but the opportunity profiles differ. LEAP's DeepTech zone features 600 startups and is heavily oriented toward companies building enterprise technology, AI, and deep-tech solutions. The investor audience at LEAP includes Saudi Arabia's massive sovereign wealth fund ecosystem (including PIF and its subsidiaries), regional venture capital firms, and corporate venture arms of major Saudi and Gulf companies. The average check size from these investors tends to be large, making LEAP particularly attractive for growth-stage startups seeking Series A and beyond.

GITEX's Expand North Star is larger in absolute terms, with more than 1,800 startups in recent editions. The investor base is more internationally diverse, with venture capital firms from Silicon Valley, Europe, Asia, and Africa alongside regional investors. Expand North Star is also more accessible to earlier-stage startups, with seed-stage pitch competitions and accelerator showcases that welcome companies at the pre-revenue stage. For first-time founders or early-stage companies looking for initial traction and visibility, Expand North Star offers a lower barrier to entry.

Travel and Logistics

Getting to and from these events is a practical consideration that should not be underestimated, especially for teams shipping booth equipment and marketing materials.

Dubai is one of the world's most connected cities by air. Emirates and flydubai together serve more than 250 destinations, and the Dubai metro's direct connection to Expo City Dubai means that attendees staying in most parts of the city can reach the venue without a car. The city's taxi and ride-hailing infrastructure is excellent, and the hotel booking ecosystem is mature and competitive.

Riyadh's connectivity is improving rapidly. Saudi Arabian Airlines, flynas, and a growing number of international carriers serve King Khalid International Airport, and the forthcoming King Salman International Airport will significantly expand capacity. However, Riyadh's public transportation infrastructure is still developing. The Riyadh Metro is under construction and partially operational, but most attendees will rely on taxis, ride-hailing apps (Uber and Careem), or shuttles provided by the event organizers to travel between hotels and the venue.

Key TakeawayDubai currently offers superior transportation infrastructure and a more seamless visitor experience. Riyadh is investing heavily to close the gap and offers the excitement of a city undergoing rapid transformation, but requires more logistical planning from exhibitors and attendees.

Content and Conference Programming

Both events invest heavily in their conference programs, bringing world-class speakers and thought leaders to their stages. LEAP's keynote lineup in recent years has featured heads of state, Fortune 500 CEOs, and leading AI researchers. The conference tracks are organized around the same thematic zones as the exhibition floor, ensuring tight alignment between the content programming and the exhibitor presence.

GITEX Global's conference programming is similarly impressive in terms of speaker caliber but covers a broader range of topics. The co-located events each have their own conference tracks, which means that a GITEX attendee can move between AI and machine learning sessions, sustainability workshops, developer talks, and health tech panels all within the same event campus. This breadth is a significant advantage for attendees looking to explore multiple areas of technology innovation.

The Verdict: Which Event Should You Choose?

Choose LEAP 2026 If:

  • Your primary market is Saudi Arabia or you are actively pursuing Saudi government contracts and enterprise deals
  • You are building deep-tech or AI solutions and want access to the world's most actively investing sovereign wealth ecosystem
  • You prefer structured, government-facilitated networking with pre-qualified buyers
  • You want to establish your brand in the fastest-growing technology market in the Middle East
  • You are a growth-stage startup (Series A+) seeking large investment checks from regional investors
  • Your product aligns with Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 technology priorities

Choose GITEX Global 2026 If:

  • You need maximum international exposure across 180+ countries and diverse emerging markets
  • Your technology platform serves multiple industries and you want to generate leads across sectors
  • You are an early-stage startup looking for visibility, initial traction, and access to a diverse investor pool
  • You prefer a more dynamic, market-driven networking environment with robust after-hours programming
  • You want the logistical convenience of Dubai's world-class infrastructure and ease of international access
  • You are interested in adjacent sectors like gaming, blockchain, developer tools, or sustainability technology

The Bottom Line

There is no wrong answer here. Both LEAP and GITEX Global are world-class technology events that belong on any serious technology company's event calendar. If your budget allows, exhibiting at both gives you the best of both worlds: LEAP for deep Saudi and GCC market penetration in April, and GITEX for broader international reach in October. The six-month gap between the events makes this logistically feasible for most organizations.

If you must choose one, the decision comes down to market focus. LEAP is the better event for companies whose growth strategy centers on Saudi Arabia and the GCC's government-driven digital transformation. GITEX Global is the better event for companies pursuing a broader international strategy across the Middle East, Africa, and South Asia.

Both are must-attend events for companies serious about the Middle East technology market. If forced to choose one, let your primary customer geography be the deciding factor.
