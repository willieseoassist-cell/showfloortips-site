How Much Does It Cost to Exhibit at SHOT Show 2026?

ShowFloorTips Editorial February 11, 2026

SHOT Show (Shooting, Hunting and Outdoor Trade Show) is the largest and most important trade show for the firearms, ammunition, hunting, and outdoor recreation industries. Organized by the National Shooting Sports Foundation (NSSF), SHOT Show 2026 took place January 20-23 at The Venetian Expo and Caesars Forum in Las Vegas. With over 60,000 attendees and 2,500+ exhibiting companies, SHOT Show is where the industry does business. Here is what it costs to exhibit.

Booth Space Costs

SHOT Show pricing is straightforward with published rates and NSSF member discounts:

  • Standard rate: $43 per net square foot
  • NSSF Patron Member rate: $33 per sq ft ($10/sq ft discount)
  • NSSF Supporting/Associate Member rate: $38 per sq ft ($5/sq ft discount)
  • Minimum booth size: 100 sq ft (10x10)
  • Corner booth surcharge: $950 per corner
  • Island booth surcharge: $4,500
  • Peninsula booth surcharge: $1,500
  • New Exhibitor Package fee: $1,895 (one-time for first-time exhibitors)

Example Booth Costs

  • 10x10 (standard rate): $4,300 + $1,895 new exhibitor fee = $6,195 first year
  • 10x10 (Patron Member): $3,300 + $1,895 = $5,195 first year
  • 10x20 (standard rate): $8,600
  • 20x20 island (standard rate): $17,200 + $4,500 island fee = $21,700

Supplier Showcase Alternative

Companies selling to manufacturers rather than dealers can exhibit in the Supplier Showcase at lower cost:

  • Supplier Showcase booth package: $5,750
  • Corner surcharge: $625
  • NSSF member discount: $500 off

Booth Design and Construction

  • Pop-up display: $500 - $2,000
  • Custom 10x10 with product cases: $8,000 - $25,000
  • Custom 20x20 island: $35,000 - $90,000
  • Rental booth: $3,500 - $14,000 for 10x10

Firearms and optics exhibitors typically invest in secure, locked display cases and require additional insurance for high-value inventory on the show floor.

Drayage and Material Handling

  • Standard drayage rate: $155 - $205 per CWT
  • Typical 10x10 booth (800-1,200 lbs): $1,240 - $2,460
  • Typical 20x20 booth (2,500-4,000 lbs): $3,875 - $8,200

Electrical and Internet

  • Standard electrical (500W): $350 - $800
  • 20A/120V service: $600 - $1,200
  • Dedicated Wi-Fi: $800 - $2,200

Travel and Lodging

  • Hotel (4 nights): $1,000 - $2,400 per person
  • Flights: $300 - $700 per person
  • Meals: $60 - $130 per person per day
  • Ground transportation: $150 - $350 per person

For a team of four, travel costs range from $7,000 to $16,000. SHOT Show occurs in late January, and Las Vegas hotels near The Venetian command premium rates.

Marketing and Security

  • Pre-show email outreach: $500 - $1,500
  • Printed materials: $300 - $1,000
  • Product display insurance: $500 - $2,000
  • Giveaways: $500 - $2,500
  • Lead retrieval: $300 - $500
  • Carpet (850+ sq ft discount rate): $2.63/sq ft

First-time exhibitors should budget $20,000 - $35,000 for a 10x10 booth. NSSF members can save $1,000+ on space alone. Established brands with 20x20 islands typically spend $80,000 - $200,000.

Tips to Reduce Your SHOT Show Costs

  1. Join NSSF: Member discounts of $5-10/sq ft can save more than the cost of membership.
  2. Consider the Supplier Showcase: At $5,750, it is the most affordable way to exhibit at SHOT Show.
  3. Rent your booth: Save 40-60% over custom construction.
  4. Ship early: Avoid The Venetian's overtime drayage surcharges.
  5. Book hotels through the SHOT Show block: Negotiated rates can save $50-100/night.
