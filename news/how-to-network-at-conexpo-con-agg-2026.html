CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 runs March 3 through 7 at the Las Vegas Convention Center and Festival Grounds, and it is the single most important networking event in the construction industry this year. With more than 2,000 exhibitors spread across 2.9 million square feet of indoor and outdoor space, this is North America's largest construction trade show — and the sheer scale of it can be overwhelming if you walk in without a plan.

This guide gives you a concrete, actionable framework for turning those five days into career-changing connections. Whether you are an equipment dealer, a general contractor, a technology vendor, or a fleet manager, the strategies below will help you navigate the massive show floor efficiently, prioritize the right conversations, and convert brief booth interactions into lasting professional relationships.

Key Stat

CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 features 2,000+ exhibitors, 2.9 million square feet of exhibit space across multiple halls and outdoor areas, and draws over 130,000 professionals from around the world. Registration is officially open with multiple ticket tiers available.

1. Why CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 Is a Must-Attend for Networking

CONEXPO-CON/AGG is not just about seeing machines. It is the gravitational center for the construction industry, pulling decision-makers from every segment — earthmoving, underground construction, concrete, lifting, demolition, paving, and beyond — into one place for one week. The show draws attendance from equipment manufacturers, rental companies, fleet operators, general contractors, subcontractors, engineering firms, technology startups, government agencies, and industry associations.

What makes this edition particularly significant for networking is the convergence of trends: autonomous equipment (like Bluelight's autonomous soil compaction demonstrations), AI-driven project management (with AWS and Autodesk presenting on the Ground Breakers Stage), electrification of heavy equipment, and the ongoing labor shortage that is pushing the industry toward new partnerships. These themes create natural conversation starters that transcend the usual "what do you do?" ice-breakers.

The venue itself is massive. The Las Vegas Convention Center spans multiple halls — West Hall, South Hall, Central Hall, North Hall, Grand Lobby — plus the Festival Grounds for outdoor demonstrations. Caterpillar alone occupies a 70,000-square-foot exhibit on the Festival Grounds. CASE Construction is bringing 40+ machines including 20 new models to West Hall. Understanding this geography is the first step to networking effectively because it dictates how you allocate your time and energy across five days.

2. Pre-Show Networking Preparation

The most productive networking at CONEXPO does not happen by accident. It starts four to six weeks before you arrive in Las Vegas. Here is your pre-show checklist:

Define Your Networking Goals

Before you even look at the exhibitor list, write down exactly what you want from this show. Are you looking for a new equipment supplier? Evaluating technology partners for fleet management? Scouting competitors? Hiring? Looking for subcontractor relationships? Your goals will determine which halls to prioritize, which events to attend, and which people to pursue.

Build a Target List of 30 to 50 Contacts

Use LinkedIn, the CONEXPO exhibitor directory, and your existing industry contacts to identify 30 to 50 specific people you want to meet. For each contact, note their company, role, booth number (if exhibiting), and what you want to discuss. This sounds like a lot of work, but it transforms you from a passive attendee wandering the halls into an active networker with purpose.

Schedule Meetings in Advance

Reach out to your top 10 to 15 targets before the show with a brief, specific message: "I will be at CONEXPO and would like 15 minutes to discuss [specific topic]. Could we meet at your booth on [day/time]?" People are far more receptive to meetings when they are already planning to be at the show. Aim to have at least three to five confirmed meetings before you land in Las Vegas.

Prepare Your Materials

  • Business cards: Bring at least 200. Digital exchange is growing, but physical cards are still the currency of trade shows.
  • One-liner pitch: Craft a 15-second statement that explains who you are and what you are looking for. Practice it until it sounds natural.
  • Portfolio or leave-behind: If you are a vendor, have a one-page spec sheet or case study ready to hand off.
  • LinkedIn profile: Update it with a photo, current role, and a headline that is relevant to the construction industry.
Pro Tip

Download the CONEXPO-CON/AGG mobile app as soon as it becomes available. It lets you browse exhibitors, build a schedule, navigate the show floor with interactive maps, and even message other registered attendees. It is your most powerful pre-show planning tool.

3. Hall-by-Hall Networking Breakdown

Understanding what is in each hall helps you allocate your time strategically and find the people you need to talk to. Here is your guide to the key areas:

West Hall

Focus: Earthmoving equipment — bulldozers, excavators, motor graders, wheel loaders. Major exhibitors include CASE Construction (40+ machines, 20 new models). This is where you will find equipment OEMs, dealers, and fleet decision-makers. Peak foot traffic on Days 1 and 2.

South Hall

Focus: Underground construction — drills, boring machines, trenchers, pipe layers. Specialized contractors and utility companies concentrate here. Less crowded than West Hall, which means better one-on-one conversation time.

Festival Grounds

Focus: Outdoor demonstrations, cranes, concrete equipment, and the massive Caterpillar 70,000 sq ft exhibit. Also home to the Community Zone (free access, podcasts, networking). The outdoor setting creates a more relaxed atmosphere for conversations.

Central Hall & North Hall

Focus: Technology solutions, software, components, attachments, safety equipment. If you are looking for telematics, fleet management, or construction technology partners, this is your zone. Smaller booths mean more accessible exhibitors.

Grand Lobby

Focus: Registration, information, meeting point. The lobby is an underrated networking area — use it as a staging point for planned meetings and chance encounters. The concentration of people during morning registration windows creates natural mingling opportunities.

Hall Navigation Strategy

Do not try to cover the entire show in one day. With 2.9 million square feet, you will exhaust yourself physically and mentally. Instead, assign two halls per day as your primary focus, and use a systematic approach: start at the back of the hall (where foot traffic is lower) and work your way forward. This counter-intuitive strategy gives you access to exhibitors who are less overwhelmed by visitors and more available for substantive conversations.

4. Day-by-Day Networking Strategy

Here is a structured plan for maximizing every day of the show. Adjust based on your specific goals, but this framework ensures you are not wasting time.

Day 1 — Tuesday, March 3: Launch and Lay the Groundwork

  • Morning (8:00-10:00 AM): Arrive early, get registered, and head directly to the Ground Breakers Stage for opening keynotes. The energy is highest on Day 1 and you will encounter the broadest mix of attendees. Take notes on keynote topics — they become excellent conversation starters.
  • Midday (10:00 AM-2:00 PM): Hit West Hall first. Earthmoving is the largest segment and exhibitors are freshest on Day 1. Visit your top-priority booths for pre-scheduled meetings. Walk the CASE Construction exhibit to see the 20 new models and connect with their team.
  • Afternoon (2:00-5:00 PM): Shift to Festival Grounds for the Caterpillar exhibit and any outdoor demos running. The Cat Global Operator Challenge draws large crowds — use the shared spectator experience to strike up conversations with fellow watchers.
  • Evening: Attend the CONEXPO After Dark Kickoff at Atomic Golf. This is your most important networking evening. The casual setting, food, and drinks break down professional barriers. Bring plenty of business cards.

Day 2 — Wednesday, March 4: Deep Dive and Demos

  • Morning (8:30-11:00 AM): Attend Ground Breakers Stage sessions featuring Caterpillar, AWS, or Autodesk (check schedule). Then head to South Hall to explore underground construction — this hall is less chaotic and allows for deeper conversations with specialized exhibitors.
  • Midday (11:00 AM-2:00 PM): Catch live demos. Liebherr runs daily demonstrations, and DEVELON offers 10-minute narrated demo sessions throughout the day. Standing alongside someone watching a machine demo is one of the easiest ice-breakers at the show.
  • Afternoon (2:00-5:00 PM): Work Central Hall and North Hall for technology and component suppliers. These areas house smaller booths where you can often speak directly with company founders and engineers — relationships that are harder to form at mega-booths.
  • Evening: CONEXPO After Dark Beer Tasting event. More intimate than the Kickoff, excellent for follow-up conversations with people you met on Day 1. Must be 21+ with tickets via registration.

Day 3 — Thursday, March 5: Strengthen Connections

  • Morning (8:30-11:00 AM): Revisit booths where you had strong initial conversations on Days 1 and 2 but did not have enough time. Day 3 exhibitors are hitting their stride and are often more relaxed and willing to talk longer.
  • Midday (11:00 AM-2:00 PM): Visit the Community Zone on Festival Grounds. This free-access area features podcast recordings, informal discussions, and unstructured networking. Grab lunch here and sit down with new contacts. The relaxed environment makes it easier to transition from business talk to genuine relationship building.
  • Afternoon (2:00-5:00 PM): Attend the Link-Belt Excavator Challenge and Bluelight autonomous soil compaction demos. Watch for the Deere and John Deere sessions at the Ground Breakers Stage. Continue working your target list.
  • Evening: CONEXPO After Dark Nightlife at LIV Nightclub (21+). The final After Dark event. This is your last structured evening networking opportunity — make it count. Follow up with key contacts from earlier in the week.

Day 4 — Friday, March 6: Quality Over Quantity

  • Morning (8:30-11:00 AM): Focus on second and third meetings with your highest-priority contacts. By Day 4, the crowds thin slightly, which means more floor space and longer conversation windows at major booths.
  • Midday-Afternoon (11:00 AM-4:00 PM): Sweep any halls or exhibitors you missed. Attend remaining live demos. This is your last full day, so prioritize any must-see booths that are still on your list. Exchange contact info with exhibitors who may be packing up early on Day 5.
  • Evening: No official After Dark event tonight. Use this time for private dinners or small-group gatherings with the strongest connections you have made during the week. Restaurant reservations along the Las Vegas Strip fill up fast — book ahead.

Day 5 — Saturday, March 7: Close and Commit

  • Morning (8:30 AM-12:00 PM): The final day runs shorter. Some smaller exhibitors will begin teardown by midday. Use this morning for any final meetings, quick booth visits, and to collect last-minute materials from vendors you missed.
  • Before leaving: Organize all business cards and notes you have collected throughout the week. Add handwritten notes to the back of each card about what you discussed. This will be invaluable during your follow-up process next week.

5. Networking at Live Demos and Challenges

Live demonstrations are more than entertainment — they are some of the best networking environments at CONEXPO. Here is why: when you are standing alongside someone watching a 100-ton excavator being put through its paces, you already share a common experience. That shared context makes starting a conversation effortless.

Key Demos to Target

  • Liebherr Daily Demos: Running throughout the show, these demonstrations attract a knowledgeable crowd of equipment buyers and operators. Arrive five minutes early to position yourself near other attendees rather than at the back of the crowd.
  • DEVELON 10-Minute Narrated Demos: The short format means high turnover, so you can catch multiple sessions and meet different groups each time. The narration also provides talking points.
  • Cat Global Operator Challenge: This competition draws a mix of operators, fleet managers, and Cat dealers. The competitive energy loosens people up and creates a lively atmosphere for networking.
  • Link-Belt Excavator Challenge: Similar to the Cat challenge, this attracts Link-Belt dealers and customers. If you are in the lifting or excavation space, this is a targeted audience.
  • Bluelight Autonomous Soil Compaction: One of the most forward-looking demos at the show. If you are interested in autonomous construction, this will attract the early adopters and technology-forward decision-makers.
Demo Networking Tip

Ask the person next to you: "Have you worked with this machine before?" or "What are you running on your current projects?" These questions immediately establish relevance and open the door to a real conversation, not just small talk.

6. CONEXPO After Dark: The Evening Networking Edge

The CONEXPO After Dark 3-night event series is where some of the most valuable connections are made. The daytime show floor favors quick, transactional exchanges. The evening events allow for longer, more personal conversations where people are more relaxed and open.

Event Schedule

  • Monday, March 3 — Kickoff at Atomic Golf: The opening night event. Atomic Golf combines a driving range with food, drinks, and a party atmosphere. This is the largest After Dark event and attracts the broadest audience. Networking strategy: circulate between the golf bays and the bar area. Do not stay in one spot all night.
  • Tuesday, March 4 — Beer Tasting: A more intimate setting than the Kickoff. The beer-tasting format gives you a built-in excuse to approach anyone: "Have you tried the IPA yet?" Use this event to deepen connections you made on Day 1 rather than trying to meet entirely new people.
  • Wednesday, March 5 — Nightlife at LIV Nightclub: The final evening event at one of Las Vegas's premier nightclubs. Higher energy, louder environment. Networking is more challenging here due to noise, so focus on people you already know and want to strengthen relationships with. Exchange contact info early in the evening before things get too loud.

Important: All CONEXPO After Dark events require attendees to be 21 years of age or older. Tickets are available through your CONEXPO registration. Register early as these events can sell out.

7. Ground Breakers Stage and Keynotes

The Ground Breakers Stage hosts keynote sessions from some of the biggest names in the industry. Confirmed speakers include executives from Caterpillar, AWS, Autodesk, and John Deere. These sessions are networking opportunities disguised as presentations.

How to Network Around Keynotes

  • Arrive 15 minutes early and introduce yourself to the people sitting next to you. You already share an interest in the topic — use that.
  • Take notes during the session with specific questions or observations. After the talk, approach other attendees with "What did you think about [specific point]?" This leads to more substantial conversations than generic networking openers.
  • Linger after the session. The 10 to 15 minutes after a keynote ends are golden networking time. People are energized, have something to talk about, and are not yet back on the show floor clock.
  • Try to connect with the speakers. Many keynote speakers will be available briefly after their sessions. A short, specific compliment or question can lead to a meaningful connection. Do not pitch — just be genuine.
"The best networking conversations at trade shows start with curiosity, not a sales pitch. Ask what problems someone is trying to solve, and you will learn more in five minutes than you would in a month of cold calls."

8. The Community Zone Advantage

The Community Zone on the Festival Grounds is one of the most underutilized networking assets at CONEXPO. This free-access area is designed specifically for community interaction, featuring live podcast recordings, informal discussion groups, and open seating areas where you can recharge while still being surrounded by industry peers.

Here is why the Community Zone is a networking goldmine: it attracts people who are specifically looking to connect rather than just browse equipment. The atmosphere is deliberately casual. Podcast recordings draw small, engaged audiences — the kind of people who are active in industry conversations online and in person. After a podcast wraps, the host and audience often continue talking, creating an organic networking environment that is nearly impossible to replicate on the show floor.

Community Zone Strategies

  • Use it as your lunch spot on Days 2 and 3 to meet people in a relaxed setting.
  • Attend at least one live podcast recording and introduce yourself to the host afterward.
  • If you have your own podcast or social media presence, reach out in advance about participating in a recording.
  • Bring a small group of new contacts here for an informal round-table discussion.

Physical business cards remain important, but digital tools give you an edge in speed, organization, and follow-up. Here is your digital networking toolkit for CONEXPO 2026:

  • CONEXPO-CON/AGG Official App: Exhibitor directory, interactive maps, session schedules, and attendee messaging. This is your primary navigation and planning tool.
  • LinkedIn: The moment you meet someone, search for them on LinkedIn and send a connection request with a brief note: "Great meeting you at the Liebherr demo today." Do it before you forget the details.

  • Voice memos: After each significant conversation, step aside and record a 30-second voice note summarizing who you met, what you discussed, and any follow-up actions. This is faster and more reliable than scribbling notes.
  • Calendar blocking: Block 30-minute windows between meetings for walking between halls, hydrating, and processing notes. The LVCC is enormous and travel time between West Hall and the Festival Grounds can take 15 to 20 minutes.

10. Post-Show Follow-Up Strategy

The networking does not end when you leave Las Vegas. In fact, the follow-up is where most of the value is actually created — and where most attendees fail. Studies consistently show that 80 percent of trade show leads are never followed up on. Do not be in that 80 percent.

The 48-Hour Rule

You have 48 hours after the show ends to send your initial follow-ups. After that, people get buried in their normal workload and the trade show glow fades. Here is your follow-up template:

  • Within 24 hours: Send LinkedIn connection requests to everyone you met. Include a one-line note referencing your conversation.
  • Within 48 hours: Send personalized emails to your top 10 to 15 contacts. Reference the specific conversation you had, any demos you watched together, or topics you discussed. Attach any materials you promised to send.
  • Within one week: Schedule follow-up calls or video meetings with your highest-priority contacts. The goal is to move from "trade show acquaintance" to "ongoing professional relationship."
  • Within one month: Share a relevant article, case study, or introduction with each contact. This demonstrates that you are thinking about them beyond the transaction.

11. Essential Gear for the Show Floor

Five days on 2.9 million square feet of concrete will test your endurance. The right gear makes the difference between productive networking and painful survival mode:

  • Comfortable walking shoes: You will log 8 to 12 miles per day. Break in your shoes before the show. Running shoes or cushioned walking shoes are far better than dress shoes.
  • Portable battery pack: Your phone is your lifeline for navigation, note-taking, and LinkedIn. A 20,000mAh power bank ensures you never go dark.
  • Lightweight backpack or messenger bag: You will collect brochures, swag, and materials. A bag with a laptop compartment also lets you take notes or present materials on the fly.
  • Water bottle: Las Vegas is a desert. The convention center is air-conditioned but the Festival Grounds are not. Stay hydrated or you will hit a wall by 2:00 PM.
  • Business card holder: Separate incoming cards from your own. A dedicated pocket or case prevents the dreaded "whose card is this?" moment during follow-up.
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses: The Festival Grounds and outdoor demo areas have limited shade. March in Las Vegas can reach the mid-70s Fahrenheit with intense sun.
  • Snacks: Trade show food is expensive and the lines are long. Protein bars and trail mix keep you fueled without losing 30 minutes in a food court.

12. Common Networking Mistakes to Avoid

After covering hundreds of trade shows, our team has identified the patterns that separate effective networkers from everyone else. Avoid these common mistakes:

  • Talking more than listening. The single most common networking mistake. At CONEXPO, your job is to ask questions and listen for opportunities, not deliver a monologue about your company. Aim for a 30/70 talk-to-listen ratio.
  • Trying to see everything. With 2,000+ exhibitors, you cannot visit every booth. Trying to will leave you exhausted with shallow interactions everywhere. Focus on 40 to 60 high-priority booths across the five days and go deep.
  • Skipping the evening events. The CONEXPO After Dark series is where walls come down. If you retreat to your hotel room every evening, you are leaving the most valuable networking time on the table.
  • Not having a clear ask. When someone asks "How can I help you?" or "What are you looking for?" you need a crisp, specific answer. "I am looking for a telematics provider that integrates with Deere and Cat mixed fleets" is far more actionable than "We are just here to see what is out there."
  • Failing to follow up. This is the number one killer of trade show ROI. Every contact you do not follow up with within 48 hours is a wasted opportunity.
  • Ignoring small booths. The massive Caterpillar and CASE exhibits get all the attention, but some of the most innovative companies at CONEXPO are in 10x10 booths in Central Hall and North Hall. These exhibitors are hungry for connections and often more accessible than enterprise-level brands.
  • Not eating or hydrating. This sounds trivial, but dehydration and low blood sugar destroy your energy, your mood, and your ability to hold engaging conversations. Eat breakfast before you arrive. Drink water constantly. Take real lunch breaks.

Final Thoughts

CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 is a once-every-three-years opportunity. The connections you make during March 3 through 7 can define your next three years of business. The exhibitors have invested millions of dollars to be there. The industry leaders are there. The decision-makers are there. Your job is to show up prepared, work the halls with purpose, attend the right events, and follow up relentlessly.

Print this guide, bookmark it on your phone, and revisit it each evening during the show to recalibrate your strategy for the next day. See you in Las Vegas.
