How to Network at CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026

ShowFloorTips Editorial February 11, 2026

Overview: North America's Largest Construction Trade Show

CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 takes over the Las Vegas Convention Center and Festival Grounds from March 10 through 14, making it the largest construction trade show in North America and one of the biggest in the world. Held every three years, this event draws more than 130,000 attendees and 2,500-plus exhibitors across nearly 2.8 million net square feet of exhibit space. The sheer scale is staggering: it takes multiple days just to walk the full show floor, and the equipment on display ranges from compact loaders to 400-ton cranes.

But CONEXPO is not just about iron. It is where contractors find new suppliers, where equipment manufacturers recruit dealers, where technology companies pitch their construction software to skeptical field veterans, and where industry associations shape policy. If you work anywhere in the construction, aggregates, concrete, earthmoving, mining, or heavy equipment supply chain, this is the networking event of the cycle. Here is how to make the most of it.

Key Networking Events at CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026

Opening Ceremony and Keynote. The CONEXPO-CON/AGG opening ceremony on Tuesday morning sets the tone for the week. Past keynotes have featured major industry figures and motivational speakers, drawing a packed audience of senior executives and company owners. The time immediately after the keynote, when the crowd filters out into the hallways, is a prime window for introductions. People are energized and open to conversation.

Education Sessions. CONEXPO offers more than 150 education sessions across tracks including project management, safety, technology, sustainability, and business management. These smaller-format sessions, typically holding 50 to 200 attendees, are far better networking environments than the vast show floor. The attendees in each room share a common interest, giving you an immediate conversation topic. Sessions on construction technology and autonomous equipment tend to draw the most forward-thinking executives.

Tech Experience Area. Located in the West Hall, the Tech Experience showcases construction technology, robotics, drones, and digital solutions. This area attracts a younger, more tech-savvy crowd compared to the traditional equipment halls, and the exhibitors are often startups eager to have in-depth conversations. If you are in construction tech, this is your home base for the week.

Association Receptions and Chapter Events. Industry associations like the Associated General Contractors of America (AGC), the National Ready Mixed Concrete Association (NRMCA), and the National Stone, Sand and Gravel Association (NSSGA) host receptions and member events throughout the week. These gatherings are excellent because attendees are pre-qualified by their membership and the atmosphere is collegial. Check with your associations for event schedules and register early, as many cap attendance.

Manufacturer-Hosted Events. Major manufacturers like Caterpillar, John Deere, Volvo CE, Komatsu, and Liebherr host private events ranging from customer appreciation dinners to product launch parties. These are among the most valuable networking opportunities at CONEXPO because the guest lists are curated and the conversations are substantive. If you are an existing customer or dealer, reach out to your sales rep now to secure an invitation. If you are not, use LinkedIn to connect with relevant contacts at these companies and express specific interest in attending.

Festival Grounds Demonstrations. The outdoor Festival Grounds area features live equipment demonstrations throughout the day. The demonstrations create natural gathering points where attendees cluster to watch, and the shared spectacle of watching a massive excavator in action makes it remarkably easy to strike up conversations with the people standing next to you.

Best Networking Spots Inside and Outside the Venue

The Skybridge Between the West Hall and North/Central Halls. This elevated walkway is one of the most heavily trafficked connectors at the Las Vegas Convention Center. People slow down as they cross, often pausing to check their phones or orient themselves. It is a natural place to catch someone you have been trying to reach or to introduce yourself to a fellow attendee.

Food Courts and Concession Areas. The concession areas in the North Hall and Central Hall fill at lunchtime with attendees who need a break from the show floor. Sharing a table with a stranger is completely normal at CONEXPO, and the casual setting lowers barriers. Some of the best relationships in the construction industry were started over a convention center hot dog.

The Las Vegas Convention Center Loop (Tesla Tunnel). The underground transportation system connecting the halls gives attendees a few quiet minutes during the ride. While the ride is short, the captive environment makes it easy to chat with whoever is sharing your vehicle.

The Strip Hotels After Hours. After the show floor closes, the action shifts to the Las Vegas Strip. The steakhouses and bars at the Wynn, Encore, and Venetian tend to fill with CONEXPO attendees, particularly the high-end restaurants that cater to the executive crowd. The construction industry loves a good steak dinner, and booking a reservation at a popular spot virtually guarantees you will be surrounded by industry peers.

Hotel Lobbies at Westgate and Renaissance. Both hotels are adjacent to the convention center and serve as unofficial meeting hubs. The Westgate Las Vegas, in particular, becomes an extension of the show, with attendees scheduling meetings in the lobby from early morning through late evening.

Conversation Starters Built for the Construction Crowd

Construction professionals tend to be direct, practical, and skeptical of smooth talkers. Your conversation starters should reflect that culture.

At an equipment demo: "Have you run one of those on a job site, or are you looking at them for the first time? I am trying to figure out if the specs hold up in real conditions." This immediately signals that you are a practitioner, not just a spectator, and invites them to share hard-won field experience.

At a booth: "What problem does this solve that I cannot already handle with what is on the market? I want to hear the honest pitch." Construction people appreciate directness. Asking a vendor to cut to the chase earns respect and often leads to a more genuine conversation.

In an education session: "What is the biggest challenge you are facing on your current projects? I am curious whether the topics they are covering here are actually hitting the mark." This shows humility and invites them to talk about what matters most to them.

At an evening event: "How many CONEXPO cycles have you been to? I am curious how the show has changed." This works especially well with veteran attendees, who love sharing their perspective on how the industry has evolved.

One important note: the construction industry is built on trust and reputation. Nobody at CONEXPO wants to hear a polished sales pitch from a stranger. Lead with genuine curiosity about their work, their challenges, and their projects. The business conversation will follow naturally once trust is established.

Your Post-CONEXPO Follow-Up Strategy

CONEXPO happens only once every three years, which means your follow-up window carries extra weight. The connections you make here need to last until the next show in 2029 and beyond.

During the show: Capture contact information immediately after every meaningful conversation. Use a badge scanning tool like Scannly or snap a photo of their business card and add a voice memo with context. By Friday afternoon, you will have met hundreds of people, and the details will start to blur. Your notes are your lifeline.

Within 72 hours of the show closing: Send personalized follow-up emails or LinkedIn messages to your top contacts. The key word is personalized. Reference the specific equipment you discussed, the project they mentioned, or the challenge they are facing. For example: "Great talking with you at the Komatsu demo on Wednesday. The autonomous haulage discussion stuck with me. I would love to continue that conversation and share some data from a project we ran last year."

Within two weeks: Schedule calls or meetings with your highest-priority contacts. Propose a specific agenda and time frame, such as "a 20-minute call to discuss the fleet management challenge you mentioned." Concrete proposals get responses; vague "let's stay in touch" messages get ignored.

Ongoing through 2026 and beyond: The construction industry is relationship-driven, and staying in touch between CONEXPO cycles is critical. Add your contacts to a CRM, set quarterly check-in reminders, and share relevant industry news or project updates periodically. When CONEXPO 2029 rolls around, you want these contacts to feel like established relationships, not cold reconnections.

CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 is a once-in-three-years opportunity. The professionals who get the most out of it are not the ones who see the most booths. They are the ones who have the best conversations and follow up with the discipline that the construction industry respects. Plan your targets, walk with purpose, and remember that in this industry, a handshake backed by follow-through is worth more than a thousand badge scans.
