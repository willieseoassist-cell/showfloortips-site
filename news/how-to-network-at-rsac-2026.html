How to Network at RSAC 2026: A Complete Guide

Published on February 11, 2026 • 9 min read

RSAC (formerly RSA Conference) is the world's largest cybersecurity gathering, bringing together approximately 44,000 professionals at the Moscone Center in San Francisco. From CISOs and security engineers to startup founders and government officials, the conference attracts the full spectrum of the information security ecosystem. Networking effectively at an event this massive requires a clear plan. This guide covers everything you need to know to build high-value connections at RSAC 2026.

Pre-Show Preparation: Build Your Strategy Weeks in Advance

At a conference with 44,000 attendees spread across multiple Moscone halls, preparation is not optional. It is the difference between productive networking and aimless wandering.

  • Set clear networking goals. Are you evaluating security vendors for your enterprise? Looking for a new role? Seeking investors for your startup? Scouting acquisition targets? Define your top three objectives and let them guide every scheduling decision.
  • Use the RSAC attendee platform. The official RSAC app and website allow you to browse attendees, exhibitors, and speakers. Search for people by company, role, and area of expertise. Send meeting requests through the platform at least two weeks before the event.
  • Join the RSAC social channels. Follow the official RSAC hashtag on LinkedIn and X (Twitter). Engage with pre-conference content by commenting on posts from speakers and exhibitors. This warms up relationships before you meet in person.
  • Block your calendar strategically. RSAC runs for a full week, but the core conference days are Tuesday through Friday. Leave at least 30 percent of your schedule unblocked for spontaneous conversations and hallway meetings. Over-scheduling is the most common mistake at large conferences.
  • Prepare talking points on current threats. RSAC attendees are deeply technical and well-informed. Be ready to discuss the latest threat landscape, recent breaches, regulatory developments like the SEC cyber disclosure rules, and emerging technologies like AI-driven security operations.

Key Networking Venues and Events at RSAC 2026

RSAC is spread across Moscone North, South, and West, plus surrounding hotels and venues. Knowing where to be and when is critical.

The Expo Floor

The exhibition hall features over 600 vendors across two massive halls. The expo floor is busiest from 10 AM to 2 PM. For meaningful conversations with vendors, visit booths in the first hour after opening or in the last hour before closing when booth staff are less overwhelmed. The Innovation Sandbox area and Early Stage Expo are especially valuable for meeting startup founders.

Keynote Sessions

The keynote hall seats thousands and features major industry leaders and government officials. Arrive early to secure a seat, and use the time before keynotes start to introduce yourself to the people sitting around you. Post-keynote hallway conversations often lead to excellent connections with senior leaders.

Track Sessions and Seminars

RSAC offers hundreds of sessions across tracks like Cloud Security, Application Security, Threat Intelligence, and Security Operations. Smaller breakout sessions are goldmines for networking because attendees share a specific interest. Introduce yourself to speakers after their talks and exchange contact information with fellow attendees who asked good questions.

RSAC Innovation Sandbox

The Innovation Sandbox contest is one of the most popular events at the conference. Ten cybersecurity startups pitch their solutions to a panel of judges. The audience is packed with investors, enterprise buyers, and media. Attending this session puts you in a room full of decision-makers and innovators.

Evening Events and Vendor Parties

San Francisco comes alive during RSAC week with vendor-hosted dinners, rooftop parties, and industry meetups at restaurants and bars around SoMa and the Embarcadero. These informal settings are where many of the most valuable relationships form. Check LinkedIn, Eventbrite, and vendor emails for invitations.

The Moscone Hallways and Lounges

Never underestimate the power of hallway conversations at Moscone. The areas between sessions, the coffee stations, and the charging lounges are natural gathering points where people are more relaxed and open to conversation than they are on the expo floor.

Cybersecurity-Specific Networking Tips

The cybersecurity community has its own culture and norms. Adapt your approach accordingly.

  • Lead with problems, not products. Security professionals are bombarded with vendor pitches every day. If you are a vendor, start conversations by asking about their challenges rather than launching into your feature list. Genuine curiosity earns trust faster than any sales deck.
  • Respect the practitioner community. Many RSAC attendees are hands-on security engineers and analysts. They value technical depth over marketing language. If you cannot speak credibly about the technical aspects of your space, bring a team member who can.
  • Leverage the CISO community. CISOs often travel in groups and attend private dinners. If you have a connection to a CISO, ask for introductions to their peers. The CISO network is tight-knit, and a warm introduction is worth more than a hundred cold outreaches.
  • Be mindful of information sensitivity. Cybersecurity professionals are naturally cautious about sharing details about their organization's security posture. Do not press for specifics about their tech stack or vulnerabilities in casual conversation.
  • Engage with the open-source and research community. RSAC has a strong contingent of security researchers and open-source contributors. Showing genuine interest in their work and contributing back to the community builds long-term credibility.

On-Site Networking Strategies

  • Wear comfortable shoes. Moscone Center is enormous and you will walk miles each day. Physical comfort directly impacts your energy and enthusiasm for networking.
  • Carry a portable charger. Your phone is your most important networking tool. A dead battery means missed connections and lost meeting details.
  • Use the buddy system. Attend sessions with a colleague and split up to cover more ground. Reconvene at the end of each day to share contacts and insights.
  • Be a connector. When you meet two people who should know each other, make the introduction. Being a connector dramatically increases your value in the network and people remember those who helped them.
  • Engage on social media in real time. Live-tweet or post LinkedIn updates from sessions using the official RSAC hashtag. Tagging speakers and companies amplifies your visibility and invites digital connections alongside physical ones.

Follow-Up Strategies After RSAC

The real ROI of RSAC networking comes in the weeks after you return home. A structured follow-up process is essential.

  • Send personalized follow-ups within 48 hours. Every attendee's inbox is flooded after RSAC. Make your message stand out by referencing a specific topic you discussed or an insight from a session you attended together.
  • Segment your contacts. Categorize new connections by priority and relationship type: potential customers, potential partners, industry peers, and recruiters or job leads. Tailor your follow-up cadence to each category.
  • Share relevant content. Forward articles, research reports, or threat intelligence that relates to what you discussed. Adding value to the relationship before asking for anything builds goodwill.
  • Propose a follow-up call. For high-priority contacts, suggest a 20-minute virtual meeting within two weeks. The longer you wait, the more the RSAC momentum fades.
  • Join year-round communities. Many RSAC connections deepen through ongoing participation in organizations like ISSA, ISACA, ISC2, and local BSides events. Stay active in the community between conferences.

Final Thoughts

RSAC 2026 represents the largest concentration of cybersecurity talent, budget, and decision-making power in the world. With 44,000 attendees, the sheer scale can be overwhelming, but a prepared and disciplined networker can build connections that transform their career or business. Plan ahead, prioritize quality over quantity, and follow up like a professional. The cybersecurity community rewards those who show up with authenticity and substance.
