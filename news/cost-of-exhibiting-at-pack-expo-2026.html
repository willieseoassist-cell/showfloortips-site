How Much Does It Cost to Exhibit at Pack Expo 2026?

ShowFloorTips Editorial February 11, 2026

Pack Expo International is the largest packaging and processing trade show in North America, organized by PMMI (The Association for Packaging and Processing Technologies). Held at McCormick Place in Chicago, Pack Expo International attracts over 40,000 attendees and 2,500+ exhibitors from the packaging, food processing, pharmaceutical, and consumer goods industries. Here is a detailed cost breakdown for exhibitors.

Booth Space Costs

Pack Expo has a transparent pricing model, and uniquely, drayage is included in the booth space price:

  • PMMI member rate: $34.25 per sq ft (drayage included)
  • Non-member rate: Approximately $36-39 per sq ft
  • Pack Expo East (Philadelphia): $33.25 per sq ft for PMMI members

Example Booth Costs (PMMI Member Rate)

  • 10x10 booth (100 sq ft): $3,425
  • 10x20 booth (200 sq ft): $6,850
  • 20x20 booth (400 sq ft): $13,700
  • 30x30 booth (900 sq ft): $30,825

The included drayage is a significant benefit. At most other major trade shows, drayage alone can add $1,000-$10,000+ to your total cost. This makes Pack Expo one of the better values in the trade show industry for heavy equipment exhibitors.

Booth Design and Construction

  • Pop-up display: $500 - $2,000
  • Custom 10x10 with machinery display: $8,000 - $30,000
  • Custom 20x20 island: $35,000 - $100,000
  • Custom 30x30+ with running machinery: $75,000 - $200,000+
  • Rental booth: $3,500 - $15,000 for 10x10

Packaging machinery exhibitors frequently ship and demonstrate running equipment on the show floor, which drives up both booth construction and utility costs.

Electrical and Utilities

Running packaging machinery requires significantly more power than a typical display booth:

  • Standard electrical (500W): $350 - $750
  • Industrial power (for running machinery): $1,000 - $5,000
  • Compressed air: $500 - $2,000
  • Water supply and drainage: $400 - $1,500
  • Dedicated internet: $600 - $2,000

Travel and Lodging

Chicago is a moderately priced convention city compared to New York or San Francisco:

  • Hotel (4 nights, Loop/South Loop): $800 - $1,800 per person
  • Flights: $200 - $500 per person (ORD or MDW)
  • Meals: $50 - $100 per person per day
  • Ground transportation: $100 - $300 per person

McCormick Place is accessible via the CTA Green Line and Metra, making it possible to stay in the Loop and commute to the venue affordably. For a team of four, travel costs range from $5,000 to $12,000.

Marketing Materials

  • Pre-show email campaign: $500 - $2,000
  • Printed materials and spec sheets: $300 - $1,200
  • Product demonstrations and samples: $500 - $3,000
  • Giveaways: $500 - $2,000
  • Lead retrieval system: $300 - $500

A basic 10x10 exhibitor at Pack Expo should budget $15,000 - $30,000. Companies demonstrating running machinery typically spend $50,000 - $200,000+. The included drayage makes Pack Expo more cost-effective than comparable shows where drayage is charged separately.

Tips to Reduce Your Pack Expo Costs

  1. Join PMMI: Member booth rates save $2-5/sq ft, which adds up quickly for larger spaces.
  2. Leverage included drayage: Ship as much as possible via the official freight program since drayage is already in your booth cost.
  3. Consider Pack Expo East: The Philadelphia show is smaller but costs less ($33.25/sq ft) and targets the East Coast market.
  4. Rent your booth: Save 40-60% over custom construction.
  5. Use CTA to commute: Stay in the Loop and take the Green Line to McCormick Place to save on hotel costs.
