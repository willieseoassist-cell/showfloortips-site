Trade Show Exhibitor's Guide to Atlanta

ShowFloorTips · January 21, 2026

In an era dominated by digital marketing, face-to-face events continue to deliver unmatched ROI. Atlanta is a major hub for the global exhibition industry, hosting dozens of significant trade shows each year. Notable upcoming events include MODEX - Material Handling & Logistics Solutions, American Probation & Parole Association - APPA, Electrify Expo - North America's Largest Electric Vehicle Event, International Woodworking Fair - IWF, Design-2-Part Show. This guide provides everything you need to plan a successful exhibition trip to Atlanta.

Exhibition Venues in Atlanta

The Georgia World Congress Center offers 3.9 million square feet of total space, making it one of the largest in the Western Hemisphere.

Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport is the busiest airport in the world by passenger volume.

Atlanta's Midtown and Buckhead neighborhoods provide a range of dining and entertainment options for client meetings.

When selecting booth space at a Atlanta venue, request a floor plan early and prioritize locations near main entrances, food service areas, or keynote stages. These high-traffic zones consistently deliver more booth visits.

Getting to Atlanta

Planning your travel to Atlanta well in advance is essential, particularly during peak trade show seasons when hotel inventory tightens and airfares increase. Book flights and accommodations at least six to eight weeks before your event.

If you are shipping booth materials internationally, work with a freight forwarder experienced in exhibition logistics for United States. Customs documentation, temporary import permits, and venue delivery schedules all require careful coordination.

Hotels and Accommodations

Most major trade shows in Atlanta offer discounted room blocks through official hotel partners. These rates are typically 15-25% below standard pricing and include shuttle service to the venue. Book through the show's official housing bureau when possible.

For extended stays or larger teams, serviced apartments and corporate housing options can provide better value. Many exhibitors also choose hotels within walking distance of the venue to minimize daily commute time and maximize time on the show floor.

Dining and Client Entertainment

Client dinners and networking meals are a critical part of trade show strategy. Atlanta offers a wide range of dining options suitable for business entertainment. Research and reserve restaurants near your venue well in advance, as popular spots fill up quickly during major events.

Many experienced exhibitors host private dinners or cocktail receptions at nearby venues to create exclusive networking opportunities outside the show floor environment.

Networking in Atlanta

Trade shows in Atlanta provide exceptional networking opportunities beyond the exhibition hall itself. Make the most of your time in the city with these proven approaches:

  • Prepare a concise, compelling elevator pitch that communicates your value proposition in under 30 seconds.
  • Approach networking at trade shows with a giving mindset. Focus on how you can help others before asking for anything in return.
  • Host a small breakfast or cocktail event adjacent to the show to create an exclusive networking opportunity for key prospects.
  • Join industry association meetings and committee sessions that take place alongside major trade shows for deeper engagement.

Booth Setup and Logistics

Exhibiting in Atlanta involves logistical considerations that vary by venue. Review these guidelines to ensure smooth setup and teardown:

  • Plan for dismantling and return shipping before the show begins to avoid scrambling during teardown.
  • Keep digital copies of all shipping documents, insurance certificates, and venue contracts accessible on your phone.
  • Coordinate with your booth builder for installation and dismantle labor well ahead of the show dates.
  • Arrange for advance warehouse delivery if the venue offers it, as it often reduces drayage costs.

Maximizing Your Trade Show Trip to Atlanta

The most successful exhibitors treat a trip to Atlanta as a comprehensive business campaign, not just an event. Schedule side meetings with local clients and partners, attend industry association events that coincide with the show, and allocate time for market research.

Track your results carefully. Document leads captured, meetings held, and follow-up actions required. This data will inform your decision about returning to Atlanta for future events and help you build a case for the investment internally.
