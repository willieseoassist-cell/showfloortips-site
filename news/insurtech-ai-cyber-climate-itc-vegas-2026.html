AI, Cyber Risk, and Climate Catastrophe — The Three Forces Reshaping Insurance Ahead of ITC Vegas 2026

ShowFloorTips Editorial · February 11, 2026

GlobalData's year-end analysis identified three themes that will have the biggest impact on the insurance market in 2026: artificial intelligence, cyber insurance, and climate change. These themes are not isolated — AI shapes how insurers price and manage cyber and climate risk, cyber loss patterns test capital models, and climate volatility stresses underwriting assumptions. With the global insurtech market projected to reach approximately $23.5 billion this year and analysts projecting that more than 35% of insurers will deploy AI agents across at least three core functions by late 2026, the insurance technology trade show circuit — anchored by ITC Vegas, InsureTech Connect, and RIMS — has never been more consequential.

$23.5B
Projected global insurtech market size in 2026

AI Moves from Experiment to Operating System

SAS characterizes AI as the insurance industry's "new operating system" for 2026. The shift is dramatic. Agentic AI is already handling claims intake, property assessments, and low-stakes customer interactions in production environments. Analysts project these AI agents will cut processing time by up to 70% for the functions where they are deployed.

But the transition is not without friction. CyberCube's forecast warns of an impending "AI disillusionment period" in 2026, where legacy systems, entrenched data silos, and cautious regulatory environments will slow AI adoption at many organizations. The gap between AI leaders and AI laggards in insurance is expected to widen significantly this year.

"AI will reduce repetitive manual tasks and deliver relevant insights, while humans will make all the critical decisions in a claim. The companies that get this balance right will define the next generation of insurance."

-- SAS Insurance AI Operating System Report, December 2025

For insurtech exhibitors, this creates a nuanced positioning challenge. The buyers at ITC Vegas range from AI-native startups to century-old carriers with mainframe-era policy administration systems. Solutions that can bridge this gap — delivering AI capabilities that integrate with existing infrastructure without requiring full platform replacement — will generate the strongest interest on the show floor.

Cyber Insurance: From Generalized Models to Technical Underwriting

The cyber insurance market is undergoing a fundamental methodology shift in 2026. The industry is moving from generalized actuarial modeling to more targeted technical underwriting on a client-by-client basis. Insurers are increasingly favoring clients that exhibit and enforce proper security controls, and pricing is becoming more granular based on specific risk profiles rather than broad industry categories.

The Convergence of AI and Cyber Risk

AI introduces new dimensions to cyber risk. Automated attack tools lower the barrier for threat actors, while AI-powered defense systems create new protection capabilities. For insurtech companies offering cyber risk assessment, threat monitoring, or incident response solutions, the intersection of AI and cybersecurity represents a high-value market opportunity.

At ITC Vegas, expect dedicated sessions and exhibitor showcases focused on AI-driven underwriting for cyber policies. The companies that can provide real-time risk scoring based on a client's actual security posture — not just questionnaire responses — will demonstrate the kind of technical differentiation that earns meetings with underwriting leadership.

Key InsightInsurtech exhibitors offering cyber risk solutions should prepare demonstrations that show real-time security posture assessment. Static risk questionnaires are being replaced by continuous monitoring models that dynamically adjust premiums.

Climate Catastrophe Tests the Industry's Foundation

Climate change and the increasing frequency of severe weather events are intensifying pressure on insurers worldwide. Natural fire and hazard insurance premiums are seeing sharp annual increases, claims are rising, and large areas of the world are becoming functionally uninsurable under traditional models. The insurance industry's ability to price and manage climate risk will determine its relevance in the decades ahead.

For exhibitors at RIMS, which focuses on risk management and insurance, climate resilience is the defining topic. Solutions that improve catastrophe modeling, enable parametric insurance products tied to weather data, or provide post-event damage assessment using satellite imagery and AI will be in high demand. The RIMS audience includes corporate risk managers who are navigating increasingly difficult conversations with carriers about coverage availability and pricing.

IoT and Real-Time Risk Mitigation

The utilization of IoT devices for real-time risk evaluation and minimization is accelerating in 2026. Connected sensors in commercial properties, vehicles, and industrial facilities provide continuous data feeds that enable proactive loss prevention rather than reactive claims processing. For insurtech companies building IoT-connected platforms, the insurance industry represents a massive and growing market.

The Insurance Trade Show Calendar in 2026

Each major insurance event serves a distinct audience and purpose:

  • ITC Vegas (InsureTech Connect): The world's largest insurtech gathering, attracting 9,000+ attendees from 65+ countries. Features keynote sessions, startup pitch competitions, networking zones, and product demos. Best for startups, growth-stage insurtechs, and technology vendors targeting the insurance industry. More than 500 of the most influential minds in insurance share strategies and insights.
  • Insurtech Insights USA (June 3-4, New York): Concentrated format focused on the intersection of insurance innovation and investment. Strong attendance from venture capital firms and corporate innovation teams.
  • RIMS (Risk and Insurance Management Society): Focused on the corporate risk management buyer. The audience includes risk managers, brokers, and carriers discussing coverage strategy, emerging risks, and enterprise risk management. Ideal for companies selling to the corporate insurance buyer rather than the carrier.

Exhibitor Strategies for Insurance Shows in 2026

The insurance industry in 2026 is simultaneously cautious and urgent — cautious about AI hype and implementation risk, but urgent about the competitive consequences of falling behind. Here is how exhibitors should navigate this dynamic:

  1. Demonstrate integration, not disruption. Insurance carriers operate on policy administration systems that can be decades old. AI solutions that require full platform replacement will face resistance. Show how your technology layers on top of existing infrastructure and delivers value within existing workflows.
  2. Bring regulatory fluency. Insurance is one of the most heavily regulated industries. Your booth staff must be able to discuss state-by-state regulatory requirements, data privacy compliance, and AI governance frameworks without hesitation. Regulatory knowledge builds credibility faster than technology demos.
  3. Quantify the claims impact. A 70% reduction in processing time translates to specific dollar amounts for a carrier processing millions of claims annually. Bring the math. Show the payback period. Insurance buyers think in actuarial terms — present your value proposition the same way.
  4. Address the talent and change management challenge. Technology adoption in insurance is as much about people as platforms. Companies that offer training, change management support, and phased implementation plans will differentiate themselves from pure technology vendors.
  5. Leverage ITC's startup competition. If you are a growth-stage insurtech, ITC Vegas's pitch competitions and investor networking events provide access to the capital and partnerships needed to scale. Prepare a concise, data-driven pitch that addresses a specific insurance pain point.
Exhibitor Action ItemBook your ITC Vegas meetings now. The event's scale means that the most productive interactions are pre-scheduled, and key insurance executives fill their calendars weeks before arriving in Las Vegas.

The insurance industry in 2026 is being reshaped by three converging forces that touch every aspect of the business — from underwriting and pricing to claims processing and customer experience. ITC Vegas, InsureTech Connect, and RIMS are where the technology decisions that define the industry's response will be made. Exhibitors who arrive with solutions that address these interconnected challenges will be the ones who generate pipeline from the show floor.
