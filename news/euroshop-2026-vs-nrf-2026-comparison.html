EuroShop 2026 vs NRF 2026: Which Retail Show Delivers Better ROI for Exhibitors?

12 min read February 14, 2026 ShowFloorTips Research Team

If you sell to the retail industry, two shows dominate the global calendar: EuroShop in Düsseldorf and NRF Retail’s Big Show in New York. EuroShop 2026 runs February 22 through 26 with over 90,000 attendees from 140+ countries. NRF 2026 ran January 11 through 13 and drew approximately 40,000 attendees to the Javits Center. Both claim to be the definitive retail event. Both attract serious buyers. But they serve fundamentally different audiences, run on different rhythms, and demand different investments. This is the honest comparison most exhibitors wish they had before signing the contract.

Size and Scale

EuroShop is more than twice the size of NRF in raw attendance numbers. With 90,000+ attendees versus NRF’s 40,000+, EuroShop delivers a larger audience and more foot traffic per day across its five-day run. The exhibition space at Messe Düsseldorf — 127,000 square meters across 16 halls — dwarfs the Javits Center’s approximately 65,000 square meters. For exhibitors, this scale means more potential contacts per day at EuroShop, but also more competition for attention and more physical ground to cover.

NRF compensates for its smaller size with higher attendee quality concentration. The three-day format and the show’s focus on senior retail executives mean that the average decision-making authority per badge at NRF is arguably higher than at EuroShop, where the broader attendee base includes architects, designers, and technical specialists alongside executive buyers.

International Reach

This is where the comparison becomes stark. EuroShop’s attendee base spans 140+ countries with particularly strong representation from Europe, the Middle East, and Asia-Pacific. For exhibitors targeting global or European markets, EuroShop is unmatched. The show’s triennial cycle amplifies this: missing one edition costs you three years of presence in front of the global retail audience.

NRF’s attendee base is predominantly North American, with growing but still secondary international attendance. If your primary market is the United States and Canada, NRF puts you in front of the buyers who matter most with significantly lower travel costs and logistical complexity than exhibiting in Germany. For global companies, the calculus is simple: NRF is your North American play, EuroShop is your global play.

Booth Costs and Total Investment

Raw booth costs are comparable, but the total investment tells a very different story. Exhibiting at EuroShop from the United States requires international shipping for booth materials, European hotel rates during a peak trade fair week in Düsseldorf (expect $250 to $400 per night), flights for your team, five days of staff time versus NRF’s three, and the logistical overhead of operating in a different time zone and business culture. For a mid-size exhibitor, the all-in cost difference between EuroShop and NRF can reach $15,000 to $30,000.

However, NRF’s New York location adds its own premium. Manhattan hotels during NRF week command $350 to $600 per night. Meals, ground transportation, and entertainment costs in midtown Manhattan are among the highest in the world. Do not assume the domestic show is automatically cheaper. The math depends on your team size, booth footprint, and how much client entertainment you plan.

Technology Focus

NRF has positioned itself as the retail industry’s premier technology and digital transformation event. The Innovation Lab, the startup pitch competitions, and the keynote lineup featuring CEOs from major technology companies create an atmosphere that prioritizes digital retail innovation. If you are a SaaS company, an AI platform, a data analytics provider, or any company whose value proposition is digital, NRF’s audience is more precisely aligned with your offering.

EuroShop covers the full spectrum of retail — both physical and digital — but its center of gravity is the physical retail environment. Store design, lighting, fixtures, visual merchandising, refrigeration, and POS hardware dominate the exhibition. The Retail Technology dimension in Halls 6 and 7a is growing rapidly, but EuroShop’s soul remains in the physical store. If your product touches the built retail environment, EuroShop is its natural stage.

Attendee Quality and Buyer Intent

Both shows attract serious buyers, but the buying dynamic differs. NRF attendees tend to arrive in “evaluation mode” — they are actively scouting technology solutions, comparing vendors, and often ready to move to pilot or procurement discussions within weeks of the show. The three-day format and the American trade show culture of direct, efficient business conversations accelerate the sales cycle.

EuroShop’s buying dynamic is more relationship-oriented. European and international buyers often use EuroShop as the beginning of a relationship rather than a transactional event. The five-day format allows for deeper conversations, return visits to booths, and the gradual trust-building that many international business cultures require before committing to a vendor. The sales cycle that starts at EuroShop may be longer, but the contracts that close tend to be larger and more durable.

The Verdict

Choose EuroShop if: You sell physical retail solutions (store design, fixtures, lighting, POS hardware, refrigeration). You want global or European market exposure. You can invest in a five-day show with international logistics. You value deep relationship building over fast sales cycles. You cannot afford to miss a triennial event.

Choose NRF if: You sell retail technology or SaaS solutions. Your primary market is North America. You prefer a fast-paced, three-day format with concentrated executive attendance. You want to be part of the retail innovation conversation driven by the NRF keynote programme.

Do both if: You have the budget and the team to execute at the highest level in both venues. NRF in January sets up conversations that EuroShop in February can close, especially with international prospects who attend both.

Which Show Is Right for You?

The answer is not about which show is “better” — it is about where your customers concentrate. If you are a European lighting manufacturer selling to global retailers, EuroShop is non-negotiable. If you are a Silicon Valley AI startup targeting US-based retail chains, NRF delivers a more targeted ROI. If you are a global retail technology company with both North American and international ambitions, the smartest strategy is to exhibit at NRF in January to build momentum and relationships, then close international deals at EuroShop five weeks later.

The one thing you cannot afford to do is ignore EuroShop’s triennial cycle. NRF happens every January. EuroShop happens every three years. The cost of skipping EuroShop is not a missed year — it is a missed three-year window of global retail presence. Plan accordingly.

Key Takeaway

EuroShop and NRF are not competitors — they are complements serving different segments of the retail industry. EuroShop owns physical retail and global reach. NRF owns retail technology and the North American market. Choose based on where your customers concentrate, not on which show has the bigger reputation.
