SuperZoo 2026 vs Global Pet Expo 2026: Which Pet Show Is Right for You?

ShowFloorTips Editorial February 11, 2026

The Pet Industry's Two Anchor Events

The American pet industry generates more than $150 billion in annual revenue, and its two largest trade shows reflect that scale. SuperZoo, produced by the World Pet Association (WPA), and Global Pet Expo, organized by the American Pet Products Association (APPA) in partnership with the Pet Industry Distributors Association (PIDA), are the anchoring events of the pet products calendar. Every serious player in the pet industry, from multinational manufacturers to independent pet boutique owners, attends at least one of these shows each year.

But they are not the same show in different cities. SuperZoo and Global Pet Expo have distinct personalities, serve different segments of the pet retail ecosystem, and offer different value propositions for exhibitors and attendees alike. Understanding those differences is essential for deciding where to invest your trade show budget, particularly if you can only attend one. This comparison provides the analysis you need to make that call.

Audience and Buyer Profile

The most important distinction between these two shows is the type of buyer who attends. SuperZoo has historically attracted a higher concentration of independent pet retailers, specialty boutique owners, pet grooming professionals, and pet services businesses. The show's education program, which includes grooming competitions, retail management workshops, and service-based business seminars, reflects this independent retailer focus. If you are a brand whose primary distribution channel is independent pet specialty stores, SuperZoo puts you in front of the store owners and buyers who make purchasing decisions for thousands of independent locations across North America.

Global Pet Expo attracts a broader and, in many ways, higher-volume buyer base. Mass-market retailers, large specialty chains, e-commerce buyers, and international distributors attend Global Pet Expo in significant numbers alongside independent retailers. The show's New Product Showcase is the industry's premier product launch platform, and the media attention it generates drives awareness among both retail buyers and end consumers. If your distribution strategy includes mass retail, online marketplaces, or international markets, Global Pet Expo provides access to the high-volume buyers who can move the needle on your revenue.

The international composition of the two shows also differs. Global Pet Expo draws a larger contingent of international buyers and distributors, making it the better venue for companies seeking to expand beyond North America. SuperZoo's international presence has been growing but remains smaller than Global Pet Expo's.

Show Floor and Exhibition Experience

SuperZoo at Mandalay Bay offers a compact, walkable exhibition spread across the convention center's main hall. The show floor is organized by product category, with dedicated zones for natural and organic products, aquatics, grooming, and new exhibitors. The scale is manageable enough that a focused attendee can see most of the show floor in two days. SuperZoo also features a unique Grooming Show within the show, with live grooming competitions that draw large crowds and create a energetic, engaging atmosphere on the floor. The Las Vegas setting adds entertainment and dining options that facilitate evening networking, though the heat of August in Las Vegas is a consideration for attendees planning to spend time outdoors.

Global Pet Expo at the Orange County Convention Center in Orlando occupies a larger footprint, reflecting its slightly larger exhibitor count and broader product range. The show floor is organized into sections including the New Product Showcase (featuring hundreds of product debuts), a dedicated international pavilion, and themed zones for categories like pet tech, sustainability, and aquatics. The three-day format and larger floor mean attendees need to plan their routes more carefully to ensure they see their priority exhibitors. Orlando in March offers pleasant weather, and the surrounding hotel and restaurant infrastructure provides ample options for off-floor meetings and networking.

Education and Programming

SuperZoo's education program is one of its strongest differentiators. The show offers an extensive schedule of seminars, workshops, and certification programs covering topics like retail merchandising, social media marketing for pet businesses, grooming techniques, and pet nutrition. The Groomer SuperShow and its associated competitions are a major draw, attracting professional groomers from across the country. For independent pet retailers and service providers looking to develop their business skills alongside sourcing new products, SuperZoo's education program delivers exceptional value.

Global Pet Expo's programming focuses more heavily on industry trends, market data, and new product discovery. APPA's annual State of the Industry presentation, delivered at Global Pet Expo, provides the benchmark data that shapes strategic planning across the pet industry for the entire year. The show's New Product Showcase, where hundreds of products make their industry debut, is the focal point of the event and generates significant media coverage. The emphasis is on what is new and what is next rather than on operational education for retailers.

Networking and Deal-Making

SuperZoo networking has a distinctly personal, community-oriented feel. The independent pet retail community is tight-knit, and many attendees have been coming to SuperZoo for years or even decades. The show's receptions, award ceremonies, and social events have a warm, collegial atmosphere that makes it easy for newcomers to break into conversations and build relationships. For brands that sell primarily through independent pet specialty, the relationships formed at SuperZoo often lead directly to purchase orders, as the buyers attending have immediate purchasing authority for their stores.

Global Pet Expo networking is more structured and commercially oriented. The presence of mass-market buyers, large chain representatives, and international distributors means that many conversations are conducted with formal procurement processes in mind. Exhibitors report that Global Pet Expo meetings are more likely to involve discussions about minimum order quantities, distribution terms, and logistics than the relationship-building conversations that characterize SuperZoo. The show organizes facilitated networking events, and many exhibitors schedule their key meetings well in advance through APPA's business matchmaking tools.

Cost and Logistics

SuperZoo in Las Vegas offers a wide range of hotel options at various price points, and August rates at Las Vegas properties tend to be more moderate than during peak convention season. The Mandalay Bay resort itself provides on-site accommodations, eliminating the need for daily commuting to the venue. Flights to Las Vegas are plentiful and generally affordable from most US cities. Registration costs for qualified retail buyers range from $75 to $175, making it accessible for independent retailers on tight budgets.

Global Pet Expo in Orlando also benefits from a strong hotel infrastructure and competitive airfares, though the March timing coincides with spring break travel, which can inflate hotel and flight prices somewhat. Registration is free for qualified retail buyers, which significantly lowers the barrier to attendance. The Orange County Convention Center is surrounded by hotel options, though most require shuttle or ride-share transportation to the venue, adding minor logistical complexity compared to SuperZoo's on-site resort setup.

The Verdict

Choose SuperZoo 2026 if your primary customers are independent pet retailers and specialty boutiques, if grooming products or services are central to your business, if you value a community-oriented networking environment, or if your team benefits from the show's extensive retail education programming. SuperZoo is also the better choice for companies launching products targeted specifically at the independent channel, where buyer feedback and relationship depth matter more than order volume.

Choose Global Pet Expo 2026 if you are seeking high-volume retail buyers, pursuing mass-market distribution, launching products that need industry-wide media exposure, expanding internationally, or using the show primarily as a commercial selling event. Global Pet Expo is also the better choice if access to APPA's industry data and market intelligence is a priority for your strategic planning.

For companies with the budget and team capacity, attending both shows is the optimal strategy. They serve complementary purposes at opposite ends of the calendar year: Global Pet Expo in March for product launches, trend identification, and large buyer meetings, followed by SuperZoo in August for independent retail relationship building, grooming industry engagement, and mid-year business development. The pet industry's most successful brands maintain a presence at both events because they understand that the independent and mass-market channels require different approaches, and these two shows are purpose-built for exactly that distinction.
