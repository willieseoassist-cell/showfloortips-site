Networking Guide

How to Network at Global Pet Expo 2026

February 11, 2026 • ShowFloorTips Editorial • 22 min read

Overview: Why Global Pet Expo Is THE Networking Event for the Pet Industry

Global Pet Expo 2026 returns to the Orange County Convention Center in Orlando, Florida from March 25 through 27, commanding its position as the pet industry's largest annual trade event in North America. Presented by the American Pet Products Association (APPA) and the Pet Industry Distributors Association (PIDA), this trade-only event brings together more than 1,000 companies across 3,500 or more booths, with over 3,000 new product launches and approximately 20,000 attendees converging on one of the most dynamic and resilient consumer industries on the planet. No other event in the pet world concentrates this volume of decision-makers, innovators, product developers, retail buyers, distributors, and industry leaders under a single roof for three intensely productive days of business.

What makes Global Pet Expo extraordinary for networking is the sheer breadth and depth of the pet industry itself. The American pet industry generates well over $150 billion in annual revenue, and it has demonstrated remarkable resilience through every economic cycle of the past two decades. The people walking those show floor aisles represent every segment of this massive market: premium pet food and nutrition, treats and chews, supplements and wellness, toys and enrichment, grooming and hygiene, accessories and apparel, aquatics and exotics, technology and smart devices, sustainability-focused products, and the rapidly expanding categories of pet health, pet services, and direct-to-consumer brands. The person standing next to you at any moment could be a national retail chain buyer deciding which products fill 2,000 store shelves, an independent pet boutique owner from Portland discovering the next viral product, a global distributor evaluating brands for the European or Asian market, or a venture-backed startup founder about to launch the product that redefines a category.

Global Pet Expo's trade-only format is a critical distinction that elevates the networking quality above consumer-facing pet events. Every person on that show floor has qualified for admission by demonstrating professional involvement in the pet industry. There are no casual consumers browsing for entertainment. This means every conversation has business potential, every badge represents a professional with purchasing authority or industry influence, and the networking density per square foot is among the highest of any trade show in any industry. This guide will show you exactly how to maximize every networking opportunity across all three days in Orlando, from the show floor to the evening events to the follow-up strategy that turns Orlando conversations into year-round business relationships.

Pre-Show Networking Preparation: Your Strategy Starts Weeks Before Orlando

Global Pet Expo is an enormous show. The Orange County Convention Center is one of the largest convention facilities in the United States, and when 3,500 booths fill its halls, the scale can be genuinely overwhelming for attendees who arrive without a plan. The professionals who extract the most value from their three days in Orlando are those who invest substantial preparation time in the weeks leading up to the event. Walking through thousands of booths without a strategy is not networking. It is an exhausting tour. Here is how to build a strategy that turns Global Pet Expo into a pipeline of meaningful business relationships that generate revenue for the next twelve months and beyond.

Define Your Networking Objectives with Precision

The pet industry is broad enough that you must narrow your focus before you step onto the show floor. Are you a retail buyer evaluating new product lines for the upcoming holiday season? A distributor searching for innovative brands to bring into international markets? A manufacturer looking for retail and distribution partnerships to scale your brand? A technology company seeking to embed your platform into pet products and services? A private equity or venture capital professional scouting investment opportunities in the fastest-growing pet categories? Write down three to five specific networking objectives before you register. These objectives will drive every decision you make about which zones to prioritize, which education sessions to attend, and which social events to show up at.

Be ruthlessly specific. Instead of "discover new products," write "identify five to eight premium natural dog treat brands with clean-label positioning, USDA-sourced ingredients, and margins that support independent retail pricing between eight and fourteen dollars per unit." Instead of "explore pet tech," write "meet three smart device companies whose products integrate with existing veterinary telehealth platforms and have demonstrated sell-through data in the specialty retail channel." Specificity transforms Global Pet Expo from an overwhelming product showcase into a focused mission with measurable outcomes. The show rewards professionals who arrive knowing exactly what they need, because the exhibitors on that floor are equally focused on finding serious partners who can move their products into the market.

Research the Exhibitor Directory and New Products Showcase

Global Pet Expo publishes its exhibitor directory, floor maps, and the critically important New Products Showcase catalog well in advance through the show website and app. Study the exhibitor list systematically. Filter by product category to identify your primary targets, and then research each company's website, social media presence, recent press coverage, and any industry awards or recognitions they have received. The New Products Showcase is particularly important because it features the products that exhibitors have specifically selected as their most innovative offerings for the year. These are the conversation starters, the products that buyers will be buzzing about, and the exhibitors who are most eager to connect with new partners.

Cross-reference the exhibitor list with the education session speaker lineup. If a target company has a founder, executive, or category expert presenting in the Global Learning Series or on the Main Stage, that session becomes a strategic priority. Attending their presentation gives you context for a deeper conversation and a natural reason to approach them afterward. LinkedIn is your essential research tool here. Identify the specific individuals you want to meet at each target company: the VP of sales, the national accounts manager, the brand founder, the product development lead. Know their names and faces before you arrive in Orlando. When you walk up to a booth and say "I saw that Sarah was presenting on the sustainability panel Thursday afternoon, and I wanted to follow up on the point she made about compostable packaging," you immediately signal that you are an engaged professional who does their homework.

Use Digital Platforms for Pre-Show Outreach

Global Pet Expo's official app and digital platform allow registered attendees and exhibitors to connect before the show opens. Activate your profile early, complete it thoroughly with your company information and product interests, and begin requesting meetings with your highest-priority targets at least three weeks before the show. The most productive attendees at Global Pet Expo arrive with ten to fifteen pre-scheduled meetings already confirmed. For companies where the platform does not provide enough traction, send direct LinkedIn messages. Your outreach should reference something specific: a product they recently launched, a market they recently entered, a trend report they published, or a challenge you share.

Social media is also a powerful pre-show tool. Follow Global Pet Expo's official accounts and relevant hashtags on LinkedIn, Instagram, and X. Many exhibitors begin their pre-show marketing campaigns weeks before Orlando, teasing new product launches, announcing booth numbers, and sharing behind-the-scenes content about what they are bringing to the show floor. Engage with this content publicly. Comment thoughtfully on posts from companies you want to meet. Share your own "getting ready for Global Pet Expo" content that explains what you are looking for and what you bring to a potential partnership. This creates name recognition so that when you introduce yourself at their booth, they may already know who you are and what you are about.

Understand the Registration and Qualification Process

Global Pet Expo is a trade-only event, and registration requires proof that you are a qualified pet industry professional. For buyers, this means demonstrating that you purchase at least $300 or more in pet products annually, which qualifies you for free buyer registration. Understanding the registration tiers and qualifying criteria matters for networking because it tells you something important about every person you meet on the show floor: they are verified professionals. This verification raises the baseline quality of every interaction. When someone hands you a business card at Global Pet Expo, you know they have been vetted. This is a significant advantage over consumer-facing events where networking quality is diluted by non-professional attendees.

Register early and complete your profile thoroughly. The more information you provide about your business, your product categories, and your purchasing interests, the better the show's matchmaking algorithms can connect you with relevant exhibitors and fellow attendees. Your badge category is also visible to exhibitors and other attendees, so make sure your registration accurately reflects your role and authority level. A buyer badge opens different conversations than a media badge or a service provider badge, and exhibitors allocate their time accordingly.

Key Takeaway

Global Pet Expo draws ~20,000 verified industry professionals to a trade-only environment. Pre-show preparation is not optional. Define specific objectives, research your targets and the New Products Showcase, pre-schedule meetings, and arrive in Orlando ready to have substantive business conversations from the moment the doors open.

Key Zones to Network In: Navigating Global Pet Expo's Show Floor

The Orange County Convention Center is a massive facility, and Global Pet Expo fills its halls with carefully organized zones that group exhibitors by category and industry focus. Understanding the geography of the show is essential for efficient networking. Here are the key zones where the most productive connections happen, including several exciting new additions for 2026.

New Products Showcase: The Heartbeat of Innovation

The New Products Showcase is the single most important networking zone at Global Pet Expo for anyone focused on discovering what is next in the pet industry. With more than 3,000 new product launches showcased at the 2026 edition, this area is where exhibitors put their most innovative, most marketable, and most buzzworthy products on display. The Showcase is organized by category, making it efficient to scan for products that align with your specific interests, whether that is natural pet food, interactive toys, grooming technology, or sustainable accessories.

The networking dynamic in the New Products Showcase is uniquely productive because the exhibitors staffing this area are specifically prepared to discuss what makes their product different, what market gap it fills, and why it is commercially viable. These are pitches honed for buyers, distributors, and media. Ask detailed questions about ingredient sourcing, manufacturing capacity, margin structure, and market positioning. The exhibitors who have invested in a New Products Showcase presence are the ones who are most actively seeking new partners, and they are ready for the kind of business-level conversation that moves beyond product features into commercial terms. Spend at least 90 minutes walking this zone on your first day, taking notes and flagging the exhibitors who deserve a deeper conversation at their main booth.

The Natural Pet Zone: Riding the Biggest Trend in Pet Products

The Natural Pet zone reflects the single most powerful consumer trend driving the pet industry: the demand for natural, organic, sustainably sourced, and clean-label products across every category from food and treats to grooming and supplements. This zone attracts the brands that are building their entire identity around natural positioning, and it draws the buyers and retailers who serve the growing population of pet parents who apply the same health-conscious purchasing criteria to their pets that they apply to themselves and their families.

Networking in The Natural Pet zone benefits from an understanding of the regulatory and certification landscape. Terms like "natural," "organic," "grain-free," "limited ingredient," and "sustainably sourced" carry specific meanings and regulatory implications that vary by product category and market. If you can speak intelligently about AAFCO guidelines, USDA organic certification, Non-GMO Project verification, or the evolving landscape of sustainability claims, you immediately elevate yourself as a knowledgeable partner in the eyes of exhibitors. This zone is where some of the most commercially valuable conversations at Global Pet Expo happen because the natural and premium segments command the highest margins and the most passionate consumer base in the pet industry.

The Modern Pet Zone: Where Design Meets Function

The Modern Pet zone showcases the intersection of aesthetics, functionality, and the evolving lifestyle of today's pet parents. This is where you will find the companies developing beautifully designed pet furniture that integrates into modern home interiors, fashion-forward collars and accessories, tech-enabled feeders and water fountains, and the products that reflect the reality that pets are family members whose products should be as thoughtfully designed as anything else in the home. The exhibitors in this zone tend to be brand-forward companies with strong visual identities and compelling stories about their design philosophy.

The networking in The Modern Pet zone often connects pet industry professionals with people who come from design, fashion, technology, and lifestyle backgrounds rather than traditional pet industry careers. This cross-pollination of perspectives makes conversations in this zone particularly stimulating and often sparks ideas that would not emerge in a more traditional pet product environment. If you are looking for products that photograph well for e-commerce and social media, that appeal to the millennial and Gen Z pet parent demographic, or that command premium price points through design differentiation, The Modern Pet zone is essential territory.

PSC Sustainability Pavilion: The Future of Responsible Pet Products

The Pet Sustainability Coalition (PSC) Sustainability Pavilion is one of the most strategically important zones at Global Pet Expo 2026. Sustainability has moved from a niche concern to a core business imperative across the pet industry, driven by consumer demand, retailer requirements, regulatory trends, and the genuine commitment of an industry that exists because people love animals and the planet those animals inhabit. The Pavilion showcases companies that are leading on sustainable packaging, responsibly sourced ingredients, carbon footprint reduction, circular economy models, and the full spectrum of environmental and social responsibility in pet products.

Networking in the Sustainability Pavilion connects you with the companies and professionals who are shaping the standards and expectations that will define the pet industry over the next decade. Retailers are increasingly requiring sustainability credentials from their suppliers. Consumers are increasingly willing to pay premium prices for products that align with their environmental values. Investors are increasingly evaluating sustainability as a component of long-term brand value. If you are not yet engaging with sustainability as a business strategy, the conversations in this Pavilion will make it clear why you need to start. If you are already committed to sustainability, this is where you will find the partners, suppliers, and collaborators who can help you advance your efforts.

Aquatics and Exotics Zone: The Specialty Opportunity

The Aquatics and Exotics zone serves the passionate and knowledgeable community of professionals who specialize in fish, reptiles, birds, small animals, and other non-traditional pets. This zone is often overlooked by attendees who focus exclusively on the dog and cat categories, but it represents a significant and growing market with a fiercely loyal consumer base that spends heavily on premium equipment, specialized nutrition, and habitat products. The exhibitors in this zone range from aquarium and terrarium manufacturers to specialty food producers to filtration and lighting technology companies.

The networking advantage in the Aquatics and Exotics zone is the depth of expertise and the tightness of the community. The aquatics and exotics industry is smaller and more specialized than the dog and cat market, which means the key decision-makers know each other. Breaking into this network as a new supplier, retailer, or distributor requires demonstrating genuine knowledge and commitment to the category. But once you earn credibility within this community, the relationships are exceptionally strong and the competition for shelf space is less intense than in the mainstream pet categories. If you have any interest in diversifying your product mix or retail offering into aquatics and exotics, this zone is where you build the foundational relationships.

Supplier Pavilion: Building Your Supply Chain

The Supplier Pavilion at Global Pet Expo connects finished goods companies with the ingredient suppliers, packaging manufacturers, contract manufacturers, logistics providers, and service companies that form the backbone of the pet products supply chain. This zone is essential for any brand that is developing new products, reformulating existing lines, evaluating new packaging solutions, or looking to optimize their manufacturing and distribution operations.

The networking dynamic in the Supplier Pavilion is different from the product-focused zones because the conversations are inherently about partnership rather than transaction. A packaging supplier is not trying to sell you a single order; they are trying to become your long-term packaging partner. A contract manufacturer is not pitching a one-time production run; they are offering to become an extension of your operations. These are foundational business relationships, and the Supplier Pavilion is the most efficient place in the pet industry to evaluate multiple potential partners side by side in a single visit.

NEW for 2026: Global Petfluence Studio

One of the most exciting additions to Global Pet Expo 2026 is the Global Petfluence Studio, a dedicated content creation space designed for brands, influencers, and media professionals to produce high-quality content during the show. This zone recognizes the reality that social media and influencer marketing have become essential channels for pet product discovery and brand building. The Studio provides professional lighting, backdrops, and production support for creating the kind of polished content that performs on Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, and other platforms.

The networking opportunity in the Petfluence Studio is unique because it brings together two groups that are actively seeking each other: brands looking for influencer partnerships and content creators looking for brand collaborations. If you are a brand with products that photograph and video well, having a presence in or near the Studio puts you in front of the content creators who can amplify your reach. If you are an influencer or content creator, the Studio is where you will find brands that understand the value of creator partnerships and are prepared to invest in them. The Studio also attracts the marketing agencies, PR firms, and social media strategists who facilitate these partnerships, making it a hub for the business of pet marketing.

NEW for 2026: Tech Innovation Lab and Pet Marketing Innovation Lab

The Tech Innovation Lab is another significant new addition for 2026, providing a dedicated space for technology companies to demonstrate the latest innovations in pet technology, from AI-powered health monitoring devices to smart home integration for pet care to advanced e-commerce and retail technology platforms. This lab-style environment encourages hands-on exploration and deeper technical conversations than are typically possible at a standard exhibition booth.

The Pet Marketing Innovation Lab takes an even more interactive approach with a hackathon-style format that brings together marketing professionals, technologists, and creative thinkers to collaboratively solve real marketing challenges facing pet brands. Participating in or observing the hackathon is a networking accelerator because collaborative problem-solving creates bonds that passive observation never can. If you join a hackathon team, you spend hours working closely with professionals from different companies and disciplines, and those shared intellectual experiences often forge stronger connections than any number of booth conversations.

Day-by-Day Networking Strategy for Global Pet Expo 2026

Day 1: Wednesday, March 25 — Lay the Foundation and Set the Pace

The opening day of Global Pet Expo sets the tone for your entire show experience. Arrive at the Orange County Convention Center early. Orlando traffic can be unpredictable, especially on International Drive where the convention center is located, so give yourself extra travel time on Day 1. Register, collect your badge, and pick up the official show guide and map. Spend the first 60 to 90 minutes doing an orientation walk through the halls. Do not stop for deep conversations yet. Instead, map out where your priority exhibitors are located, assess booth traffic patterns, identify the zones that deserve your most focused attention, and locate the New Products Showcase, the education stages, and the key gathering areas where you will spend time between meetings.

Use the late morning and early afternoon for your pre-scheduled meetings. Day 1 is the best day for planned one-on-one conversations because exhibitors are fresh, fully staffed, and energized by the excitement of the show opening. If you pre-scheduled meetings with your top targets, stack your most important ones on Wednesday. Between meetings, attend the opening Main Stage Keynote. The keynote attracts the most senior attendees at the show and sets the intellectual agenda for the entire event. The networking in the audience before and after the keynote is exceptionally high quality because you are surrounded by the leaders and decision-makers who prioritize being in the room for the show's marquee content.

After the keynote, dedicate focused time to the New Products Showcase. Walk the entire Showcase methodically, taking photos and notes on the products that align with your objectives. Tag each product with your reaction: "must visit booth," "interesting but not urgent," or "not relevant." This systematic approach gives you a curated list of booths to visit on Day 2 and Day 3, which is far more efficient than wandering the show floor hoping to stumble onto the right products. The Showcase is also a prime networking zone because it attracts the most innovation-focused attendees, and you can learn a tremendous amount by listening to how other buyers and retailers react to the products on display.

Wednesday afternoon is also the ideal time to explore the education sessions in the Global Learning Series. The 2026 show features six distinct education tracks: Tech and Innovation, Data and Insights, Brand Storytelling, Redefining Retail, Financial Perspectives, and Pet Sustainability. Choose one or two sessions that align with your objectives and attend them not just for the content but for the networking. The audience at a session on "Redefining Retail" self-selects for retailers and retail-focused brands, which means every person in that room is a potential business contact who shares your focus area. Introduce yourself to the people sitting near you, exchange perspectives on the presentation, and use the session as a springboard for deeper conversations on the show floor.

Wednesday evening brings the Unleashed Party, the official welcome reception that has become one of the most anticipated social events on the pet industry calendar. This is not a casual afterthought. The Unleashed Party is a deliberately designed networking environment where the social barriers that exist on the show floor dissolve. People are relaxed, drinks are flowing, music is playing, and the atmosphere encourages the kind of genuine personal connection that accelerates business relationships. Prioritize the Unleashed Party above any other Wednesday evening activity. Arrive early, work the room with intention, and use the social energy of the event to deepen the connections you began building on the show floor during the day.

Day 2: Thursday, March 26 — Go Deep and Expand Your Network

Thursday is the pivot day at Global Pet Expo, the day where your preparation and Day 1 reconnaissance converge into the most productive booth conversations of the show. Start the morning by visiting the exhibitors you flagged during your New Products Showcase walk on Wednesday. You have already seen their products and formed initial impressions, so you can arrive at their booth with specific, informed questions that immediately signal you are a serious buyer or partner. This approach saves the exhibitor the time of their standard pitch and moves the conversation directly to the business-level topics that matter: pricing, minimums, exclusivity, distribution terms, and timeline.

Dedicate the mid-morning to exploring zones you did not cover thoroughly on Wednesday. If you spent Day 1 focused on the natural and sustainability zones, use Thursday morning to explore The Modern Pet zone, the Aquatics and Exotics area, or the Supplier Pavilion. Some of the most valuable connections at Global Pet Expo come from unexpected encounters with companies or product categories you did not initially plan to explore. The pet industry is increasingly interconnected, and a conversation with a packaging supplier in the Supplier Pavilion might solve a challenge you have been struggling with for months, or a product in the Aquatics zone might reveal a category expansion opportunity you had not considered.

Thursday is also the day to maximize the new features of Global Pet Expo 2026. Visit the Global Petfluence Studio and observe the content being created. If your brand has a visual product, explore the possibility of creating content in the Studio or connecting with the influencers and content creators who are using it. Spend time in the Tech Innovation Lab, especially if technology integration is part of your business strategy. If the Pet Marketing Innovation Lab hackathon is running, attend as a participant or observer. These new zones are where the most forward-thinking professionals at the show will congregate, and the networking there will be with the people who are actively shaping the future of the industry.

Thursday afternoon is prime time for the second Main Stage Keynote and the deeper education sessions. By Day 2, you have enough context from your show floor conversations to get maximum value from the education content. You can ask better questions, challenge assumptions, and connect the insights from the stage to the real products and companies you have been evaluating on the floor. The networking in the education sessions is also richer on Day 2 because many attendees are ready to share their impressions and discoveries, creating a more collaborative and open atmosphere.

Thursday evening features the Topgolf Buyer Networking event, which is specifically designed to connect buyers with exhibitors and industry leaders in a relaxed, social environment outside the convention center. Topgolf's format is inherently social and interactive, which breaks down the formality of the trade show dynamic and creates opportunities for the kind of genuine personal connection that drives long-term business relationships. If you can secure an invitation or registration for the Topgolf event, treat it as a high-priority networking opportunity. The shared activity of hitting golf balls and the casual atmosphere make it easy to strike up conversations with people you might not have approached on the show floor.

By Thursday afternoon and evening, you should also begin your same-day follow-up on Wednesday's conversations. If an exhibitor said they would send you a sample kit or a pricing sheet, send a quick email or LinkedIn message confirming your interest and referencing a specific point from your conversation. This same-day follow-up separates you from the hundreds of other buyers who had similar conversations and signals that you are a serious prospect who moves with urgency and professionalism.

Day 3: Friday, March 27 — Close, Consolidate, and Build for the Year Ahead

The final day of Global Pet Expo is your closing window, and the professionals who use it strategically extract disproportionate value from their three days in Orlando. Attendance is typically lighter on the last day, which creates a significant advantage for serious networkers. Exhibitors have more available time, less competition for their attention, and often a genuine willingness to engage in longer conversations about partnership structures, pricing details, and concrete next steps. If there is a conversation you need to close or a commitment you need to confirm, Friday morning is your best opportunity.

Use the first two hours of Friday for your most important follow-up visits. Return to the exhibitors who represent your highest-priority opportunities and confirm concrete next steps: a sample shipment, a follow-up video call, a visit to their manufacturing facility, a formal quotation, or a meeting at their headquarters. Get specific about dates and deliverables. The exhibitors who hear "I would like to schedule a video call during the week of April 6 to review the distribution terms we discussed, and I will have my purchasing director on the call" are far more likely to follow through than those who hear "Let us keep in touch." Specificity communicates seriousness, and seriousness earns priority in a post-show follow-up queue that may include hundreds of contacts.

Friday is also the day to circle back to the zones and exhibitors that surprised you during the show. Perhaps you discovered a category you had not previously considered, or an exhibitor outside your initial target list made a compelling case for their product. Use the lighter Friday traffic to have extended conversations with these unexpected discoveries. The pet industry rewards curiosity, and some of the most successful product introductions and partnerships in the industry's history started as unexpected discoveries at Global Pet Expo.

Attend any remaining education sessions that are relevant to your objectives. The Friday sessions often feature the most practical, actionable content because the speakers can reference what has been happening on the show floor throughout the week. The audience is also more engaged and participatory on the final day, which elevates the quality of the Q&A discussions that often produce the most valuable insights.

Before you leave the Orange County Convention Center for the last time, spend 30 minutes in a quiet area reviewing every contact you collected across all three days. Categorize them into three tiers: immediate follow-up required within 48 hours, follow-up within two weeks, and long-term relationship nurturing. Add context notes for each contact while the conversations are still fresh: what products you discussed, what their specific business challenges are, what next steps you agreed to, and any personal details that will make your follow-up message feel genuinely personal rather than templated. This 30-minute investment is arguably the most important networking activity of your entire Global Pet Expo experience because it determines whether your three days in Orlando translate into lasting business value or fade into a pile of forgotten business cards.

Friday evening brings the Pet Summit After Hours Party, which serves as both a celebration of the show and a final networking opportunity. The After Hours Party is where attendees reflect on their discoveries, share their highlights, and solidify the relationships they built during the show. The energy is a mix of celebration and genuine business connection, and many attendees report that some of their most productive conversations happen at the closing party because three days of shared experience have created a foundation of familiarity and mutual respect that accelerates relationship building.

"Global Pet Expo is where I build my product pipeline for the entire year. Every major brand relationship, every breakthrough product I have brought to our shelves, every category expansion we have made in the past decade started with a conversation on that show floor in Orlando. There is no substitute for being there, walking those aisles, and connecting with the people who are creating the future of this industry."
— Vice President of Merchandising, national specialty pet retail chain

Orlando After-Hours Networking: Where Business Continues After the Show Floor Closes

Orlando offers a vibrant landscape for after-hours networking that extends the productive energy of the show floor into the evening. The conventions center's location on International Drive places attendees within easy reach of countless dining, entertainment, and social venues that fill with pet industry professionals every evening during Global Pet Expo.

International Drive Dining and Entertainment

International Drive, known locally as I-Drive, is the main commercial corridor near the Orange County Convention Center and the default gathering zone for Global Pet Expo attendees after the show closes. The stretch of I-Drive between the convention center and the major resort hotels features dozens of restaurants ranging from casual chains to upscale dining experiences. During Global Pet Expo week, many of these restaurants become de facto networking venues where you can spot attendee badges on tables and strike up conversations with fellow professionals. The restaurants with outdoor patios and bar areas are particularly effective for casual networking because the social atmosphere lowers barriers and encourages conversation.

Pointe Orlando, a dining and entertainment complex on I-Drive, is a popular destination for Global Pet Expo attendees seeking a concentrated area with multiple restaurant options. The advantage of Pointe Orlando is that you can move between venues easily, which allows you to maximize your exposure to different groups of attendees throughout the evening. Arrive around 7:00 PM when the post-show dinner crowd is at its peak, grab a seat at a bar or a communal table, and you will inevitably end up in conversation with industry professionals who are eager to decompress and talk shop.

Resort Hotel Lobbies and Bars

The major resort hotels near the convention center become the evening networking headquarters during Global Pet Expo. Hotels like the Rosen Centre, the Rosen Plaza, the Hilton Orlando, and the Hyatt Regency Orlando serve as the official or unofficial host hotels for the show, and their lobbies, restaurants, and pool bars fill with pet industry professionals every evening. The lobby bar at a host hotel is one of the most reliably productive networking environments at any trade show because everyone present is an attendee, and the social convention of hotel lobby networking is well understood across the industry.

If you are staying at one of the host hotels, use your lobby as a networking home base. Position yourself in the bar or lounge area during the 6:00 to 9:00 PM window and watch the networking happen organically. If you are staying elsewhere, it is still worth spending an evening or two at the host hotel bars. The concentration of senior industry executives, brand founders, and key decision-makers in these venues is often higher than at any formal networking event on the show's official calendar.

Themed Attractions and Group Outings

Orlando's world-famous entertainment and attraction ecosystem provides unique networking opportunities that do not exist at trade shows in most other cities. During Global Pet Expo week, it is common for exhibitor groups, industry associations, and informal networks to organize outings to venues like ICON Park, Topgolf (beyond the official event), or dinner cruises. These shared experiences create memorable bonds that strengthen business relationships. If you receive an invitation to a group outing during the show, say yes. The professionals who participate in these experiences often develop the deepest and most lasting relationships of the show because they have shared something beyond a business card exchange.

Orlando's restaurant scene beyond I-Drive also offers excellent networking opportunities for those willing to venture further. The restaurant districts in Winter Park, the Mills 50 corridor, and downtown Orlando attract the food-savvy attendees who prefer to explore the local dining scene. Organizing a dinner at a well-regarded Orlando restaurant and inviting a mix of exhibitors, buyers, and industry colleagues is one of the highest-return networking investments you can make during the show. A shared meal with eight to ten carefully selected industry professionals often generates more actionable business outcomes than an entire day of booth visits.

Official Evening Events: Unleashed Party, Topgolf, and Pet Summit After Hours

The official evening events at Global Pet Expo 2026 deserve special emphasis because they represent the show's most deliberately designed networking opportunities. The Unleashed Party on Wednesday evening is the welcome reception that sets the social tone for the entire show. The Topgolf Buyer Networking event on Thursday provides an activity-based networking format that is particularly effective for building relationships with buyers and retail professionals. The Pet Summit After Hours Party on Friday serves as the closing celebration and final networking opportunity. These three events create a social rhythm across the three days that complements the business intensity of the show floor. Attend all three if possible, because the relationships you build in these social settings often become the framework for the business conversations you have during the day.

Key Takeaway

Orlando's hospitality infrastructure turns every evening into a networking opportunity. From I-Drive restaurants to host hotel lobbies to the official Unleashed Party and Topgolf events, the after-hours scene at Global Pet Expo is where business relationships deepen and personal connections form.

Post-Show Follow-Up Strategy: Turning Orlando Conversations into Year-Round Business

The pet industry is a relationship industry. The contacts you make at Global Pet Expo have value that extends far beyond the immediate transaction. Your follow-up strategy must reflect both the urgency of short-term opportunities and the long-term relationship potential of this passionate, interconnected community.

During the Show: Nightly Contact Review

Every evening after the show closes and before you head out to evening networking, spend 20 minutes reviewing the contacts you collected that day. For each significant connection, record what you discussed, what their specific business challenge or opportunity is, what products or services they are evaluating, and any next step you agreed to. Include personal details: what market they serve, whether they mentioned a new store opening or a product line expansion, what they said about their experience at the show. These personal notes are what transform a generic follow-up into a message that demonstrates you were genuinely engaged in the conversation and value the relationship as more than a transaction.

Within 48 Hours: Personalized Follow-Up Messages

The pet industry is social, tight-knit, and fast-moving. The 48-hour window after Global Pet Expo closes on Friday is your most important follow-up period. Send personalized messages that reference specific details from your conversation. "It was great meeting you at your booth on Thursday. Your new line of freeze-dried raw toppers looks like a perfect fit for our premium natural section, and I think the 8-ounce format you showed me would be ideal for our independent retail customers. Can we schedule a 30-minute call during the week of April 6 to discuss pricing, minimum orders, and the exclusivity window for our region?" This level of specificity proves the conversation mattered and invites a concrete next step.

Use LinkedIn as your primary follow-up platform for professional connections. Send connection requests the same weekend the show ends, while the experience is still fresh for both of you. Include a personalized note that references your conversation and the specific topic you discussed. For commercial follow-ups, email is more appropriate because it allows you to attach product information, pricing documents, and other materials that advance the business discussion. The professionals who follow up on both LinkedIn and email within 48 hours establish themselves as organized, serious, and worth prioritizing in the post-show flood of communications.

Within Two Weeks: Concrete Next Steps

For your highest-priority contacts, the two-week mark should bring a concrete next step. A video call to review product specifications and packaging options. A sample shipment of the product you evaluated at the booth. A formal quotation with volume pricing and distribution terms. An invitation to visit their manufacturing facility or your retail locations. A shared document outlining the partnership terms you discussed. The pet industry rewards action and follow-through. People who convert show floor conversations into specific business activity within two weeks earn trust, and trust is the currency of long-term business relationships in this community where reputation travels fast and recommendations carry enormous weight.

Ongoing Quarterly Engagement

The pet industry produces a constant stream of content and activity: new product launches, store openings, industry research reports from APPA, regulatory updates, seasonal planning cycles, and event announcements. Share relevant content with your Global Pet Expo contacts quarterly. Congratulate them on milestones. Introduce them to other people in your network who could add value. Forward industry research that is relevant to their business. The professionals who become central nodes in the pet industry network are those who are consistently generous with their knowledge, connections, and attention. Global Pet Expo happens once a year, but the relationships you build there should generate value every quarter. When you see your contacts at SuperZoo later in the year or at the next Global Pet Expo, the relationship should feel continuous rather than annual.

Key Takeaway

Your 48-hour post-show follow-up window is the most important networking activity of the entire show. Personalized messages with specific references to your conversations and concrete next steps are what convert trade show contacts into business partners and long-term industry relationships.

Tips for International Attendees: Navigating Global Pet Expo from Abroad

Global Pet Expo draws significant international attendance, with buyers, distributors, and industry professionals traveling from dozens of countries to source American pet products, explore US market trends, and build cross-border business relationships. If you are attending from outside the United States, these tips will help you maximize your networking effectiveness and navigate the practical aspects of doing business in Orlando.

Visa and Travel Planning

International attendees should begin their visa and travel planning well in advance of the show. Depending on your country of origin, you may need a B-1 business visa to attend Global Pet Expo, and processing times can vary significantly. The show's official invitation letter can support your visa application, so contact the organizers early if you need documentation. Orlando International Airport (MCO) receives direct flights from many international destinations, and the airport is approximately 20 minutes from the Orange County Convention Center by car or rideshare. Book your flights and hotel early because Orlando hotel rates near the convention center increase significantly during major events.

Understanding American Trade Show Culture

American trade show culture is generally informal, direct, and fast-paced compared to trade shows in many other countries. Exhibitors at Global Pet Expo expect to engage in business conversations quickly. There is less emphasis on extended personal relationship building before business discussion compared to trade shows in the Middle East, Asia, or parts of Europe. This does not mean relationships do not matter; they matter enormously in the pet industry. But the cultural norm at an American show is to get to the business topic efficiently while being friendly and personable. A firm handshake, direct eye contact, a clear statement of who you are and what you are looking for, and a genuine smile will open virtually every door at Global Pet Expo.

American business cards are exchanged casually compared to the formal rituals in many Asian business cultures. However, handing someone your card while referencing a specific conversation point shows professionalism in any culture. If you are attending from a country where business card exchange is ritualistic, feel free to present your card with the formality you are accustomed to. American professionals will appreciate the gesture even if they do not reciprocate it with the same level of ceremony.

Currency, Payments, and Pricing Discussions

All pricing at Global Pet Expo will be discussed in US dollars. International buyers should be prepared to discuss pricing in USD terms and understand the implications of currency exchange rates for their purchasing decisions. If you are a distributor or retailer evaluating products for import into your home market, arrive with a clear understanding of your landed cost calculation: product cost in USD, shipping costs, customs duties for pet products in your country, regulatory compliance costs, and your target retail margin. Exhibitors at Global Pet Expo are accustomed to working with international buyers and will appreciate your preparation on pricing and logistics.

Shipping and Regulatory Considerations

If you are an international buyer planning to import pet products from exhibitors you meet at Global Pet Expo, understand the regulatory landscape in your home market before you arrive. Pet food and treat regulations vary dramatically by country, and many American pet products require specific certifications, labeling modifications, or ingredient adjustments to comply with international regulations. Arriving at Global Pet Expo with a clear understanding of your country's pet product import requirements allows you to have productive regulatory conversations with exhibitors and positions you as a knowledgeable and reliable international partner. Many exhibitors have existing international distribution and can share their experience with export compliance, but they will prioritize working with buyers who have already done their regulatory homework.

Language and Communication

English is the language of business at Global Pet Expo, and all signage, education sessions, and official communications are in English. However, the show attracts exhibitors and attendees from many countries, and you may encounter opportunities to network in Spanish, Portuguese, Mandarin, Japanese, Korean, German, French, or other languages. If you share a language with a potential business partner, using it creates an immediate bond and can differentiate you from competitors who can only communicate in English. If your English is limited, consider bringing a bilingual colleague or business partner who can facilitate conversations. The pet industry is global, and exhibitors who are serious about international distribution are accustomed to working across language barriers and will be patient and accommodating.

Leveraging Global Pet Expo for Market Intelligence

For international attendees, Global Pet Expo is not only a sourcing event but also the single best opportunity to understand the American pet market, which is the largest in the world. The trends you observe on the show floor in Orlando often predict the trends that will emerge in your home market 12 to 24 months later. The education sessions provide data-driven insights into American consumer behavior, retail trends, and category performance that can inform your business strategy regardless of where you operate. The APPA research team presents industry data at the show that is not available anywhere else, and attending these sessions gives you access to market intelligence that can shape your purchasing and product development decisions for the year ahead.

Conversation Starters That Work at Global Pet Expo

Global Pet Expo's trade-only format and passionate attendee base mean your conversation starters should signal industry knowledge, purchasing intent, and genuine enthusiasm for the products and categories you are discussing.

At a pet food or treat booth: "We are seeing enormous demand for limited-ingredient proteins in the premium segment, and our customers are specifically asking for novel proteins like rabbit, venison, and bison. What is your sourcing story for the proteins in this line, and can you walk me through your supply chain transparency?" This signals a knowledgeable buyer with real consumer demand behind their inquiry, which earns immediate attention from any pet food exhibitor.

In the New Products Showcase: "This is one of the most interesting products I have seen today. Can you tell me about the development process and what consumer insight drove the design? We serve a premium customer base that values innovation, and I am always looking for products that give our stores a differentiation advantage." Genuine curiosity combined with a clear description of your retail positioning creates a productive conversation.

In the Natural Pet zone: "We are expanding our natural and organic section to represent 40 percent of our total shelf space by Q4. What certifications does this product line carry, and how do you support retailers with merchandising and consumer education materials?" This communicates a concrete purchasing plan and immediately qualifies you as a high-priority buyer in the eyes of a natural pet brand.

At the Sustainability Pavilion: "Sustainability is becoming a differentiator for us with our customer base, and we are evaluating every product category through a sustainability lens. How are you addressing packaging waste, and what does your roadmap look like for moving to post-consumer recycled or compostable materials?" This positions you as a forward-thinking retailer whose sustainability commitment creates a business case for the exhibitor's investment in sustainable practices.

In the Tech Innovation Lab: "We are trying to integrate smart feeding and health monitoring data into our customer loyalty program so we can provide personalized product recommendations. Has your platform been integrated with any retail or e-commerce systems yet?" Technical specificity combined with a real business application invites a substantive conversation rather than a surface-level product demo.

At an evening networking event: "What has been the standout product or trend for you at the show this year? I have been focused on the natural zone and the sustainability pavilion, but I am curious what is catching people's attention in other categories." This works across every attendee category because it invites people to share their enthusiasm and expertise, which is the universal language of the pet industry.

Education Tracks: Learning as a Networking Strategy

Global Pet Expo 2026 features an expanded education program with six distinct tracks and the new Global Learning Series on the show floor. Using education sessions as a networking strategy is one of the most underutilized approaches at any trade show, and at Global Pet Expo, where the education content is specifically designed for pet industry professionals, the sessions attract precisely the people you want to meet.

Tech and Innovation Track

This track attracts the technology companies, digitally-forward retailers, and innovation-focused brand executives who are shaping the future of the pet industry. Sessions cover topics from AI and machine learning in pet health to the evolution of e-commerce and omnichannel retail. The attendees in these sessions are the early adopters and strategic thinkers in the industry, and networking with them positions you at the leading edge of industry evolution.

Data and Insights Track

The Data and Insights track features presentations from APPA's research team and other industry analysts who present the market data, consumer behavior research, and category performance metrics that drive strategic decision-making across the pet industry. The attendees in these sessions tend to be the most analytically oriented professionals at the show: category managers, business development executives, and strategy leaders who make decisions based on data rather than intuition. If you are a brand or supplier, networking with data-driven buyers is extremely valuable because they are looking for products backed by market evidence.

Brand Storytelling Track

The Brand Storytelling track focuses on how pet brands build emotional connections with consumers through narrative, visual identity, content marketing, and social media strategy. This track attracts marketing directors, brand founders, content creators, and the agency professionals who support them. Networking here connects you with the financial decision-makers who drive industry consolidation and growth. These two tracks attract some of the most senior and strategically focused attendees at the show.

Choose sessions strategically, and use the shared experience of education content as a foundation for deeper business conversations.

Networking Etiquette at Global Pet Expo

Respect the Trade-Only Environment

Global Pet Expo is a trade-only event, and every person on that show floor has earned their badge by demonstrating professional involvement in the pet industry. Honor this environment by treating every interaction as a professional engagement. Do not bring non-industry family members or friends onto the show floor. Do not treat product samples as free souvenirs. Do not spend extended time at a booth without a genuine business purpose. The trade-only format is what makes the networking quality at Global Pet Expo exceptional, and every attendee shares the responsibility of maintaining that professional standard.

Respect Exhibitor Time and Investment

Exhibitors at Global Pet Expo invest tens of thousands of dollars in their booth presence: design, construction, product displays, shipping, travel, staffing, and samples. Respect their investment by arriving at their booth with a clear purpose. Introduce yourself, state your role and your interest, and ask if this is a good time for a conversation. If the booth is busy, exchange contact information and schedule a specific time to return. The exhibitors who feel their time is respected are the ones who invest the deepest energy in the relationship. And remember that those product samples have real cost. Take samples of products you are genuinely evaluating, not products you intend to give to friends and family.

Manage Your Energy Across Three Days

Global Pet Expo is an intense three-day experience. The combination of constant walking, continuous conversation, loud environments, product evaluation, education sessions, evening events, and the mental effort of processing thousands of visual and verbal inputs is genuinely exhausting. Pace yourself. Take breaks. Eat properly. Stay hydrated in Orlando's warm climate. The networkers who are sharp and energetic on Friday afternoon are the ones who managed their energy wisely across all three days. Burning out by Thursday evening means missing the valuable final-day networking opportunities that Friday provides, including the Pet Summit After Hours Party.

Photography and Social Media Etiquette

Always ask permission before photographing products, booth displays, or people at Global Pet Expo. Many exhibitors are showcasing pre-launch products or proprietary designs and may have restrictions on photography and social media sharing before an official launch date. When you do post on social media, tag the exhibitor and the show, use the official hashtag, and add genuine commentary rather than just posting a photo. Exhibitors notice and appreciate social media engagement that adds value, and a thoughtful social post tagging a company can strengthen the relationship you began at their booth.

Best Networking Spots Inside and Around the Convention Center

The New Products Showcase Area

As discussed earlier, the New Products Showcase is the most concentrated networking zone at Global Pet Expo. The area attracts the most innovation-focused buyers, media, and industry leaders. The foot traffic is consistently high, and the products on display provide natural conversation starters with everyone around you. Position yourself in the Showcase area during the late morning or mid-afternoon when traffic is at its peak, and engage with the other professionals who are evaluating the same products.

Education Session Rooms and the Global Learning Series Stage

The education session rooms and the on-floor Global Learning Series stages are prime networking zones. The five minutes before a session begins and the ten minutes after it concludes are your windows. The audience has self-selected based on shared professional interest, giving you an immediate conversation starter with every person in the room. Sit near the front where you are visible and accessible, and use the post-session energy to introduce yourself to speakers, panelists, and the attendees who asked the most insightful questions.

The Main Stage Area

The two Main Stage Keynotes at Global Pet Expo 2026 draw the largest and most senior audiences at the show. The area surrounding the Main Stage before and after keynotes functions as an informal networking lounge. Arrive ten minutes early and use the waiting time to meet the people around you. After the keynote, linger in the area and engage with attendees who are processing and discussing the content they just heard. Main Stage networking connects you with the industry's most engaged and forward-thinking professionals.

Food and Beverage Areas

The food courts, coffee stands, and concession areas within the Orange County Convention Center are among the most reliably productive networking zones at the show. The simple act of standing in a food line or sitting at a shared table creates natural proximity and a social context that invites conversation. During the mid-morning coffee break and the lunch window, these areas fill with attendees who are between meetings and open to connection. If you see someone wearing a badge for a company you want to meet, the food area is one of the most natural places to introduce yourself.
