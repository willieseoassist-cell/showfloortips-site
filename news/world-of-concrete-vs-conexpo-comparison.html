World of Concrete vs ConExpo: Which Should You Exhibit At?

ShowFloorTips · February 9, 2026

Two trade shows dominate the construction calendar, and both call Las Vegas home. World of Concrete in January owns the concrete and masonry niche. ConExpo-Con/Agg in March owns the entire construction equipment universe. For exhibitors caught between them, the decision is not about which is "better" -- it is about which aligns with how you sell, who you sell to, and what you can afford.

World of Concrete: The Profile

World of Concrete takes place at the Las Vegas Convention Center, January 20-23, 2026. The event draws approximately 60,000 attendees who are laser-focused on concrete, masonry, and related construction trades. This is not a generalist crowd. These are concrete contractors, ready-mix producers, decorative concrete specialists, and masonry professionals who came to see what is new in their specific world.

Booth pricing: $3,000 - $20,000.

The show's outdoor demonstration areas are a distinguishing feature. Exhibitors can run live equipment demos, pour concrete, and showcase products in action. For companies whose products need to be seen working -- not just displayed behind glass -- this is a rare and valuable format.

ConExpo-Con/Agg: The Profile

ConExpo-Con/Agg takes place at the Las Vegas Convention Center, March 3-7, 2026. It is the largest construction trade show in North America and one of the three largest in the world, drawing approximately 130,000 attendees across five days. The show covers every segment of construction: earthmoving, lifting, paving, aggregates, concrete, utilities, and more.

Booth pricing: $5,000 - $40,000.

ConExpo runs on a three-year cycle, making each edition a high-stakes opportunity. The show occupies over 2.8 million square feet of exhibit space, including massive outdoor lots where Caterpillar, John Deere, and Volvo stage their largest equipment. The scale is difficult to overstate.

Head-to-Head: What the Numbers Say

Specialist vs. Generalist

This is the core distinction. World of Concrete delivers a highly concentrated audience of concrete and masonry professionals. If your product is a concrete admixture, a finishing tool, a decorative overlay system, or a masonry saw, nearly every person walking past your booth is a potential customer. The signal-to-noise ratio is exceptional.

ConExpo's audience is broader. The 130,000 attendees include everyone from crane operators to aggregate producers to paving contractors. If your product serves multiple construction segments -- think GPS machine control, safety equipment, fleet management software -- ConExpo's breadth is an advantage. If your product is concrete-specific, you may find yourself competing for attention with excavators and haul trucks.

The Cycle Factor

World of Concrete runs annually, giving you a consistent yearly touchpoint with your market. ConExpo runs every three years. Missing a ConExpo edition means waiting until 2029 for the next one. This makes ConExpo a must-attend for companies that need to make a major statement to the broad construction market, but it also means the pressure to perform is significantly higher.

Cost Comparison

World of Concrete's booth costs top out around $20,000 for premium space, while ConExpo can reach $40,000 for comparable positioning. But the real cost gap is in total investment. ConExpo runs five days versus World of Concrete's four. The larger show demands a larger booth, more staff, and more aggressive pre-show marketing to cut through the noise of 2,800+ exhibitors. A mid-size exhibitor might spend $40,000 all-in at World of Concrete and $80,000-$120,000 at ConExpo.

Both shows are in Las Vegas, which keeps travel and logistics relatively comparable. However, Las Vegas hotel rates during ConExpo week are notoriously inflated due to the show's massive draw.

"World of Concrete is where concrete professionals buy. ConExpo is where the construction industry gathers. Know the difference, and choose accordingly."

-- Associated General Contractors of America

The Verdict

If your products and services are specific to the concrete, masonry, or decorative concrete markets, World of Concrete is your anchor event. The audience concentration, lower total cost, and annual frequency make it the most efficient way to reach your buyers. Add ConExpo in three-year intervals when you want to expand visibility across the broader construction market.

If your products serve multiple construction segments -- equipment, software, safety, fleet management -- ConExpo is the must-attend event despite its higher cost and three-year cycle. The sheer scale of audience and media attention make it the industry's premier brand-building opportunity. Use World of Concrete annually to maintain presence in the concrete niche between ConExpo years.

Broad construction companies should anchor on ConExpo and use World of Concrete to maintain niche presence. Both are in Las Vegas, making a dual strategy logistically straightforward.
