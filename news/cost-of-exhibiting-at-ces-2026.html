How Much Does It Cost to Exhibit at CES 2026?

ShowFloorTips Editorial February 11, 2026

CES (Consumer Electronics Show) is the world's largest and most influential consumer technology trade show. Held every January at the Las Vegas Convention Center, CES 2026 drew more than 138,000 attendees across 2.9 million square feet of exhibit space. If you are planning to exhibit at CES 2027 or evaluating whether CES makes financial sense for your brand, this guide walks through every major cost category so you can build an accurate budget.

Booth Space Costs

CES booth rental is priced per square foot, and rates vary by hall location, booth type, and whether you qualify for early-bird or member pricing. Here are typical 2026 rates:

  • 10x10 inline booth (100 sq ft): $4,200 - $5,500 ($42 - $55/sq ft)
  • 10x20 inline booth (200 sq ft): $8,400 - $11,000
  • 20x20 island booth (400 sq ft): $16,800 - $22,000
  • Corner premium: 10-20% surcharge on base rates
  • Eureka Park (startup area): $1,500 - $4,000 for a tabletop display

Early-bird discounts of 15-20% typically require commitment six or more months before the show. CES opens space selection in the spring for the following January event, and prime locations fill quickly.

Booth Design and Construction

After securing floor space, your second largest expense is the physical booth structure. Options range from a simple pop-up banner to a fully custom build:

  • Pop-up or portable display: $500 - $2,000
  • Custom modular 10x10: $10,000 - $30,000
  • Custom 20x20 island: $40,000 - $100,000+
  • Rental booth (turnkey): $3,000 - $15,000 for a 10x10; $12,000 - $23,000 for a 10x20

Renting instead of building saves 40-60% and eliminates long-term storage fees. Many first-time CES exhibitors start with rental booths to test the show before investing in a custom build.

Drayage and Material Handling

Drayage is the cost of moving your freight from the loading dock to your booth space, and it is one of the most commonly underestimated expenses at CES. The Las Vegas Convention Center uses Freeman as its official general contractor:

  • Standard drayage rate: $150 - $200 per CWT (per 100 lbs)
  • Typical 10x10 booth shipment (800-1,200 lbs): $1,200 - $2,400
  • Typical 20x20 booth shipment (2,500-4,000 lbs): $3,750 - $8,000
  • Special handling surcharge: 30-50% premium for crates, oversized items, or uncrated materials

You can reduce drayage costs by shipping lightweight materials, consolidating shipments, and avoiding last-minute deliveries that incur overtime labor charges.

Electrical and Internet

Power and connectivity are mandatory for most CES exhibits but are not included in your booth rental:

  • Standard electrical outlet (500W/5A): $350 - $800
  • Higher-power service (20A/120V): $600 - $1,200
  • Dedicated Wi-Fi connection: $800 - $2,500
  • Hardwired ethernet: $1,200 - $3,000

Order electrical and internet services early. Advance-order pricing is typically 20-30% lower than on-site rates, and CES show floor Wi-Fi is unreliable for product demos.

Travel and Lodging

Las Vegas hotel rates surge 2-3x during CES week, and availability is limited. Budget for your team well in advance:

  • Hotel (4 nights, CES week): $1,200 - $3,000 per person
  • Flights: $300 - $800 per person
  • Meals and entertainment: $75 - $150 per person per day
  • Ground transportation (rideshare, taxi, shuttle): $200 - $400 total per person

For a team of four over four nights, travel costs alone can reach $8,000 - $16,000. Booking hotels through the CES housing block may offer modest savings and guaranteed availability, but off-Strip hotels can save 30-50% over convention corridor properties.

Marketing Materials and Promotion

Getting noticed at CES requires investment beyond the booth itself:

  • Pre-show email campaign: $500 - $2,000
  • Printed brochures and collateral: $300 - $1,000
  • Branded giveaways and swag: $500 - $3,000
  • Press outreach and media kits: $1,000 - $5,000
  • Social media advertising: $500 - $2,000

CES also offers official sponsorship and advertising packages that range from $5,000 to well over $100,000 for premium placements.

Additional Show Services

  • Carpet and padding (10x10): $300 - $500
  • Furniture rental (table, chairs, counter): $200 - $800
  • Lead retrieval scanner: $300 - $500 per unit
  • Audio-visual equipment: $500 - $3,000
  • Cleaning service: $100 - $300

The median first-time CES exhibitor with a 10x10 booth spends approximately $25,000 - $35,000 all-in. Startups in Eureka Park can get in for under $15,000 total. Large brands with 20x20+ island booths frequently spend $100,000 to $200,000 or more.

Tips to Reduce Your CES Costs

  1. Book early: Secure booth space and hotel rooms 6-9 months out for the best rates.
  2. Rent your booth: A turnkey rental saves 40-60% compared to custom construction.
  3. Ship smart: Use lightweight materials and consolidate shipments to reduce drayage fees.
  4. Consider Eureka Park: If you qualify as a startup (under 3 years old with recent funding), this is the most affordable entry point.
  5. Stay off-Strip: Hotels 10-15 minutes from the convention center can save $100-200 per night.
  6. Pre-order all services: Electrical, internet, and furniture cost 20-30% more when ordered on-site.
