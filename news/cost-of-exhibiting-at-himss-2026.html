How Much Does It Cost to Exhibit at HIMSS 2026?

ShowFloorTips Editorial February 11, 2026

HIMSS (Healthcare Information and Management Systems Society) Global Health Conference is the largest healthcare IT event in the world. HIMSS 2026 takes place March 9-13 at The Venetian Convention & Expo Center in Las Vegas and attracts over 45,000 healthcare professionals, including hospital CIOs, clinical informaticists, and health system procurement officers. For health technology companies, exhibiting at HIMSS represents one of the highest-value lead generation opportunities in the industry. Here is what it costs.

Booth Space Costs

HIMSS booth rental rates are among the highest in the trade show industry, reflecting the high-value audience:

  • 10x10 inline booth (100 sq ft): $4,500 - $6,000 ($45-$60/sq ft)
  • 10x20 inline booth (200 sq ft): $9,000 - $12,000
  • 20x20 island booth (400 sq ft): $18,000 - $24,000
  • Startup showcase / tabletop: $3,000 - $5,000

HIMSS uses a priority point system for returning exhibitors. First-time exhibitors typically get space assignment after the rebook cycle, which means less desirable locations unless you book early and negotiate directly with the HIMSS sales team.

Booth Design and Construction

Healthcare IT exhibitors generally invest in polished, professional booth environments:

  • Pop-up display with monitors: $1,000 - $3,000
  • Custom modular 10x10: $10,000 - $35,000
  • Custom 20x20 island with demo stations: $40,000 - $120,000
  • Rental booth (turnkey): $4,000 - $15,000 for 10x10

Demo stations are critical at HIMSS. Budget for at least 2-3 large monitors or tablets for product demonstrations, as HIMSS attendees expect hands-on product experiences.

Drayage and Material Handling

The Venetian Expo uses union labor for all material handling:

  • Standard drayage rate: $160 - $210 per CWT
  • Typical 10x10 booth shipment (800-1,200 lbs): $1,280 - $2,520
  • Typical 20x20 booth shipment (2,500-4,000 lbs): $4,000 - $8,400
  • Overtime and weekend surcharges: 1.5x - 2x standard rates

The Venetian's mandatory union labor rules mean you cannot self-carry most booth materials. Plan for this cost and ship early to avoid overtime premiums.

Electrical and Internet

  • Standard electrical (500W/5A): $400 - $900
  • 20A/120V service: $700 - $1,400
  • Dedicated Wi-Fi: $1,000 - $3,000
  • Hardwired internet: $1,500 - $3,500

Reliable internet is essential for HIMSS exhibitors running cloud-based health IT demos. Budget for a dedicated connection and bring a mobile hotspot as backup.

Travel and Lodging

Las Vegas hotel rates during HIMSS are elevated but slightly less extreme than during CES:

  • Hotel (4 nights): $1,000 - $2,500 per person
  • Flights: $300 - $800 per person
  • Meals and entertainment: $75 - $150 per person per day
  • Ground transportation: $200 - $400 per person
  • Client dinners: $500 - $3,000

For a team of four, travel costs range from $8,000 to $18,000. HIMSS attendees expect professional hospitality, so budget for at least one or two client dinners.

Marketing and Lead Generation

  • Pre-show email campaign: $500 - $2,000
  • HIMSS conference guide ad: $3,000 - $8,000
  • Lead retrieval system: $400 - $600 per scanner
  • Printed collateral: $500 - $1,500
  • Giveaways: $500 - $2,000
  • HIMSS sponsorship packages: $10,000 - $100,000+

A startup with a 10x10 booth should budget $22,000 - $40,000 all-in for HIMSS. Mid-market companies typically spend $50,000 - $100,000. Enterprise health IT companies with large island booths regularly invest $150,000 - $300,000+.

Tips to Reduce Your HIMSS Costs

  1. Start with the Startup Showcase: Affordable tabletop spaces designed for early-stage health tech companies.
  2. Rent instead of build: Turnkey rental booths save 40-60% over custom construction.
  3. Ship early: Avoid overtime drayage charges by getting freight to The Venetian before the deadline.
  4. Book hotel through HIMSS block: Official housing blocks sometimes offer better rates than direct booking.
  5. Focus on appointments: Pre-schedule meetings with target prospects to maximize booth time efficiency.
  6. Use Scannly for lead capture instead of renting expensive on-site scanners.
