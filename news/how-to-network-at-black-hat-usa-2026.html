How to Network at Black Hat USA 2026: A Complete Guide

Published on February 11, 2026 • 8 min read

Black Hat USA is the cybersecurity industry's most technically rigorous conference, attracting approximately 20,000 attendees to the Mandalay Bay Convention Center in Las Vegas each August. Unlike broader security conferences, Black Hat draws a heavily technical crowd: penetration testers, vulnerability researchers, security architects, and the engineers who build and break things. Networking here requires a different approach, one rooted in technical credibility and community respect. This guide will help you make the most of your time at Black Hat USA 2026.

Pre-Show Preparation: Getting Ready for the Technical Crowd

Black Hat attendees are a discerning audience. Preparation here means more than packing business cards. It means coming armed with knowledge and a clear sense of purpose.

  • Define your networking purpose. Are you a security researcher looking to connect with peers? A vendor trying to reach technical buyers? An enterprise security leader evaluating tools? A job seeker looking for your next red team role? Your purpose should drive everything from which sessions you attend to which parties you prioritize.
  • Review the Briefings schedule. Black Hat Briefings are peer-reviewed presentations on cutting-edge security research. Review the accepted talks and identify sessions that align with your expertise or interests. Knowing the research landscape gives you credibility in hallway conversations.
  • Sign up for Trainings if applicable. The first two days of Black Hat are dedicated to hands-on training courses. These intimate, multi-day classes of 20 to 40 students create strong bonds between participants. If budget allows, a training course is one of the best networking investments you can make.
  • Engage the community online. Follow Black Hat speakers on X (Twitter), Mastodon, and LinkedIn. Share their research, comment thoughtfully on their work, and introduce yourself digitally before the event. The infosec community on social media is active and responsive.
  • Prepare your own story. In a room full of technical talent, what makes you interesting? Maybe you discovered a novel vulnerability, built an open-source tool, or led a major incident response. Have a concise narrative ready that establishes your credibility.

Key Networking Venues and Events at Black Hat USA 2026

Black Hat Briefings

The Briefings are the heart of the conference. These 25- and 50-minute presentations cover zero-day research, novel attack techniques, and defensive innovations. Sitting in a Briefings session gives you an automatic conversation starter with everyone around you. After a compelling talk, approach the speaker or engage with other audience members about the implications of the research.

The Business Hall

The Business Hall is Black Hat's expo floor, featuring several hundred cybersecurity vendors. It opens on the second and third days of the main conference. Visit the Arsenal area within the Business Hall, where open-source tool developers demonstrate their projects live. Arsenal presenters are often security practitioners rather than sales teams, making these interactions more technical and authentic.

Black Hat Arsenal

Arsenal deserves special mention. This is where security researchers demo open-source tools they have built. The conversations here are deeply technical and collegial. If you have built something, consider applying to present at Arsenal. If you are attending, these demonstrations are excellent networking opportunities with builders and makers in the community.

DEF CON (The Week After)

DEF CON takes place immediately following Black Hat, also in Las Vegas. Many attendees stay for both. If your schedule and budget allow, attending DEF CON extends your networking window significantly. The vibe at DEF CON is more casual, community-driven, and hacker-oriented, which leads to different kinds of connections than the more corporate Black Hat.

Vendor Parties and Community Events

Las Vegas during Black Hat week is packed with evening events. Major security companies host dinners and parties at venues along the Strip. Community-organized meetups happen at bars and restaurants near Mandalay Bay. The Hacker Summer Camp social media channels and sites like InfoSec Conferences publish event listings. Some of the most memorable networking happens at these informal gatherings.

The Hallway Track

Experienced Black Hat attendees know that the "hallway track" is often more valuable than the sessions themselves. The areas between briefing rooms, the coffee stations in the convention center, and the hotel lobbies are where spontaneous conversations happen. Do not feel guilty about skipping a session to continue a great conversation in the hallway.

Cybersecurity-Specific Networking Tips for Black Hat

  • Lead with technical substance. Black Hat attendees can spot a pure salesperson from across the room. Even if you are in a business development role, demonstrate genuine technical understanding of the problems you solve. Ask technical questions and listen carefully to the answers.
  • Respect the hacker culture. The Black Hat and DEF CON community has deep roots in hacker culture, which values transparency, skill, and community contribution over titles and corporate affiliations. Do not be dismissive of independent researchers or dismiss unconventional approaches.
  • Share your research. If you have published research, given talks, or contributed to open-source projects, mention it naturally in conversation. Technical contributions are the currency of credibility in this community.
  • Be cautious with your devices. This is a security conference, and the network is famously hostile. Use a VPN, avoid connecting to unknown Wi-Fi networks, and consider bringing a dedicated travel laptop. Showing good security hygiene earns respect.
  • Participate in CTFs and challenges. Several Capture the Flag competitions and hacking challenges run during Black Hat week. Participating in these events is both fun and an excellent way to bond with other technical professionals under pressure.

Best Apps and Tools for Networking at Black Hat

  • Scannly. Capture badge and business card information instantly. In the fast-paced Black Hat environment, you do not want to be fumbling with a stack of cards. Scan, tag with notes, and move on to the next conversation.
  • Black Hat Official App. Access the full schedule, floor maps, and exhibitor details. Mark sessions you want to attend and get reminders so you do not miss key talks.
  • Signal. The preferred messaging app for security professionals. Many Black Hat attendees will share their Signal handle rather than a phone number. It demonstrates mutual respect for privacy and security.
  • LinkedIn. Still the primary platform for professional connections in cybersecurity. Send connection requests quickly after meeting someone, with a personalized note.
  • GitHub. If you are networking with researchers and tool builders, follow their GitHub profiles. Star their repositories and engage with their projects. This is more meaningful in the Black Hat community than a LinkedIn endorsement.

On-Site Strategies for Maximum Impact

  • Arrive for Trainings if possible. The two days of training before the main conference are smaller and more focused. The relationships you build in a multi-day training class can become some of your strongest professional connections.
  • Visit the Business Hall with purpose. Make a list of the vendors and Arsenal presentations you want to see. Walk the floor with a plan rather than drifting randomly from booth to booth.
  • Stay hydrated and rested. Las Vegas is hot in August, and Mandalay Bay is a sprawling complex. Dehydration and fatigue will undermine your networking energy. Drink water, take breaks, and do not try to attend every party.
  • Wear your conference badge visibly. It sounds obvious, but your badge is a conversation starter. People will glance at your name and company before deciding whether to engage. Make sure it is visible and not flipped backward.
  • Be open to serendipity. Some of the best connections at Black Hat come from unexpected encounters: a conversation at the hotel pool, a shared Uber ride, or a chance meeting at a coffee shop. Stay open and approachable throughout the week.

Follow-Up Strategies After Black Hat

  • Follow up within 48 hours. The post-conference window is short. Send personalized messages that reference your specific conversation, not generic follow-ups.
  • Share research and resources. If you discussed a particular vulnerability, tool, or technique, send relevant links, papers, or code repositories. Adding value immediately strengthens the connection.
  • Connect on multiple platforms. Unlike most conferences, Black Hat connections often span LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Mastodon, Signal, and GitHub. Connect on the platforms your new contact actually uses.
  • Attend local security meetups. Keep the momentum going by attending BSides events, OWASP chapter meetings, and local hacker meetups in your city. Many Black Hat connections will appear at these events throughout the year.
  • Plan for next year. If Black Hat was valuable, start planning for the following year immediately. Submit a CFP for Briefings or Arsenal, and begin nurturing the relationships you built this year.

Final Thoughts

Black Hat USA 2026 is where the cybersecurity industry's most technical minds converge. With 20,000 attendees at Mandalay Bay, the event is large enough to offer incredible diversity of connections but focused enough to maintain a strong community feel. Come prepared with technical knowledge, genuine curiosity, and respect for the hacker ethos. The relationships you build at Black Hat can accelerate your career, strengthen your security program, and connect you to a global community of professionals who are passionate about protecting the digital world.
