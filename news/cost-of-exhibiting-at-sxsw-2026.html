How Much Does It Cost to Exhibit at SXSW 2026?

ShowFloorTips Editorial February 11, 2026

SXSW (South by Southwest) has grown from a music festival into one of the most important convergence events in technology, film, and culture. Held annually in Austin, Texas, SXSW 2026 runs March 12-18 and draws over 70,000 attendees from the tech, entertainment, and creative industries. The SXSW Trade Show and Expo offers exhibitors a chance to showcase products to a uniquely diverse, innovation-focused audience. Here is what it actually costs.

Booth Space Costs

SXSW exhibitor pricing is handled through direct sales inquiries, and rates are not publicly listed. Based on exhibitor reports and industry data, here are typical ranges for the SXSW Expo:

  • Tabletop display: $3,500 - $5,000
  • 10x10 booth (100 sq ft): $5,500 - $8,000
  • 10x20 booth (200 sq ft): $10,000 - $15,000
  • 20x20 island booth (400 sq ft): $20,000 - $30,000

SXSW also offers different exhibiting contexts, including the main Interactive Expo, the Gaming Expo, and the SXSW EDU Expo. Pricing varies by event. Contact the SXSW sales team at sales@sxsw.com for current rates and availability.

Booth Design and Construction

SXSW has a more casual, startup-friendly culture than traditional trade shows. You do not necessarily need a polished custom booth to make an impact:

  • Pop-up banner and table display: $300 - $1,500
  • Modular custom 10x10: $5,000 - $20,000
  • Custom 20x20 experiential build: $25,000 - $75,000
  • Rental booth (turnkey): $2,500 - $12,000

Many successful SXSW exhibitors focus on interactive experiences and product demos rather than elaborate booth structures, which can keep construction costs lower than at traditional trade shows.

Drayage and Material Handling

The Austin Convention Center handles drayage through its official contractor. Rates for SXSW tend to be slightly lower than Las Vegas shows:

  • Standard drayage rate: $100 - $160 per CWT (per 100 lbs)
  • Typical 10x10 booth shipment (500-800 lbs): $500 - $1,280
  • Typical 20x20 booth shipment (1,500-3,000 lbs): $1,500 - $4,800

Because SXSW skews toward tech demos and lightweight displays, many exhibitors can hand-carry their materials and avoid drayage costs entirely.

Electrical and Internet

  • Standard electrical outlet (500W/5A): $250 - $600
  • 20A/120V service: $500 - $1,000
  • Dedicated Wi-Fi: $600 - $1,800
  • Hardwired internet: $800 - $2,000

If you are running product demos that depend on internet connectivity, invest in a dedicated connection. The convention center Wi-Fi can be overloaded during peak hours.

Travel and Lodging

Austin hotel rates spike dramatically during SXSW week. Downtown availability is extremely limited if you do not book months in advance:

  • Hotel (5 nights, SXSW week): $1,500 - $3,000 per person (downtown), $600 - $1,200 (outside downtown)
  • Flights: $250 - $700 per person
  • Meals and entertainment: $75 - $150 per person per day
  • Ground transportation (rideshare): $150 - $400 per person total
  • SXSW conference badges (optional): $1,000 - $1,900 per person

For a team of three over five nights, travel costs range from $5,000 to $15,000. Many companies use short-term rental apartments as a more affordable alternative to downtown hotels.

Marketing Materials and Promotion

  • Pre-show email and social outreach: $500 - $2,000
  • Printed collateral and business cards: $200 - $800
  • Branded swag and giveaways: $500 - $2,500
  • SXSW event sponsorship: $5,000 - $50,000+
  • Off-site activation or party: $5,000 - $50,000+

SXSW is as much about networking off the show floor as on it. Many brands invest in evening events, house parties, or pop-up activations in addition to their booth presence.

Additional Show Services

  • Furniture rental: $200 - $600
  • A/V equipment: $400 - $2,000
  • Lead retrieval system: $200 - $400
  • Carpet and flooring: $200 - $400
  • Cleaning service: $75 - $200

A lean startup exhibiting at SXSW with a tabletop can get in for under $10,000. A mid-range 10x10 exhibitor should budget $18,000 - $30,000. Brands building large experiential activations at SXSW frequently spend $75,000 - $200,000+.

Tips to Reduce Your SXSW Costs

  1. Start with a tabletop: Test the SXSW audience before committing to a larger booth.
  2. Book lodging early: Austin hotels sell out 3-6 months before SXSW. Consider short-term rentals.
  3. Travel light: SXSW rewards interactive demos over heavy booth infrastructure. Skip the drayage.
  4. Leverage organic buzz: SXSW attendees are highly active on social media. A compelling demo can generate free press.
  5. Skip the official party: Hosting a small gathering at a local venue is far cheaper than official sponsorship packages.
