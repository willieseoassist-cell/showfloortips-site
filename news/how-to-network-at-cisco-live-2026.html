How to Network at Cisco Live 2026: The Complete Exhibitor's Guide

16 min readFebruary 12, 2026ShowFloorTips Research Team

Cisco Live 2026 is the IT industry's most important annual gathering for network professionals, and the 2026 edition promises to be the biggest yet. Running May 31 through June 4 at the Mandalay Bay Convention Center in Las Vegas, Cisco Live brings together more than 20,000 IT professionals -- network engineers, security architects, cloud specialists, collaboration experts, and technology executives -- for five days of technical education, hands-on labs, certification opportunities, and some of the most energetic networking events in the technology conference world.

But Cisco Live is not a passive conference where you sit in sessions and collect slide decks. It is an immersive, high-energy experience designed to connect you with the people, technologies, and communities that will shape your IT career. From technical breakout sessions and hands-on labs where you configure live equipment alongside fellow engineers, to certification exams you can take on-site, epic receptions that rival concert productions, and gamification competitions that turn the entire convention center into a networking game, Cisco Live has built networking into the DNA of every format. This guide will show you how to maximize every opportunity across all five days, plus the free global broadcast that extends the event's reach worldwide.

Step 1: Choose the Right Pass

Cisco Live offers several pass tiers, and your choice directly impacts the quality and range of networking opportunities available to you. The Full Conference Pass is the gold standard, granting access to all technical sessions, hands-on labs, the World of Solutions exhibition floor, keynote presentations, and the legendary social events including the Customer Appreciation Event. This is the pass we recommend for anyone serious about networking.

IT Management Pass

The IT Management Pass is designed for technology leaders and executives who are less focused on deep technical sessions and more interested in strategic content, leadership discussions, and executive-level networking. This pass includes access to the CIO Exchange and executive networking sessions that bring together CTOs, CIOs, and IT directors from enterprise organizations around the world.

Free Global Broadcast

Cisco Live also offers a free global broadcast that streams keynotes, select technical sessions, and community content to anyone worldwide. While this does not replace the in-person networking experience, it serves as a valuable pre-event research tool. Watch sessions from previous years to identify speakers and topics that align with your interests, then prioritize those tracks when building your in-person schedule.

Pro Tip: Book Labs Early

Hands-on labs are among the most valuable networking environments at Cisco Live. You spend hours configuring real Cisco equipment alongside other engineers, which creates natural bonds that no keynote presentation can replicate. Popular labs fill up fast, so register for your preferred sessions immediately when the schedule builder opens. If a lab is full, add yourself to the waitlist -- cancellations are common as schedules shift.

Step 2: Prepare Your Networking Arsenal

Cisco Live attracts a technically sophisticated audience. Your preparation should reflect the depth of knowledge and specificity that IT professionals expect in conversation.

Technical Business Cards

Standard business cards work, but consider adding your Cisco certifications (CCNA, CCNP, CCIE, DevNet), your primary technology focus areas, and a QR code linking to your LinkedIn profile or technical blog. At Cisco Live, certifications are a social currency that immediately establishes your technical credibility and creates common ground with fellow attendees.

Download the Cisco Live App

The Cisco Live app is essential for schedule management, session registration, interactive maps, and the gamification program. The app also enables in-event messaging with other attendees, making it a powerful tool for arranging impromptu meetings. Familiarize yourself with the app before the event so you can hit the ground running on Day 1.

Join the Cisco Learning Network

Before arriving in Las Vegas, join the Cisco Learning Network community forums and introduce yourself in the Cisco Live 2026 threads. Engage with discussions about session recommendations, study groups, and meetup plans. Many of the strongest networking connections at Cisco Live begin online weeks before the event and are cemented in person at the convention center.

Essential Pre-Show Actions

  • Register for hands-on labs and technical sessions as soon as the schedule builder opens
  • Join the Cisco Learning Network and Cisco Live social media conversations (#CiscoLive)
  • Identify and reach out to 20-30 target contacts via LinkedIn
  • Schedule certification exams if you plan to test on-site
  • Download the Cisco Live app and complete your attendee profile
  • Review the World of Solutions exhibitor list and bookmark priority booths
  • Prepare your technical elevator pitch: certifications, specialization, and what you are looking for

Step 3: Research Sessions and Target Contacts

Cisco Live's technical program is enormous, with hundreds of sessions spanning networking, security, collaboration, data center, cloud, IoT, automation, and developer technologies. Strategic session selection is critical not just for learning but for networking with the right peers.

Session Types and Networking Value

Breakout Sessions are 45-90 minute technical deep dives led by Cisco engineers and industry experts. These attract focused audiences with shared technical interests, making post-session conversations highly productive. Hands-On Labs are multi-hour sessions where you configure live equipment -- these are the highest-networking-value sessions at the entire event because the collaborative environment creates natural bonds. DevNet Sessions focus on programmability and automation, attracting a younger, developer-oriented crowd that is increasingly influential in IT purchasing decisions. Instructor-Led Labs provide guided technical training in small groups, combining education with networking in an environment that feels more like a study group than a conference session.

The World of Solutions

The exhibition floor -- called the World of Solutions -- features Cisco's own product demonstrations alongside partner companies and emerging technology startups. Unlike traditional trade shows where you walk a floor of independent booths, the World of Solutions is orchestrated by Cisco to create a unified experience around solution areas. This means that walking through the networking section, for example, will expose you to both Cisco platforms and the ecosystem of partners and integrators that surround them. This is where you meet the people who actually deploy and manage the technologies you are evaluating.

Step 4: Technical Sessions and Hands-On Labs

The technical program is the beating heart of Cisco Live, and it is where the most meaningful professional relationships are formed. Engineers who learn together, troubleshoot together, and solve problems together form bonds that outlast any exchange of business cards.

The Lab Partner Strategy

In hands-on labs, you will be seated at a workstation alongside other engineers working through the same configuration exercises. Introduce yourself to your lab neighbors. Ask what they are working on in their day jobs. Offer help if you see them stuck on a configuration step. These micro-interactions build genuine professional rapport because they demonstrate competence and generosity -- two qualities that make people want to stay connected with you.

Post-Session Networking Windows

The five minutes after a breakout session ends are the most valuable networking window of the hour. Speakers are approached by a cluster of attendees, and the hallway outside the session room becomes an impromptu networking zone. Prepare a specific question or comment related to the session content. Approach the speaker with a reference to something they discussed, and you will position yourself as a peer worth connecting with rather than a passive audience member.

DevNet Zone

The DevNet Zone is Cisco Live's dedicated space for developers and automation professionals. It features coding challenges, API workshops, and informal meetups that attract some of the most innovative IT professionals at the event. Even if you do not consider yourself a developer, visiting the DevNet Zone exposes you to the people who are automating network operations -- and these are increasingly the decision-makers who influence technology purchasing.

Pro Tip: The Certification Testing Networking Hack

If you are taking a certification exam at Cisco Live, use the testing center waiting area as a networking zone. Everyone there shares your commitment to professional development, and the nervous energy of exam preparation creates an instant bond. After the exam, celebrate (or commiserate) with fellow test-takers over coffee. These connections are some of the most authentic you will make all week.

Step 5: Epic Receptions, Meetups, and Gamification

Cisco Live is famous for its social events, which go far beyond typical conference receptions. The production quality rivals major concerts, and the energy level is unmatched in the IT conference world.

Customer Appreciation Event

The Customer Appreciation Event (CAE) is Cisco Live's signature social experience. In previous years, Cisco has rented out entire entertainment venues and brought in A-list musical acts. The CAE is open to all Full Conference Pass holders and is the single best networking event of the week. Arrive early, explore the venue, and use the relaxed atmosphere to connect with people you have been meaning to approach all week. The shared experience of an extraordinary evening creates natural conversation starters and lasting memories that cement professional relationships.

Meetups and Birds of a Feather Sessions

Cisco Live organizes meetups around specific technology areas, career interests, and community groups. These smaller gatherings are networking gold because they bring together 20-50 professionals who share your exact focus area. Whether it is a Security meetup, a Wireless meetup, or a Women in Technology gathering, these sessions provide the intimate setting that large-scale events cannot replicate. Check the Cisco Live app for the full meetup schedule and register early.

Gamification Competitions

Cisco Live's gamification program turns the entire convention center into a networking game. By attending sessions, visiting exhibitor booths, completing challenges, and interacting with the Cisco Live app, you earn points that can be redeemed for prizes. The gamification program is more than a gimmick -- it incentivizes the exact behaviors (attending diverse sessions, visiting the World of Solutions, engaging with the community) that lead to high-quality networking. The leaderboard creates friendly competition that gives you a natural conversation topic with fellow attendees.

Step 6: The Follow-Up System

Cisco Live generates an enormous volume of technical connections. Your follow-up strategy should reflect the technical nature of the relationships you have built.

The 48-Hour Technical Follow-Up

For contacts you made in technical sessions and labs, send a follow-up that references the specific technology or configuration you discussed: "Great working through the SD-WAN lab with you on Tuesday. Your approach to the branch deployment scenario was really clean. I would love to continue that conversation -- are you open to a 30-minute call next week to compare notes on our respective deployments?" This kind of specific, technical follow-up demonstrates that you paid attention and valued the interaction.

LinkedIn and the Cisco Community

Connect on LinkedIn with a personalized note that references your shared Cisco Live experience. Also connect on the Cisco Learning Network if you met during study groups or certification-related activities. The Cisco community is remarkably active year-round, and maintaining these connections through online forums creates opportunities for ongoing knowledge exchange that extends far beyond the five days in Las Vegas.

Day-by-Day Networking Blueprint

Day 1 - Sunday, May 31

Opening Day: Orientation and Early Connections

Morning: Arrive early for registration and badge pickup. Explore the Mandalay Bay Convention Center layout. Locate the World of Solutions, lab spaces, and session rooms on the interactive map.

Afternoon: Attend opening sessions and keynotes. Use the large gatherings as opportunities to meet seatmates and engage in post-keynote discussions.

Evening: Attend any early-arrival networking events or community meetups. Join #CiscoLive conversations on social media to connect with fellow attendees.

Day 2 - Monday, June 1

Technical Deep Dive: Sessions and Labs

Morning: Attend your highest-priority breakout sessions. Sit near the front and engage with speakers during Q&A. Network with seatmates during breaks.

Afternoon: Complete your first hands-on lab. Introduce yourself to lab partners and exchange contact information. Visit the World of Solutions exhibition floor.

Evening: Attend technology-specific meetups (Security, Wireless, Data Center, etc.). These small-group events produce the highest-quality connections of the week.

Day 3 - Tuesday, June 2

Peak Day: Maximum Engagement

Morning: Continue with technical sessions and labs. Explore the DevNet Zone if automation and programmability are relevant to your work.

Afternoon: Revisit priority exhibitors in the World of Solutions for deeper conversations. Take a certification exam if scheduled.

Evening: Attend the Customer Appreciation Event. This is the biggest networking opportunity of the week. Set a target of connecting with at least 10 new contacts.

Day 4 - Wednesday, June 3

Consolidation: Deepen Relationships

Morning: Attend remaining must-see sessions and labs. Focus on tracks you have not yet explored to diversify your connection portfolio.

Afternoon: Schedule one-on-one meetings with your most important new contacts. Use the Cisco Live app to find available meeting spaces.

Evening: Host or join a small-group dinner with contacts you want to deepen. Las Vegas offers endless dining options near Mandalay Bay.

Day 5 - Thursday, June 4

Final Day: Close Strong

Morning: Attend closing sessions and any remaining labs. Exchange final contact information with people you have connected with throughout the week.

Afternoon: Use the quieter afternoon for wrap-up conversations. Visit any remaining World of Solutions booths.

Evening: Begin your 48-hour follow-up process. Send LinkedIn connections and personalized messages while conversations are fresh.

Certification Networking Strategy

Cisco Live offers on-site certification testing, and the certification ecosystem creates one of the most powerful networking structures at the event. Professionals studying for the same exam share a common goal that naturally facilitates connection. If you are pursuing CCNA, CCNP, CCIE, or DevNet certifications, seek out study groups and certification meetups. The bonds formed during shared exam preparation are among the most durable professional relationships in the IT industry.

CCIE Networking

CCIE holders form a tight-knit community within the broader Cisco ecosystem. If you hold a CCIE or are pursuing one, attend the CCIE-specific events and meetups at Cisco Live. The CCIE community is small enough that personal introductions carry significant weight, and the shared experience of the expert-level certification creates instant credibility and trust.

Logistics and Budget Planning

Venue and Accommodation

Cisco Live 2026 takes place at the Mandalay Bay Convention Center in Las Vegas. Booking a room at Mandalay Bay through Cisco's official hotel block is the best networking play -- you will encounter fellow attendees in lobbies, restaurants, and common areas throughout the week. If Mandalay Bay is sold out, the adjacent Delano, Luxor, or other south Strip hotels offer convenient access via the free Mandalay Bay tram.

Budget Considerations

A Full Conference Pass represents a significant investment, but the combination of technical education, hands-on labs, certification testing, and networking opportunities makes it one of the highest-ROI events in the IT calendar. Factor in hotel (5 nights), flights, meals, and transportation. Many employers cover Cisco Live as a professional development expense -- prepare a business case that quantifies the networking, certification, and educational value.

Final Thoughts

Cisco Live 2026 is more than a conference. It is the annual gathering point for the global IT community, a place where network engineers meet security architects, where developers connect with infrastructure specialists, and where the technologies that will shape the next decade of enterprise IT are demonstrated, tested, and debated. The professionals who extract the most value from Cisco Live are not the ones who attend every session and collect the most swag. They are the ones who arrive with a networking plan built around labs, meetups, and strategic session selection, execute that plan with technical curiosity and genuine engagement, and follow up with the kind of specific, technical attention that transforms a conference handshake into a lasting professional relationship.

Start your preparation now. Register for your pass, book your labs, schedule your certification exams, and download the Cisco Live app. The connections you make at Mandalay Bay this June could define the next chapter of your IT career.
