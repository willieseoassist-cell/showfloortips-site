How to Network at InfoComm 2026: The Ultimate Guide to the World's Premier Pro AV Trade Show

17 min read February 11, 2026 ShowFloorTips Research Team

InfoComm 2026 is shaping up to be the biggest professional audiovisual event of the year. Running June 13 through 19 at the Las Vegas Convention Center, this AVIXA-organized powerhouse brings together more than 1,000 exhibitors and tens of thousands of Pro AV professionals from around the globe. Whether you are an integrator scouting new solutions, a manufacturer launching products, or a consultant expanding your client base, the connections you make at InfoComm can reshape the trajectory of your business for years to come.

But here is the reality most attendees do not talk about: walking into the LVCC without a networking plan is like stepping onto a show floor with no map. You will burn time, miss key contacts, and leave Las Vegas wondering what could have been. This guide is designed to prevent that. We have assembled a week-long, hour-by-hour networking strategy built around the actual InfoComm 2026 schedule, registration tiers, and venue layout so you can extract maximum value from every single day.

Step 1: Choose the Right Pass for Your Networking Goals

Your networking ceiling at InfoComm 2026 is determined before you even set foot in Las Vegas. The pass you select dictates which rooms you can enter, which sessions you can attend, and which exclusive events appear on your schedule. Here is how each tier maps to networking opportunity.

Premier Pass ($999 AVIXA Members / $1,249 Non-Members)

This is the all-access tier and the one we recommend for anyone who is serious about high-value networking. The Premier Pass grants entry to every conference session from June 13 through 19, all keynote presentations, the full exhibition floor, and exclusive networking receptions that are not open to lower tiers. You will find yourself in smaller rooms with decision-makers during the early conference days, which is where the most meaningful relationships are forged. If your goal is to meet CTOs, directors of AV operations at enterprise organizations, or senior leaders at major integrators, this is the pass that puts you in those rooms.

Essentials Pass ($699 Members / $899 Non-Members)

The Essentials Pass covers core education programming and exhibition access. It is a strong choice for mid-career professionals who want a curated learning experience paired with floor-time networking. You will still have access to many of the conference sessions and the full exhibition floor during show days, but you may miss some of the premium receptions and VIP events reserved for Premier badge holders.

Exhibit Hall Pass (Free for AVIXA Members / $399 Non-Members)

If you are primarily focused on booth-to-booth networking and product discovery, the Exhibit Hall Pass covers the June 17-19 exhibition days. This is the most budget-friendly route, and for AVIXA members it costs nothing. Pair this with strategic after-hours networking and you can still build a powerful contact list, but you will miss the intimate conference-session networking that happens during the first four days.

Pro Tip: The Add-On Strategy

Even with a base Exhibit Hall Pass, you can bolt on high-networking-value experiences. Pro Training Sessions ($99 members / $149 non-members) place you in focused workshops with fellow specialists. The Pro Summit ($699 / $899) offers deep-dive content with senior professionals. And the new AVIXA AI Accelerator ($399 / $499) puts you in a room with the people defining the future of AI in Pro AV. Each add-on is a networking multiplier.

Step 2: Prepare Your Networking Arsenal

The weeks before InfoComm 2026 are when the real networking work begins. Here is your pre-show preparation checklist.

Update Your Digital Presence

Your LinkedIn profile is the first thing a new contact will check after meeting you on the show floor. Before June, update your headline to reflect your current role and Pro AV specialization. Add a professional headshot if you do not already have one. Write a brief summary that answers the question every trade show contact silently asks: "What can this person do for me or my organization?"

Prepare Physical and Digital Business Cards

Yes, physical business cards still matter at trade shows. But upgrade them for 2026: include a QR code that links to your LinkedIn profile, portfolio, or a custom landing page. Carry at least 200 cards. You will go through more than you expect, especially during exhibition days when you are moving between dozens of booths.

Craft Your Elevator Pitch

You will introduce yourself hundreds of times over the course of InfoComm week. Prepare a crisp, 15-second introduction that covers three things: who you are, what you specialize in (be specific within Pro AV, whether that is unified communications, digital signage, immersive projection, audio engineering, or XR), and what kind of connections or solutions you are looking for. Practice it until it feels natural, not rehearsed.

Download the InfoComm App

The official InfoComm app features an interactive map of the Las Vegas Convention Center that is essential for navigating the exhibition floor. Use it to bookmark exhibitor booths, plan your route, and check session schedules in real time. The app also lets you see which attendees are at the show and can facilitate in-app messaging, a feature that is underutilized but incredibly valuable for setting up meetings on the fly.

Essential Pre-Show Actions

  • Join the AVIXA community forums and introduce yourself in InfoComm 2026 threads
  • Follow the #InfoComm2026 hashtag on LinkedIn and X (Twitter) and start engaging
  • Reach out to 20-30 target contacts via LinkedIn with personalized connection requests mentioning the show
  • Schedule at least 10 confirmed meetings before you arrive in Las Vegas
  • Prepare a one-page leave-behind document that summarizes your company's capabilities

Step 3: Research Exhibitors and Target Contacts

InfoComm 2026 features over 1,000 exhibitors spanning every segment of the professional audiovisual industry: audio, video, lighting, projection, digital signage, unified communications, collaboration platforms, streaming infrastructure, and extended reality (XR). Attempting to visit every booth is neither realistic nor strategic. Here is how to build a focused target list.

Categorize by Priority

Create three tiers of exhibitors. Tier 1 (Must Visit) includes companies you are actively evaluating for partnerships, procurement, or integration projects. Tier 2 (Should Visit) covers emerging players and competitors whose offerings you need to understand. Tier 3 (If Time Permits) includes booths that are interesting but not directly tied to your current business objectives. Allocate 60% of your floor time to Tier 1, 30% to Tier 2, and leave 10% for serendipitous discovery.

Identify Decision-Makers

Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator or the exhibitor directory on the InfoComm website to identify the specific people you want to meet at each target booth. Do not just show up and hope to bump into the VP of Sales. Email or message them in advance, reference a specific product or initiative, and propose a 15-minute meeting at their booth during a specific time slot. Booth staff are far more receptive to pre-scheduled meetings than cold walk-ups.

Map the Floor Zones

The LVCC is enormous. Use the InfoComm app's interactive map to group your target booths by physical location. Plan your route so that you are moving efficiently through adjacent aisles rather than crisscrossing the hall. Mark the locations of Audio Demo Rooms and Activation Hubs as well, as these are prime networking environments where attendees linger and are more open to conversation.

Step 4: Conference Days Strategy (June 13-16)

The first four days of InfoComm 2026 are conference-only, meaning the exhibition floor is not yet open. Many attendees skip these days entirely, which is precisely why they represent the highest-quality networking window of the entire week. Session attendance is smaller, the pace is more relaxed, and the people who invest in conference passes tend to be senior professionals and industry leaders.

Session Selection for Networking

Choose sessions based not only on content interest but also on the type of people who are likely to attend. A session on enterprise AV-over-IP deployment will attract a different crowd than a panel on experiential retail digital signage. Think about your target contacts and select sessions that align with their professional interests. Arrive five minutes early, sit near the middle of the room (not the back), and be prepared to introduce yourself to seatmates.

The Post-Session Window

The most valuable networking at any conference session happens in the five minutes immediately after it ends. Speakers are approached by a small cluster of attendees, and the hallway outside the session room becomes an impromptu networking zone. Have your questions and comments ready. Approach the speaker with a specific reference to something they said and ask a thoughtful follow-up question. This positions you as someone worth talking to, not just another badge in the crowd.

Women's Breakfast and Special Events

InfoComm 2026 hosts a Women's Breakfast ($89 AVIXA members / $99 non-members) that is one of the most effective networking events of the entire week. The format encourages table-based conversation, and the attendees are typically accomplished professionals who are actively looking to expand their networks. Even if you are not attending, be aware of the event's timing so you can connect with attendees before or after.

Step 5: Exhibition Floor Domination (June 17-19)

The exhibition opens on June 17, and the energy shifts dramatically. The LVCC will be filled with thousands of professionals, and the pace will be intense. Here is how to work the floor like a seasoned networker.

The First-Hour Advantage

Arrive within the first hour of floor opening on June 17. Booth staff are fresh, lines are shorter, and exhibitors are eager to engage. This is when you will have your best conversations with Tier 1 targets. By midday, the floor is packed, attention spans are shorter, and booth staff are managing dozens of simultaneous conversations.

Audio Demo Rooms and Activation Hubs

These are InfoComm's secret networking weapons. Audio Demo Rooms provide controlled listening environments where attendees gather for extended product demonstrations. The shared experience of hearing a system together creates a natural conversation starter. Activation Hubs are hands-on interaction zones where you can engage with technology alongside other attendees. Both environments encourage the kind of organic, extended interaction that leads to genuine professional relationships rather than transactional card exchanges.

Keynote Sessions as Networking Anchors

Keynote presentations draw large crowds and create natural gathering points before and after. Use keynotes strategically: arrive early and network in the queue, then linger afterward to discuss the presentation with fellow attendees. The shared experience provides an instant conversation topic. Reference a specific insight from the keynote when meeting someone new, and you will immediately have common ground.

Show Floor Tours

InfoComm offers guided Show Floor Tours ($99 members / $149 non-members) that walk small groups through curated selections of exhibitor booths. These tours are networking gold. You are placed in a group of 15-20 professionals who share your specific area of interest, and you spend an extended period moving through the floor together. By the end of the tour, you have built a natural rapport with your fellow participants that would have taken hours to develop through random floor encounters.

Pro Tip: The Booth Staff Approach

When approaching an exhibitor booth, do not lead with "What does your company do?" Booth staff hear this hundreds of times per day. Instead, lead with context: "I saw your announcement about [specific product] and I work with [type of client/project] where this could be a fit. Can you walk me through the integration workflow?" This signals that you are a qualified lead, not a casual browser, and will earn you a higher-quality conversation with a more senior team member.

Step 6: The Follow-Up System That Actually Works

According to industry research, 80% of trade show leads never receive a follow-up. This means that simply following up at all puts you ahead of the vast majority of InfoComm attendees. But timing and personalization matter enormously.

The 48-Hour Rule

Every contact you make at InfoComm should receive a personalized follow-up within 48 hours of your last interaction. Not a template. Not a bulk email. A specific message that references your conversation, mentions something you discussed, and proposes a clear next step. If you met someone at the Crestron booth and discussed AV-over-IP for their campus deployment, your follow-up should reference that exact conversation and offer to send a relevant case study or schedule a technical deep-dive call.

Organize Contacts by Priority

At the end of each show day, spend 20 minutes sorting your new contacts into three categories: Hot (clear mutual interest, specific next step identified), Warm (good conversation, potential future relevance), and Cool (pleasant exchange, no immediate opportunity). Your Hot contacts get follow-ups within 24 hours. Warm contacts get follow-ups within 48 hours. Cool contacts get a LinkedIn connection request with a brief personalized note within one week.

LinkedIn as Your Follow-Up Engine

Every business card you collect should translate into a LinkedIn connection request before you leave Las Vegas. When sending the request, always include a note: "Great meeting you at InfoComm at the [Company] booth. I enjoyed our conversation about [topic]. Let's stay connected." This transforms a transient trade show encounter into a persistent professional relationship.

Day-by-Day Networking Blueprint

Day 1 - Friday, June 13

Conference Opens: Set the Foundation

Morning: Attend your first conference sessions. Focus on workshops and smaller-format events where interaction is encouraged. Introduce yourself to at least five new people before lunch.

Afternoon: Attend Pro Summit or CTS certification prep sessions if applicable. These attract dedicated professionals who are invested in their careers, making them high-quality networking targets.

Evening: Explore the area around the LVCC. Many attendees gather at nearby restaurants and hotel lobbies. Join any unofficial meetups posted on social media under #InfoComm2026.

Day 2 - Saturday, June 14

Deep Dive: Build Depth Over Breadth

Morning: Attend the AVIXA AI Accelerator if you added it to your registration. This newer event is drawing innovators and early adopters who are defining the intersection of artificial intelligence and professional AV. The networking value is exceptional.

Afternoon: Continue conference sessions. By day two, you will start seeing familiar faces in hallways and session rooms. Approach people you recognized from yesterday and deepen those connections.

Evening: Host or attend a small-group dinner. Invite three to five people you have met during the first two days. A shared meal creates far stronger bonds than any amount of hallway conversation.

Day 3 - Sunday, June 15

Strategic Sessions and Relationship Building

Morning: Attend the Women's Breakfast if registered. Otherwise, use the morning for targeted one-on-one meetings with contacts you have been cultivating.

Afternoon: Focus on sessions that align with your Tier 1 exhibitor categories. Take notes on presenters and panelists you want to approach during exhibition days.

Evening: Review your schedule for exhibition days. Confirm pre-booked booth meetings. Charge your devices. Rest well; the exhibition floor demands physical endurance.

Day 4 - Monday, June 16

Final Conference Day: Bridge to the Exhibition

Morning: Attend your remaining must-see sessions. Exchange contact information with any session acquaintances you have not yet connected with.

Afternoon: Use any free time to finalize your exhibition floor plan using the InfoComm app's interactive map. Group Tier 1 booths by location.

Evening: Early rest. Tomorrow the exhibition opens, and you want to arrive at the LVCC with full energy.

Day 5 - Tuesday, June 17

Exhibition Opens: Maximum Impact

Morning: Arrive at the LVCC within the first 30 minutes of floor opening. Head directly to your highest-priority Tier 1 booths. Focus on pre-scheduled meetings.

Afternoon: Visit Audio Demo Rooms and Activation Hubs. Join a Show Floor Tour if registered. Attend the opening keynote and network in the queue.

Evening: Attend any exhibitor-hosted receptions or after-parties. These evening events often yield the most candid and productive conversations of the entire show.

Day 6 - Wednesday, June 18

Exhibition Day 2: Go Wide

Morning: Complete your Tier 1 visits. Begin working through Tier 2 exhibitors. Focus on emerging companies and startups that may be offering innovative solutions.

Afternoon: Revisit any Tier 1 booths where you want to deepen a conversation or meet a different team member. Attend afternoon keynotes and sessions.

Evening: Spend 30 minutes organizing the day's contacts. Send immediate follow-ups to your hottest new connections. Attend evening networking events.

Day 7 - Thursday, June 19

Final Day: Close Strong

Morning: Hit any remaining Tier 2 and Tier 3 booths. Revisit favorite exhibitors for final questions. Collect any remaining materials or product samples.

Afternoon: The floor thins out in the afternoon. Use this quieter period for longer, more substantive conversations at booths that were too busy earlier in the week.

Evening: Begin your 48-hour follow-up process. Send LinkedIn connections. Organize your notes and business cards while conversations are still fresh.

Advanced Networking Tactics

The Hallway Strategy

Some of the best networking at InfoComm happens not in sessions or on the show floor, but in the hallways, coffee areas, and charging stations of the LVCC. These are the zones where people are relaxed, approachable, and not in "booth mode." Position yourself in high-traffic hallway areas during session breaks. Stand near the coffee service. Sit at communal tables in the food court. These casual environments produce conversations that feel natural rather than transactional.

Social Media as a Live Networking Tool

Post consistently on LinkedIn and X throughout the show. Share insights from sessions you attend, photos from the exhibition floor, and commentary on trends you are observing. Tag exhibitors and speakers when relevant. This serves two purposes: it signals your presence at the show to your existing network (prompting inbound meeting requests), and it gives you a reason to approach new contacts ("I saw your post about the Shure booth -- what did you think of their new microphone array?").

The Introduction Economy

The most powerful networking move at any trade show is not meeting someone new; it is introducing two people to each other. When you connect two contacts who can help each other, you create goodwill with both parties and position yourself as a connector rather than a collector. Keep a mental list of what people are looking for, and when you meet someone who fits, make the introduction on the spot.

CTS Certification as a Networking Badge

If you are pursuing AVIXA's CTS certification (prep courses range from $2,749 to $3,499), the certification prep environment is an outstanding networking setting. You are spending multiple days in intensive study with fellow professionals who are investing significant time and money in their careers. The bonds formed during certification prep often last for years.

Logistics, Hotels, and Getting Around

Accommodation Strategy

InfoComm's official housing partner is onPeak, and it is the only endorsed accommodation provider for the show. Booking through onPeak ensures you receive negotiated rates at partner hotels and, critically, access to the complimentary shuttle service that runs between the LVCC and select partner hotels. This shuttle is not just a convenience; it is a networking venue. Some of the best conversations happen on the morning ride to the convention center when attendees are caffeinated, energized, and not yet overwhelmed by the show floor.

Navigating the LVCC

The Las Vegas Convention Center is massive. Wear comfortable shoes rated for all-day walking. Use the InfoComm app's interactive map to plan your daily routes. Identify the locations of restrooms, charging stations, water fountains, and food courts so you can take efficient breaks without losing momentum. The underground Tesla Loop tunnel connects the convention center's halls and can save significant walking time.

Timing Your Arrival

Fly into Las Vegas no later than the evening of June 12 if you have a Premier or Essentials Pass. This gives you time to settle in, review your plan, and attend any pre-show networking events happening the night before the conference opens. If you have an Exhibit Hall Pass only, arrive by the evening of June 16 so you are rested and ready for the floor opening on June 17.

Final Thoughts

InfoComm 2026 is more than a trade show. It is the annual gathering point for the global Pro AV community, a place where careers are accelerated, partnerships are launched, and the future of audiovisual technology is revealed. The professionals who extract the most value from this event are not the ones who wander the floor and collect the most swag. They are the ones who arrive with a plan, execute that plan with discipline and genuine curiosity, and follow up with the kind of personalized attention that transforms a trade show handshake into a lasting professional relationship.

Start your preparation now. Register for your pass, build your target list, update your LinkedIn profile, and download the InfoComm app the moment it is available. The connections you make at the Las Vegas Convention Center this June could define the next chapter of your Pro AV career.
