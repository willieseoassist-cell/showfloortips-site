Cannabis & Hemp

Cannabis Rescheduling Meets Hemp Crackdown — How 280E Tax Relief and the November Deadline Will Reshape MJBizCon 2026

ShowFloorTips Editorial · February 11, 2026

The cannabis industry enters 2026 in the midst of two seismic regulatory shifts happening simultaneously. President Trump's December 18 executive order directing the Attorney General to reschedule marijuana from Schedule I to Schedule III promises the most significant tax relief in the industry's history — the end of IRC Section 280E, which has prevented cannabis businesses from deducting ordinary business expenses for years. At the same time, a federal spending bill signed in late 2025 narrowed the definition of hemp, creating a November 2026 deadline that will make nearly all intoxicating hemp-derived products illegal under federal law. With MJBizCon returning to Las Vegas December 1–4, NECANN shows running throughout the year, and the Cannabis Conference on the calendar, exhibitors face an industry that is simultaneously expanding and contracting — depending on which side of the regulatory line you stand.

Schedule III and the End of 280E: A Financial Revolution

For plant-touching cannabis businesses, the move from Schedule I to Schedule III is not about legalization — recreational marijuana remains federally illegal. It is about money. Section 280E of the Internal Revenue Code currently prevents cannabis businesses from deducting expenses that every other business takes for granted: payroll and benefits, rent and facility costs, marketing and advertising, professional fees for legal and accounting services, and general overhead. The financial burden has been crushing, with effective tax rates for cannabis companies often exceeding 70 percent.

If the DOJ finalizes the rescheduling rule — expected in the first half of 2026 — Section 280E would no longer apply to cannabis. The financial impact would be transformative: businesses that have been operating at razor-thin margins or losses could immediately become profitable. Tax planning strategies, deferred expenditures, and capital investment decisions made under the 280E regime would need to be revisited.

However, tax experts caution that relief from 280E, if it comes, will likely not be retroactive. Companies that paid inflated taxes under 280E should not expect refunds for prior years. The forward-looking benefit is enormous, but the backward-looking reality is that years of excess tax burden are not recoverable.

Key InsightFor cannabis industry exhibitors, the rescheduling creates new market opportunities. Companies selling accounting software, tax advisory services, financial planning tools, and business management platforms can position their products around the post-280E operational reality. This is a once-in-a-generation pivot point for ancillary cannabis businesses.

The November 2026 Hemp Deadline

While rescheduling benefits the regulated cannabis market, the hemp-derived product sector faces an existential threat. Congress narrowed the federal definition of hemp in the spending bill signed in late 2025, with an effective date in November 2026. When that deadline arrives, nearly all of the products keeping the $28.3 billion U.S. hemp sector afloat — Delta-8 THC, THCA products, and other intoxicating hemp derivatives — become illegal under federal law.

Hemp operators are required to begin winding down these product lines well before the November deadline. For exhibitors in the hemp-derived product space, 2026 is a transition year: either pivot to compliant product categories (CBD, non-intoxicating hemp products, industrial applications) or prepare to exit the market. The trade shows throughout 2026 will be the primary venues where these transition strategies are discussed, partnerships are formed, and new business models are validated.

Industry Consolidation Continues

The number of active cannabis business licenses in the United States declined to 37,555 in the most recent quarter, down approximately 1 percent from the previous quarter and extending a downturn that has persisted since late 2022. Over the past two years, the total number of active licenses has fallen 13 percent. This consolidation reflects a market that is maturing — weaker operators are exiting while stronger companies are positioning for the post-rescheduling landscape.

The 2026 Cannabis Trade Show Calendar

  • NECANN (Multiple dates, multiple cities): Regional cannabis conferences and expos held across the Northeast and expanding nationally. NECANN events serve state-level operators, cultivators, and ancillary businesses. Best for exhibitors targeting specific state markets or building regional distribution networks.
  • Cannabis Conference: Focused on education, compliance, and business operations. The audience includes regulators, compliance officers, and cannabis business operators seeking operational guidance. Ideal for exhibitors selling compliance technology, training platforms, and consulting services.
  • MJBizCon (December 1–4, Las Vegas): The industry's flagship B2B event, attracting approximately 30,000 professionals. MJBizCon is where the largest deals happen, the biggest brands exhibit, and the industry's direction for the coming year is set. The Las Vegas Convention Center provides the scale needed for the industry's most comprehensive exhibition.

Exhibitor Strategies for Cannabis Trade Shows in 2026

  1. Frame your offering around the post-280E reality. Whether you sell cultivation equipment, retail software, or professional services, show buyers how your product's value proposition changes when normal business deductions become available. Build ROI models that reflect the new financial landscape.
  2. Address the hemp transition directly. If you serve both cannabis and hemp markets, have clear messaging about how your products and services adapt to the November 2026 hemp deadline. If you serve only the regulated cannabis market, position yourself as the compliant alternative for hemp operators making the transition.
  3. Prepare for the safe banking conversation. The industry's other major regulatory watch item is safe banking access. If clarity arrives before your trade show, be ready to discuss how your financial products, payment processing solutions, or banking partnerships serve the newly banked cannabis industry.
  4. Target the surviving operators. With active licenses down 13 percent, the companies still operating are the industry's survivors — better capitalized, more operationally disciplined, and more likely to invest in quality technology. Adjust your lead qualification criteria to focus on operators with multi-state footprints, established brands, or recent capital raises.

The cannabis industry in 2026 is experiencing the most consequential regulatory year in its history. Rescheduling promises financial liberation for plant-touching businesses. The hemp crackdown is forcing an entire product category to reinvent itself. And industry consolidation is concentrating market power among the strongest operators. For exhibitors who understand these dynamics and prepare accordingly, the 2026 trade show circuit — from NECANN regionals to the MJBizCon main stage — offers extraordinary opportunities to capture a market in transformation.
