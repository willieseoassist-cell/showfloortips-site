Setup and Installation Checklist for DesignCon

ShowFloorTips · February 04, 2026

In an era dominated by digital marketing, face-to-face events continue to deliver unmatched ROI. The setup and installation phase at DesignCon sets the stage for your entire exhibition experience. This checklist ensures your booth at in Santa Clara is ready to impress visitors from the moment the show floor opens.

Arrival and Check-In

  • Arrive at during the designated exhibitor setup window (check your exhibitor manual for exact times).
  • Check in at the exhibitor registration desk and collect badges, parking passes, and any pre-ordered materials.
  • Locate your booth space and verify the dimensions match your floor plan.
  • Confirm that pre-ordered services are in place: electrical outlets, internet connections, carpet, and furniture.
  • Check for any damage to freight shipments and document issues immediately with the freight carrier and show management.
6hrs
average setup time for a 10x10 booth

Booth Assembly

  • Unpack and inventory all booth components, checking against your master packing list.
  • Begin structural assembly according to your setup instructions, starting with the frame and working outward.
  • Install graphics, signage, and branding elements, ensuring proper alignment and professional appearance.
  • Position furniture, product displays, and demonstration stations according to your floor plan.
  • Route all cables and wiring cleanly -- use cable covers and zip ties to prevent tripping hazards.
  • Install lighting and adjust positions to highlight key products and create visual hierarchy.
  • Set up any audio-visual equipment: monitors, speakers, and presentation systems.

Technology Setup

  • Connect to venue Wi-Fi or set up your portable hotspot and verify connection stability.
  • Test all lead capture devices, badge scanners, and CRM connectivity.
  • Load and test all presentations, product demos, and interactive displays.
  • Configure any touchscreen kiosks or interactive experiences for visitor use.
  • Test all power connections and ensure backup charging solutions are accessible.
  • Run a complete demo cycle of all technology to identify any issues before the show opens.

Final Inspection

  • Walk through the booth from an attendee's perspective -- is the key message visible and clear from 15 feet away?
  • Stock marketing materials, business cards, and giveaway items in accessible but tidy locations.
  • Ensure all products and demonstration units are clean, functional, and presentation-ready.
  • Take photos of the completed booth setup for documentation and social media content.
  • Conduct a final team briefing at the booth, reviewing positions, responsibilities, and day-one objectives.
  • Confirm that cleaning supplies and personal comfort items (water, snacks, mints) are stocked and accessible.
  • Set up the booth security plan for overnight periods during multi-day shows.
  • Walk the surrounding aisles to see your booth from various approaches and angles.
