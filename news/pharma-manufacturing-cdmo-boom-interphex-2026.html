The GLP-1 Gold Rush Is Reshaping Pharma Manufacturing — What to Watch at INTERPHEX 2026

ShowFloorTips Editorial · February 11, 2026

The pharmaceutical contract development and manufacturing organization (CDMO) market is on an extraordinary trajectory. Valued at $258.88 billion in 2025, the sector is forecast to reach $374.68 billion by 2031, expanding at a 6.33% CAGR according to a February 2026 report from GlobeNewsWire. Driving this surge is an unprecedented wave of demand for GLP-1 receptor agonist therapies, biologics capacity, and AI-powered manufacturing platforms — themes that will dominate the show floor at INTERPHEX 2026 when it opens at New York's Javits Center on April 21.

The GLP-1 Catalyst: A $200 Billion Market in the Making

Some analysts estimate total GLP-1 sales will exceed $200 billion annually by 2031. Meeting that demand requires a manufacturing ramp-up that is already reshaping facility investment worldwide. CordenPharma has committed over 900 million euros to expand its GLP-1 peptide production capacity. Lonza's $1.2 billion acquisition of Roche's Vacaville site added 330,000 liters of biologics capacity almost overnight. In South Korea, SK pharmteco is deploying $260 million for a new facility slated to come online this year.

These are not incremental expansions. They represent a structural shift in how the pharmaceutical industry approaches capacity planning. For exhibitors at INTERPHEX, the implication is clear: anyone offering solutions that accelerate time-to-market for peptide, biologics, or high-potency API manufacturing will have an eager audience.

Consolidation Is Accelerating

Novo Holdings' $16.5 billion acquisition of Catalent signaled a decisive shift toward end-to-end providers that combine development, scale-up, and commercial production under one roof. The deal is the clearest indicator yet that pharma companies are no longer content to stitch together fragmented supply chains for their most critical products.

This consolidation wave has direct implications for exhibitors positioning themselves at INTERPHEX 2026. Smaller CDMOs and specialty service providers need to articulate where they fit in an ecosystem increasingly dominated by vertically integrated giants. Meanwhile, companies offering technologies that enable seamless tech transfer, real-time quality monitoring, and flexible manufacturing will be essential partners for organizations navigating these consolidation dynamics.

INTERPHEX Responds with the Contract & Outsourcing Exchange

Recognizing this trend, INTERPHEX 2026 is debuting the Contract & Outsourcing Exchange (COEX), a new show-within-a-show destination that brings together pharma and biotech leaders, CDMOs, and emerging companies navigating early development and manufacturing decisions. The COEX is designed to reflect the full outsourcing ecosystem, creating dedicated meeting spaces where companies can evaluate partners, negotiate terms, and build the relationships that underpin successful manufacturing partnerships.

AI-Driven Manufacturing Moves from Pilot to Production

The 2026 INTERPHEX education lineup will feature sessions on the effective adoption of AI-driven innovation across development and manufacturing. This reflects a broader industry transition: AI in pharma manufacturing is no longer a research project. It is a competitive requirement.

Predictive maintenance algorithms are reducing unplanned downtime by up to 30% at leading facilities. Machine learning models are optimizing batch processes in real time, cutting waste and improving yield consistency. Digital twins of manufacturing lines allow companies to simulate process changes before committing physical resources, dramatically reducing validation timelines.

"The companies that generate the highest return on their manufacturing technology investment in 2026 are those deploying AI not as a bolt-on, but as the foundation of their quality and process control systems."

-- Outsourced Pharma Industry Analysis

For exhibitors bringing AI-powered quality systems, process analytical technology, or predictive analytics platforms to INTERPHEX, the timing could not be better. Attendees are actively seeking solutions that deliver measurable improvements in yield, compliance, and speed to market.

Supply Chain Resilience Remains a Board-Level Priority

INTERPHEX 2026 education sessions will explore how pharmaceutical quality and resilience are strengthened through secure supply chains and proactive regulatory compliance. The pandemic exposed critical vulnerabilities in global API supply chains. Those lessons have not been forgotten.

Regional diversification is accelerating. Asia-Pacific is logging the fastest CDMO growth at 7.18% CAGR through 2031, with China and South Korea investing heavily in monoclonal antibody and oligonucleotide manufacturing capacity. INTERPHEX WEEK TOKYO, scheduled for May 20-22 at Makuhari Messe, is expected to draw 35,000 visitors and 950 exhibitors, underscoring the region's growing importance in the global manufacturing network.

Key TakeawayExhibitors showcasing supply chain visibility tools, dual-sourcing strategies, or regionalized manufacturing capabilities should position these solutions front and center at their INTERPHEX booths.

What Exhibitors Should Prioritize at INTERPHEX 2026

With over 600 suppliers and 140 speakers expected across three days, INTERPHEX offers a concentrated window to reach the decision-makers shaping pharma manufacturing's next chapter. Here is what successful exhibitors will focus on:

  • Demonstrate GLP-1 and peptide manufacturing capability. If your equipment, formulation technology, or fill-finish services support GLP-1 or HPAPI production, lead with this messaging. The demand signal is unmistakable.
  • Show measurable AI outcomes. Move beyond conceptual demonstrations. Bring case studies with specific metrics — percentage yield improvement, reduction in batch failures, time saved in deviation investigations.
  • Address the CDMO consolidation narrative. Whether you are a one-stop provider or a specialized niche player, your value proposition must clearly articulate how you fit into a rapidly consolidating landscape.
  • Leverage the COEX. The new Contract & Outsourcing Exchange is purpose-built for partnership conversations. Exhibitors should book dedicated meeting slots and prepare tailored presentations for the buyers and sponsors in attendance.
  • Invest in the Learning Lab. INTERPHEX's enclosed private education space returns in 2026, offering an immersive setting to deliver thought leadership content to qualified attendees.

The Bigger Picture: BIO and CPHI on the Horizon

INTERPHEX kicks off a critical stretch of pharma industry events. The BIO International Convention follows in June, offering a broader biotech lens that encompasses drug discovery, clinical development, and investor relations alongside manufacturing. CPHI Worldwide in October brings a global perspective on pharmaceutical ingredients and outsourcing, with particular relevance for companies pursuing international expansion.

For exhibitors planning their 2026 trade show calendar, the through-line across all three events is the same: the industry is investing at historic levels to build manufacturing capacity for the next generation of therapies. Companies that demonstrate tangible solutions for speed, quality, and supply security will be the ones generating pipeline from the show floor.

Exhibitor Action ItemStart your INTERPHEX outreach now. Pre-show meetings and scheduled demonstrations are filling fast, and the highest-value connections happen when both parties arrive prepared.

The pharma manufacturing sector is entering a period of investment and transformation that rivals any in its history. INTERPHEX 2026 arrives at exactly the right moment to bring this community together. The exhibitors who succeed will be those who match the urgency of the market with the specificity of their solutions.
