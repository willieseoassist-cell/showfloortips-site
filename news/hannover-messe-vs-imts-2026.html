Hannover Messe vs IMTS 2026: Which Should You Exhibit At?

If you manufacture industrial technology, the two most important trade shows on your calendar are on different continents and operate on different rhythms. Hannover Messe (April 20-24 in Hannover, Germany) runs every year and is the world's largest industrial technology fair. IMTS, the International Manufacturing Technology Show (September 14-19 at McCormick Place in Chicago), runs every two years and is the largest manufacturing trade show in the Western Hemisphere. In 2026, both events take place, creating a rare opportunity, and a significant budgeting question, for companies that serve the global manufacturing sector.

This comparison will help you decide where to invest your trade show dollars, or how to justify investing in both.

Audience Comparison

Hannover Messe

Hannover Messe draws over 130,000 attendees from more than 70 countries, with approximately 4,000 exhibitors. The audience spans a broad industrial spectrum: factory automation, process automation, energy technology, digital ecosystems, compressed air and vacuum technology, and industrial supply. Attendees include manufacturing executives, plant engineers, procurement officers, automation specialists, energy managers, and government trade officials. The international mix is the defining characteristic. European companies dominate the exhibitor base, but significant delegations attend from Asia, North America, the Middle East, and Africa. Approximately 40 percent of attendees come from outside Germany.

If you are building or expanding an international sales network, Hannover Messe provides unmatched global reach in a single venue.

IMTS

IMTS attracts over 86,000 registrants and more than 1,800 exhibitors, making it the largest manufacturing technology show in the Americas. The audience is more narrowly focused on metalworking and manufacturing technology: CNC machine tools, cutting tools, tooling systems, additive manufacturing, metrology, automation, and controls. Attendees are predominantly North American manufacturing professionals, including plant managers, production engineers, machining specialists, quality managers, and purchasing agents. While IMTS draws some international attendance, particularly from Mexico, Canada, and Asia, the show is fundamentally a North American event.

If your primary market is North American manufacturing, and particularly if your products relate to metal cutting, forming, or precision machining, IMTS delivers the most concentrated buyer audience available.

Show Size and Scope

  • Hannover Messe: 130,000+ attendees, 4,000 exhibitors, spread across 27 halls at the Hannover Exhibition Grounds, one of the world's largest convention campuses. The scope is enormously broad, covering factory automation, energy solutions, digital transformation, logistics, compressed air, surface treatment, and research. This breadth is both a strength and a challenge: your booth competes for attention across a massive venue, but the diversity of attendees means unexpected cross-industry connections are common.
  • IMTS: 86,000+ registrants, 1,800+ exhibitors, held at McCormick Place in Chicago across 1.4 million square feet of exhibit space. IMTS is more tightly focused on manufacturing technology, and the show floor is organized by technology type: metal cutting, metal forming, controls and CAD/CAM, tooling, abrasive machining, additive manufacturing, and quality assurance. This focus means that a higher percentage of people walking past your booth are likely to be relevant prospects.

Location and Logistics

Hannover Messe: Hannover, Germany

Hannover is a mid-sized German city in Lower Saxony with efficient public transit connecting the city center to the fairgrounds. The U-Bahn ride takes approximately 20 minutes. Hotels in Hannover fill up weeks before the show and rates spike to two to three times their normal levels. Some exhibitors stay in Hamburg or Hanover's suburbs and commute by train. International exhibitors face transatlantic shipping for booth materials, customs clearance, and potential language barriers. The venue itself is superbly organized, and the German exhibition infrastructure, including customs brokers, booth builders, and logistics companies, is world-class.

IMTS: Chicago, Illinois

McCormick Place is located on Chicago's lakefront, accessible via public transit, taxi, and rideshare. Chicago offers enormous hotel inventory across every price point, with the McCormick Place hotel campus and nearby South Loop properties providing the most convenient options. Domestic shipping to McCormick Place is straightforward, with major freight and drayage providers serving the venue routinely. The surrounding area offers strong dining and entertainment options, though the convention center itself is somewhat isolated from Chicago's downtown restaurant scene. For North American exhibitors, the logistics are considerably simpler and cheaper than Hannover.

Exhibitor Costs

  • Hannover Messe: Booth space is priced per square meter, and the costs are substantial. A standard presence in a well-trafficked hall runs in the mid-five-figure to low-six-figure range for space alone. When you add booth construction (German exhibition standards are high, and elaborate custom builds are the norm), international shipping, staff travel, hotels at peak-week rates, and client entertainment, a mid-sized exhibitor should budget $150,000 to $350,000 for a complete Hannover Messe presence. The German tradition of elaborate, architecturally designed booths means that exhibitors who bring a simple pop-up display may appear underinvested relative to their neighbors.
  • IMTS: Booth space at IMTS is priced per square foot and varies by hall and location. A standard 20x20 booth in a good location typically runs in the $30,000 to $60,000 range for space. Total exhibitor costs, including booth construction, equipment shipping (many exhibitors display heavy machinery), staffing, travel, hotels, and entertainment, range from $80,000 to $250,000 for a mid-sized presence. Shipping and installing heavy machine tools adds significant cost, but the logistics infrastructure at McCormick Place is well-equipped for oversized freight.

ROI Potential

Hannover Messe ROI Profile

Hannover Messe delivers its highest ROI for companies seeking international distribution partnerships, OEM relationships, and cross-border sales. The global attendee mix means you can meet potential partners from 70 countries in a single week. European manufacturing procurement cycles tend to be longer and more relationship-driven than American ones, so the ROI timeline extends accordingly. However, the average contract value for industrial equipment and automation systems sold through European channels is often higher than domestic US equivalents, particularly for companies that successfully establish agent or distributor relationships. The presence of government trade delegations also creates opportunities for market entry support and funding.

IMTS ROI Profile

IMTS delivers its highest ROI for companies selling directly to North American manufacturers. The audience is tightly focused on buying or evaluating manufacturing technology, and many attendees arrive with active projects and allocated budgets. IMTS is known as a "buying show" where purchase orders are placed on the floor, particularly for machine tools, cutting tools, and automation equipment. The biennial schedule creates urgency: buyers know they will not have this opportunity again for two years, which compresses decision timelines. Many exhibitors report that their IMTS leads convert at a higher rate and shorter cycle than any other show on their calendar.

Timing and Frequency

This is a critical strategic difference. Hannover Messe runs every year, giving exhibitors an annual touchpoint with their international audience. IMTS runs every two years, alternating with other manufacturing events. In 2026, both shows occur, which means exhibitors can use Hannover Messe in April to build relationships with European prospects and IMTS in September to close deals with North American buyers, effectively covering both hemispheres in a single year.

In odd-numbered years when IMTS does not run, Hannover Messe becomes even more important as the only world-class industrial show on the calendar. Companies with constrained budgets in non-IMTS years should prioritize Hannover Messe for global reach or consider smaller North American events to maintain domestic visibility.

Which Show Should You Choose?

  1. Choose Hannover Messe if your growth strategy is international. If you are entering European, Middle Eastern, or Asian markets, or if you need to build distributor and agent networks outside North America, Hannover Messe is irreplaceable. It is also the stronger show for companies focused on Industry 4.0 digital platforms, energy technology, and hydrogen, topics where European buyers and policymakers are often ahead of their US counterparts in adoption.
  2. Choose IMTS if your primary market is North America and your products are related to metalworking, machining, and manufacturing technology. If your buyers are US and Canadian plant managers and production engineers, IMTS delivers the highest-quality, most purchase-ready audience available. The "buying show" reputation is well-earned, and the biennial urgency works in your favor.
  3. Choose both if you are a global industrial technology company with the budget and organizational capacity to execute two major trade shows in a single year. The April-to-September cadence is actually well-suited for campaign planning: use Hannover Messe to launch products and build European relationships in the spring, then leverage that momentum at IMTS in the fall for North American sales. Companies that exhibit at both consistently report that each show amplifies the other, as global customers see consistent presence and commitment.

Manufacturing is a global industry, and the choice between Hannover Messe and IMTS ultimately reflects where your customers are and where you want to grow. Both shows are best-in-class for their respective audiences. In 2026, with both events on the calendar, the companies that invest in the right show, or both, will have a decisive advantage over those that sit on the sidelines.
