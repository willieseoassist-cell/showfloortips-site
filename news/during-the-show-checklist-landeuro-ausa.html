During the Show Checklist for LANDEURO - AUSA

ShowFloorTips · January 24, 2026

Smart exhibitors treat trade shows as a complete marketing campaign, not a one-off event. The show floor at LANDEURO - AUSA is where preparation meets execution. This daily checklist keeps your team focused, energized, and productive throughout the event at in Wiesbaden.

Daily Opening Routine

  • Arrive at your booth 30-45 minutes before the show floor opens.
  • Power on all technology and verify that lead capture devices, displays, and demos are functioning.
  • Restock marketing materials, business cards, and giveaway items.
  • Quick-clean the booth: wipe surfaces, straighten displays, and remove any debris.
  • Hold a 10-minute team huddle to review the day's objectives, scheduled meetings, and talking points.
  • Review leads captured the previous day and share any insights or adjustments to the approach.
  • Assign booth positions and break schedules for the day.

Throughout the Day

  • Maintain at least two team members in the booth at all times during show hours.
  • Actively engage visitors who pause or show interest -- do not wait for them to approach.
  • Qualify leads immediately using your pre-established criteria and tag them appropriately in your lead capture system.
  • Add context notes to every lead while the conversation is fresh -- key interests, pain points, next steps, and urgency level.
  • Rotate booth staff every 2-3 hours to maintain energy and prevent fatigue.
  • Monitor and respond to social media mentions, event hashtags, and direct messages throughout the day.
  • Post booth updates, photos, and content to your social media channels during the show.
  • Track booth traffic patterns and adjust team positioning during peak and slow periods.

Meetings and Networking

  • Confirm scheduled meetings with a brief text or app message in the morning.
  • Prepare any materials or presentations specific to each scheduled meeting.
  • Attend at least one official event, reception, or networking session each day.
  • Walk the show floor during a break to visit competitors, potential partners, and industry peers.
  • Exchange contact information with all meaningful connections and note the context for follow-up.

End of Day

  • Review and back up all lead data captured during the day.
  • Send a quick summary email to your home team with lead count, key wins, and notable conversations.
  • Secure valuable items and lock any cabinets or storage within the booth.
  • Power down technology and plug in devices for overnight charging.
  • Debrief with the team over dinner: what worked, what did not, and what to adjust tomorrow.
  • Send connection requests or brief follow-up messages to the day's top contacts while recall is fresh.
