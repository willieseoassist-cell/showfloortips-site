MODEX 2026 vs CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026: Supply Chain Precision Meets Construction Power — Which Mega Show Is Right for You?

ShowFloorTips Research Team February 11, 2026 15 min read

Spring 2026 pits two of North America's most consequential trade shows against each other in a battle for your travel budget, attention, and deal-making energy. CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 storms into Las Vegas from March 3-7 with 2.9 million square feet of construction firepower, while MODEX 2026 fires back from Atlanta on April 13-16 with a laser-focused deep dive into supply chain innovation, warehouse automation, and logistics technology. Both are industry-defining events. Neither is skippable if your business intersects with their domains. But if you can only attend one, this guide will help you decide.

We have evaluated both shows across nine dimensions: scale, exhibitor quality, educational programming, networking opportunity, cost, innovation focus, live demos, audience profile, and overall ROI potential. Below, you will find side-by-side tables, pros and cons breakdowns, and our recommendation framework to make your decision easier.

MODEX 2026 at a Glance

  • Dates: April 13–16, 2026
  • Location: Georgia World Congress Center, Atlanta, GA
  • Exhibitors: 1,200+
  • Floor Space: 600,000 sq ft (Halls A, B, C)
  • Registration: Free
  • Seminars: 180 across 13 theaters, 10 tracks
  • Organizer: MHI

CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 at a Glance

  • Dates: March 3–7, 2026
  • Location: Las Vegas Convention Center + Festival Grounds
  • Exhibitors: 2,000+
  • Floor Space: 2.9 million sq ft
  • Highlights: Live outdoor equipment demos daily
  • Networking: CONEXPO After Dark (3 nights)
  • Organizer: AEM (Association of Equipment Manufacturers)

Scale and Exhibition Footprint

The numbers tell an immediate story. CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 is nearly five times larger than MODEX in raw square footage—2.9 million square feet versus 600,000. That is not a fair comparison in isolation, though, because CONEXPO uses a significant portion of its Festival Grounds for outdoor demonstrations of heavy equipment that simply cannot fit inside convention halls. Caterpillar alone occupies a staggering 70,000 square feet, an exhibit footprint larger than most regional trade shows in their entirety.

MODEX counters with density. Its 1,200+ exhibitors are packed into three purpose-designed exhibition halls (A, B, and C) at the Georgia World Congress Center. The result is a tighter, more navigable show floor where you can realistically visit every relevant vendor in three focused days. At CONEXPO, the five-day format exists partly because the sheer geography of the show demands it—walking from the West Hall (earthmoving) to the Festival Grounds (outdoor demos) to the South Hall (underground construction) is a full-day workout.

For attendees who value depth over breadth, MODEX's compact footprint is a feature, not a limitation. For those who want to see every category of construction technology under one enormous umbrella, CONEXPO is unmatched anywhere on Earth.

Educational Programming and Thought Leadership

MODEX 2026 wins on volume and structure. With 180 seminars spread across 13 on-floor theaters covering 10 distinct tracks, the educational program is massive for a show of its size. Topics range from warehouse robotics and autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) to sustainability in supply chain, predictive analytics, and workforce development. Every seminar is included with free registration, which means the educational ROI alone can justify your trip.

CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 takes a different approach. Its Ground Breakers Stage features heavyweight keynotes from C-suite leaders at Caterpillar, AWS, Autodesk, and John Deere, focusing on macro trends like AI in construction, autonomous machinery, and the infrastructure spending pipeline. The sessions skew toward strategic vision rather than tactical implementation. For executives and senior decision-makers, the CONEXPO keynote lineup may deliver more value per hour than MODEX's broader seminar catalog.

If you are a mid-level operations manager looking for specific, implementable takeaways you can act on when you return to work, MODEX has the edge. If you are a VP or C-suite executive seeking big-picture strategic insights and industry direction, CONEXPO's curated keynote program may be the better fit.

Networking and Special Events

Both shows understand that hallway conversations often matter more than booth visits. MODEX 2026 includes the Women in Supply Chain Forum, designed to connect, mentor, and elevate female professionals in a historically male-dominated industry. The Young Professionals Network Reception targets early-career attendees and creates a pipeline of contacts that can pay dividends for decades. Both events are purpose-built for relationship building rather than selling.

CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 counters with three nights of CONEXPO After Dark—large-scale networking events that blend entertainment, food, and deal-making in the Las Vegas nightscape. These events are legendary in the construction industry for their ability to forge connections in a relaxed, high-energy environment. The sheer size of the CONEXPO audience also means your odds of running into a specific prospect or partner are higher simply because more people are there.

Innovation and Startup Ecosystems

MODEX 2026 has a clear structural advantage here. Its StartUp Pavilion is a dedicated zone for emerging companies in supply chain technology, featuring a $10,000 pitch contest that draws venture-backed startups and bootstrapped innovators alike. If you are scouting for disruptive technologies—robotic picking systems, AI-driven demand forecasting, blockchain-based traceability—the StartUp Pavilion is a curated shortcut.

CONEXPO-CON/AGG features a Tech Experience area, but its innovation narrative is different. Innovation here means seeing autonomous haul trucks navigate obstacle courses, watching remote-controlled excavators operate from a thousand miles away, and observing 3D-printed concrete structures take shape in real time. The innovation is visceral and physical rather than digital and software-driven.

Audience Profile and Who Should Attend

The ideal MODEX attendee is a supply chain professional, warehouse operations leader, logistics director, e-commerce fulfillment manager, or technology buyer evaluating material handling equipment, robotics, software platforms, or automation solutions. If your daily work involves moving goods through a warehouse, distribution center, or fulfillment operation, MODEX is purpose-built for you. The free registration model also makes it accessible for mid-career professionals and teams—you can bring your entire operations staff without worrying about per-person registration fees.

The ideal CONEXPO-CON/AGG attendee is a construction company owner, heavy equipment fleet manager, mining executive, infrastructure project manager, or aggregate producer. If your work involves earthmoving, underground construction, concrete, asphalt, cranes, or heavy hauling, CONEXPO is the definitive industry event. The triennial schedule means this show only happens every three years, creating a stronger sense of urgency and commitment from both exhibitors and attendees.

The Crossover Attendee

There is a meaningful overlap for professionals in construction logistics, jobsite material handling, and heavy equipment telematics. If you manage the supply chain that feeds construction projects—delivering materials, managing inventory across job sites, or coordinating fleet logistics—both shows have relevant content. In this case, we recommend prioritizing CONEXPO for its equipment and infrastructure focus, and then attending MODEX for its supply chain software and automation solutions.

Free registration, lower Atlanta hotel rates, a shorter stay, and MARTA transit access all contribute to a total cost that can be 40-60% less than a comparable CONEXPO trip. For budget-conscious teams or companies sending multiple attendees, this cost difference is significant.

Live Demonstrations and Hands-On Experiences

This is where CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 delivers an experience that no indoor-only trade show can match. The Festival Grounds host daily live equipment demonstrations where attendees watch (and sometimes operate) excavators, bulldozers, cranes, and autonomous vehicles in action. Caterpillar's 70,000 square foot exhibit includes working equipment, simulators, and technology stations that create lasting impressions no brochure can replicate.

MODEX 2026 offers live technology demonstrations on the show floor, but the scale and visceral impact are different. You will see robotic arms picking and placing products, autonomous mobile robots navigating simulated warehouse environments, and conveyor systems sorting packages at high speed. These demonstrations are impressive in their precision and technology, but they lack the ground-shaking physical presence of a 100-ton excavator carving through earth.

Both shows understand that seeing technology in action is fundamentally more persuasive than reading a spec sheet. The difference is in the type of technology and the sensory experience it creates.

Bottom Line: Which Show Should You Choose?

If your world is supply chain, warehousing, logistics, fulfillment, or material handling automation, MODEX 2026 is your show. The free registration, 180 included seminars, and focused exhibitor base deliver exceptional ROI at a lower cost. If your world is construction, heavy equipment, earthmoving, or infrastructure, CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 is unmissable—especially since it only happens every three years. If you live at the intersection of both industries, attend both and thank us later.

Planning Your Visit: Practical Tips

For MODEX 2026

  • Book early: Hotels near the Georgia World Congress Center fill fast. Look at properties in downtown Atlanta or along the MARTA Blue/Green line.
  • Pre-register for seminars: Popular sessions fill to capacity. Use the MODEX app to build your schedule before you arrive.
  • Visit the StartUp Pavilion early: Monday afternoon is the least crowded time to have meaningful conversations with emerging companies.
  • Wear comfortable shoes: 600,000 square feet across three halls is still a lot of walking.
  • Bring your team: Free registration means there is no incremental cost to add people. The more eyes you have on the floor, the more opportunities you will find.

For CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026

  • Book hotels months in advance: Las Vegas rates during CONEXPO can double or triple. Consider staying off-strip for better rates.
  • Plan your hall strategy: The West Hall (earthmoving), South Hall (underground), and Festival Grounds (outdoor demos) are spread out. Dedicate specific days to specific zones.
  • Attend CONEXPO After Dark: These networking events are where deals happen. Budget your energy accordingly.
  • Sunscreen and water: The Festival Grounds are outdoors in the Nevada desert. Even in March, the sun is strong.
  • Check the Community Zone: Free access areas can provide value even without a full-price pass.

Final Verdict

Both MODEX 2026 and CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 are exceptional trade shows that dominate their respective industries. They are not competitors—they serve different audiences with different needs. The question is not which show is better, but which show is better for you.

If you work in supply chain management, warehouse operations, e-commerce fulfillment, or logistics technology, MODEX 2026 delivers unmatched value with free registration, 180 seminars, and a curated exhibitor base of 1,200+ companies focused exclusively on your industry. The Atlanta location keeps costs manageable, and the four-day format respects your time.

If you work in construction, heavy equipment, mining, aggregates, or infrastructure development, CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026 is the one event you cannot afford to miss. With 2.9 million square feet, 2,000+ exhibitors, live outdoor demos, and a triennial schedule that creates urgency, this is the industry's definitive gathering. The Las Vegas trip will cost more, but the scale and spectacle deliver proportional value.

Whichever show you choose, arrive with a plan, leave with contacts, and follow up within 48 hours. That is the formula that turns trade show attendance into measurable business results.
