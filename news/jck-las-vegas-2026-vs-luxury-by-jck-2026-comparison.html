JCK Las Vegas 2026 vs Luxury by JCK 2026: Which Jewelry Show?

Published on February 11, 2026 • 8 min read

If you work in the jewelry industry and are planning your trade show calendar for 2026, you have likely encountered both JCK Las Vegas and Luxury by JCK. These two events are co-located at the Venetian Expo and Convention Center in Las Vegas, run during the same week, and are organized by the same parent company. Yet they serve distinctly different segments of the jewelry market. Choosing which one to attend, or whether to attend both, requires understanding the key differences in audience, scope, and value proposition.

Understanding the Audience Differences

The most important distinction between JCK Las Vegas and Luxury by JCK is the audience each event serves.

JCK Las Vegas casts a wide net. The 30,000 attendees include independent jewelers sourcing affordable fashion jewelry, large chain store buyers placing high-volume orders, gemstone dealers from around the world, packaging and display vendors, and jewelry technology companies. The show covers every price point and product category in the industry. If you are in the jewelry business in any capacity, JCK has something for you.

Luxury by JCK, on the other hand, is a curated experience designed for the upper echelon of the market. Attendees are typically buyers from high-end boutiques, luxury department stores, and prestigious independent jewelers who stock designer brands and high-value pieces. The atmosphere is more exclusive, with wider aisles, refined booth designs, and a quieter energy that matches the luxury positioning.

Scope and Product Differences

On the JCK Las Vegas show floor, you will find everything from loose diamonds and colored gemstones to finished sterling silver jewelry, watch components, packaging supplies, point-of-sale systems, and jewelry manufacturing equipment. The breadth is staggering. This makes JCK ideal for buyers who need to source across multiple categories or who are looking to discover new product lines at various price points.

Luxury by JCK strips away the volume-oriented categories and focuses exclusively on prestige. Exhibitors include renowned designer brands, high-end watchmakers, couture bridal collections, and one-of-a-kind fine jewelry pieces. If you are sourcing pieces that retail for thousands or tens of thousands of dollars, Luxury by JCK puts the right products in front of you in a setting that matches their caliber.

Cost Considerations

Both events offer free registration for qualified trade buyers who register in advance, so there is no registration fee difference to worry about. The real cost difference comes down to your time and travel budget.

Since both shows are co-located at the Venetian, you are not paying for separate hotel stays or flights. However, attending both events requires more time on the ground in Las Vegas. Luxury by JCK often opens a day before the main JCK show, so to experience both fully, you may need to add an extra day to your trip.

For exhibitors, the cost picture is different. Booth space at Luxury by JCK commands premium pricing that reflects the exclusive positioning and curated exhibitor list. JCK Las Vegas offers a wider range of booth sizes and price points, making it accessible to smaller vendors and startups.

Who Should Attend JCK Las Vegas?

  • Independent retailers looking to stock a wide range of products across price points.
  • Chain store buyers who need to source high-volume orders at competitive prices.
  • Gemstone and diamond dealers seeking new suppliers or showcasing their inventory.
  • Jewelry manufacturers looking for new retail partners or technology solutions.
  • Industry newcomers who want to survey the entire jewelry market landscape in one trip.
  • Technology and services vendors targeting the jewelry retail community.

Who Should Attend Luxury by JCK?

  • High-end boutique owners sourcing designer and luxury brands for their stores.
  • Luxury department store buyers curating upscale jewelry departments.
  • Designer brands seeking to connect with qualified luxury retail partners.
  • Fine jewelry collectors and advisors looking for rare and exceptional pieces.
  • Bridal jewelry specialists sourcing premium engagement rings and wedding bands.
  • Media and influencers covering the luxury jewelry segment.

Can You Attend Both? Logistics and Strategy

Yes, and many attendees do. Since JCK Las Vegas and Luxury by JCK are co-located at the Venetian Expo, you can walk between the two events within the same day. A single trip to Las Vegas gives you access to both shows, which is one of the great advantages of this co-located format.

Here is a practical strategy for attending both:

  • Day one: Start at Luxury by JCK in the morning when it tends to be quieter. This is the best time for focused conversations with high-end designers and brands. Spend the afternoon at the main JCK floor for an initial overview.
  • Day two: Focus on JCK Las Vegas for sourcing and meetings with mid-market suppliers and service providers. Return to Luxury by JCK in the late afternoon for any follow-up conversations.
  • Day three: Use the final day for deeper dives and closing discussions at whichever event held the most promising leads. This is also a good day for education sessions and networking events.

The key logistical consideration is pacing yourself. Trying to cover every booth at both shows in three days is a recipe for exhaustion. Prioritize your meetings and be willing to skip lower-priority areas to protect your energy for the conversations that matter most.

Verdict: Which Show Is Right for You?

If you operate in the luxury segment exclusively, Luxury by JCK delivers a more focused and efficient experience. You will spend less time walking past irrelevant booths and more time connecting with brands and buyers who match your market position. The refined atmosphere also sets the right tone for high-value business discussions.

If you are a general jewelry retailer, manufacturer, or service provider who serves multiple market segments, JCK Las Vegas is the must-attend event. Its unmatched breadth of exhibitors and attendees makes it the single most important trade show in the North American jewelry calendar.

For professionals who straddle both worlds, attending both events during the same trip is the optimal strategy. The co-located format makes this logistically easy and cost-effective. Plan your schedule thoughtfully, prioritize quality conversations over booth count, and you will leave Las Vegas with a contact list that covers the full spectrum of the jewelry industry.
