How to Network at MODEX 2026: The Ultimate Guide to Atlanta's Premier Supply Chain Show

MODEX 2026 is not just the largest supply chain trade show in the Western Hemisphere — it is the single best networking opportunity for anyone working in logistics, warehousing, manufacturing, or distribution technology. Taking place April 13–16, 2026 at the Georgia World Congress Center (GWCC) in Atlanta, GA, this biennial powerhouse brings together over 1,200 exhibitors spread across 600,000 square feet of show floor in Halls A, B, and C.

Whether you are a procurement manager sourcing your next AS/RS installation, a startup founder pitching autonomous mobile robots, or a supply chain consultant looking to expand your client base, MODEX 2026 offers an unmatched density of decision-makers in a single venue. But with four packed days of exhibits, 180 conference sessions across 13 theaters, live technology demos, and a dozen networking events, the show can overwhelm even experienced attendees.

This guide will give you a concrete, hour-by-hour strategy for maximizing every handshake, every conversation, and every follow-up. We have broken it down into pre-show preparation, a day-by-day playbook, hall-by-hall tactics, and a post-show follow-up system that turns badge scans into business relationships.

1. Pre-Show Preparation (4 Weeks Out)

The most productive networkers at MODEX do not start networking on April 13. They start weeks before the doors open. Here is your pre-show checklist:

Register Early and Strategically

MODEX registration is completely free for access to the exhibit hall and all 180 conference sessions. Go to modexshow.com and register immediately if you have not already. Once registered, you will gain access to the exhibitor directory, the matchmaking tool, and the mobile app — all critical for pre-show planning.

Build Your Target List

With 1,200+ exhibitors, you cannot visit them all. Narrow your focus to three tiers:

  • Tier 1 (Must-See, 10-15 companies): These are exhibitors you need to meet, period. Schedule meetings using the MODEX matchmaking platform. Companies like Toyota Material Handling, Honeywell Intelligrated, Dematic, FANUC, Exotec, and Kardex will have massive booths and tight schedules — pre-booked meetings are essential.
  • Tier 2 (Should-See, 20-30 companies): Exhibitors you want to explore but do not need dedicated meetings with. Plan to walk by their booths during off-peak hours (typically first thing in the morning or during major keynotes).
  • Tier 3 (Nice-to-See): Mark these on your floor map for spontaneous visits when you have gaps in your schedule.

Set Up Pre-Show Meetings

The MODEX matchmaking tool lets you request meetings with exhibitors directly. Send requests at least two weeks before the show. In your message, be specific about what you are looking for. A request that says "I manage a 200,000 sq ft distribution center processing 10,000 orders/day and I am evaluating AS/RS solutions for a Q3 deployment" will get a response from every exhibitor. A generic "I would like to learn about your products" may not.

Pro Tip

Connect with exhibitors on LinkedIn before the show. Send a personalized connection request mentioning MODEX and your specific interest. This warms up the relationship so that when you arrive at their booth, you are already a known contact rather than a cold walk-up.

Prepare Your Digital Identity

Update your LinkedIn profile with a professional headshot, a compelling headline, and a summary that clearly states what you do and what you are looking for. During the show, people will look you up on LinkedIn within minutes of meeting you. Make sure what they find reinforces the impression you made in person.

2. What to Bring: Your Networking Kit

Your networking success depends partly on what you carry on the show floor. Here is the essential kit:

  • Business cards (200+ minimum): Yes, in 2026, business cards still matter at trade shows. They are the fastest way to exchange contact information when you are having rapid-fire conversations. Bring more than you think you need.
  • A portable phone charger: You will be using the MODEX app, scanning QR codes, taking photos of demos, and messaging contacts all day. Your phone battery will not last. Bring a compact 10,000+ mAh charger.
  • Comfortable shoes: The GWCC is massive. You will walk 15,000-20,000 steps per day across 600,000 square feet. Wear broken-in, professional-looking shoes with good support. Do not sacrifice comfort for style.
  • A slim notebook or note-taking app: After each meaningful conversation, jot down the person's name, company, what you discussed, and any follow-up items. This is critical for post-show follow-up. Your memory will blur after 50+ conversations.
  • A lightweight bag: You will collect brochures, product samples, and swag. Bring a professional-looking tote or messenger bag to keep your hands free for handshakes.

3. Hall-by-Hall Breakdown

Understanding the GWCC layout is essential for efficient networking. The show spans three enormous halls, each with distinct focuses. Plan your route to minimize backtracking.

Hall A

Focus: Emerging Technology & Innovation

  • StartUp Pavilion
  • AMR demonstrations
  • AI/ML software vendors
  • Robotics integrators
  • Pitch contest stage

Best for: Innovation scouts, VCs, startup founders, technology buyers

Hall B

Focus: Core Material Handling

  • Conveyor & sortation systems
  • AS/RS solutions
  • Forklift & lift equipment
  • Toyota, Dematic, Honeywell
  • Live conveyor demos

Best for: Warehouse managers, distribution center operators, procurement teams

Hall C

Focus: Software & Services

  • WMS/WES/WCS platforms
  • ERP integrations
  • Consulting firms
  • Sustainability solutions
  • Conference theaters

Best for: IT directors, operations executives, supply chain consultants

Navigation Strategy

The halls are connected but the walks between them are long. Dedicate morning blocks to one hall and afternoon blocks to another. Do not zigzag between halls — you will lose 30-40 minutes per transition, and fatigue will kill your networking energy by 3 PM.

4. Day-by-Day Strategy

Each day of MODEX has a different character. Tailoring your approach to the rhythm of the show will dramatically increase your networking ROI.

Day 1 — Monday, April 13: Orientation & First Impressions

8:00 - 9:30 AM
Arrive early, collect your badge, and do a reconnaissance walk through all three halls. Do not stop for deep conversations yet — just orient yourself to the layout, mark booths you want to return to, and note where the demonstration stages, food courts, and charging stations are located.
9:30 - 12:00 PM
Begin your Tier 1 meetings. The first morning is when exhibitor teams are freshest, most enthusiastic, and least rushed. This is your best window for in-depth conversations with decision-makers at major exhibitors.
12:00 - 1:30 PM
Skip the crowded food court. Walk to a restaurant on Andrew Young International Boulevard or grab lunch at CNN Center (a 5-minute walk). Use lunch to debrief your morning notes and adjust your afternoon plan.
1:30 - 4:00 PM
Attend 2-3 conference sessions that align with your focus tracks. Sit near the front and introduce yourself to people in adjacent seats before the session starts. Ask a question during Q&A to raise your visibility.
5:30 - 7:30 PM
Young Professionals Network Reception. This is the first major social event of the show. Attend even if you do not consider yourself "young" — the energy is high and the conversations are less transactional than on the show floor. Arrive in the first 20 minutes when groups are still forming and people are more open to new connections.

Day 2 — Tuesday, April 14: Deep Dives & Innovation

8:30 - 10:00 AM
Women in Supply Chain Forum. This event is open to all genders and draws senior leaders from major companies. It is one of the highest-value networking opportunities at the show because the attendee quality is exceptional and the format encourages genuine conversation rather than sales pitches.
10:00 - 12:30 PM
Focus on Hall A and the StartUp Pavilion. Meet emerging companies, see cutting-edge technology before it becomes mainstream, and connect with founders who are eager to build relationships. Startups are the most receptive to conversations because they need visibility.
12:30 - 1:30 PM
Working lunch — invite a new contact you met in the morning to join you. One-on-one meals are where surface-level trade show exchanges become genuine professional relationships.
3:00 - 4:00 PM
StartUp Pavilion Pitch Contest ($10,000 prize). Do not miss this. The audience is filled with investors, corporate innovation leaders, and media. The presentations themselves surface the most promising new technologies, and the networking during breaks between pitches is outstanding. Position yourself near the exit where people congregate to discuss what they just saw.
4:00 - 5:30 PM
Return to Tier 2 exhibitors in Halls B and C. The late afternoon is quieter, and booth staff are more willing to have relaxed, detailed conversations. This is the ideal time to ask in-depth technical questions and request follow-up demos.

Day 3 — Wednesday, April 15: Relationship Building & Industry Night

9:00 - 11:30 AM
Follow up on Day 1 and Day 2 conversations. Return to booths where you had promising initial meetings, bring specific questions, and advance the conversation toward concrete next steps (a post-show call, a site visit, an RFP timeline).
11:30 - 1:00 PM
Attend sessions on sustainability and manufacturing planning/sourcing — two of the 10 conference tracks that are reshaping supply chain strategy. These sessions attract C-level attendees who are harder to catch on the show floor.
1:00 - 3:00 PM
Live demo time. Watch AMR and AS/RS demonstrations from companies like Exotec, FANUC, and Kardex. Stand near the demo area and strike up conversations with fellow attendees. Shared experiences (like watching a robot palletize in real time) are natural conversation starters.
Evening
MHI Industry Night. This is the marquee social event of MODEX 2026. The atmosphere is relaxed, the conversations flow freely, and the relationships formed here often outlast the ones made on the show floor. Do not skip it. Dress one notch above show-floor casual, bring plenty of business cards, and set a personal goal of making five meaningful new connections.

Day 4 — Thursday, April 16: Close Strong

9:00 - 11:00 AM
Final booth visits. Many attendees leave on the last day, so the halls are less crowded and exhibitors have more time for you. Use this window for the Tier 3 exhibitors you have not yet visited and for any follow-up conversations you need to close.
11:00 - 1:00 PM
Attend your final conference sessions. Focus on forward-looking tracks like automation/robotics and distribution/warehousing that will give you talking points for post-show follow-up emails.
1:00 - 3:00 PM
Use the final hours for "collection" conversations — brief stops at booths to grab materials, confirm contact details, and solidify commitments for post-show calls. Begin organizing your notes while details are fresh.

5. Key Networking Events

MODEX 2026 offers structured networking events that are far more productive than random show-floor encounters. Prioritize these:

  • Young Professionals Network Reception (Monday, April 13): Ideal for early-career professionals but valuable for anyone who wants to meet the next generation of supply chain leaders. The format is informal and the energy is high.
  • Women in Supply Chain Forum (Tuesday, April 14): A morning event with panels and facilitated networking. Draws senior decision-makers and is consistently rated as one of the highest-quality networking events at the show.
  • StartUp Pavilion Pitch Contest (Tuesday, April 14, 3:00-4:00 PM): Ten startups compete for a $10,000 prize. The audience includes investors, corporate innovation leaders, and media. Excellent place to discover emerging technologies and connect with entrepreneurial founders.
  • MHI Industry Night (Wednesday evening): The signature social event. This is where formal business conversations give way to genuine relationship-building over food and drinks. Plan to stay for at least two hours.
Event Networking Rule

Arrive in the first 15 minutes and leave in the last 15 minutes. The beginning of an event is when people are most open to meeting new faces. The end is when the strongest connections linger for deeper conversations. The middle is noise.

6. Conversation Tactics That Work

The difference between a forgettable badge scan and a relationship that generates business comes down to how you handle conversations. Here are proven tactics for MODEX:

The 30-Second Introduction

Prepare a concise, compelling introduction that answers three questions: Who are you? What do you do? What are you looking for? Example: "I am Sarah Chen, Director of Distribution at a mid-market e-commerce company. We process 15,000 orders a day out of two DCs on the East Coast, and I am here to evaluate robotic picking solutions for a deployment we are planning in Q4." This gives the other person immediate context and a reason to continue the conversation.

Lead with Questions, Not Pitches

The best networkers ask questions. On the show floor, try these openers:

  • "What is the most interesting thing you have seen at the show so far?"
  • "What challenge brought you to MODEX this year?"
  • "How does your solution handle [specific use case]?"
  • "What is different about your approach compared to what I would see at the booth next door?"

The Graceful Exit

You will have hundreds of potential conversations over four days. Not every one deserves 20 minutes. When you need to move on, use the exchange-and-exit: "This has been really helpful — can I grab your card? I would love to follow up after the show to discuss [specific topic]." This closes the conversation positively while creating a concrete follow-up path.

The Booth Staff Strategy

When visiting exhibitors like Toyota Material Handling or Dematic, do not just speak with the first person who approaches you. Ask to speak with a solutions engineer or a regional sales manager who covers your territory. The booth greeter is trained to qualify leads; the specialist can have the substantive conversation you actually need.

7. Leveraging Live Demos

MODEX is famous for its live technology demonstrations, and they are one of your best networking tools. You will see AMRs navigating warehouse environments, AS/RS systems picking and storing in real time, conveyor and sortation systems processing packages at production speeds, and execution software platforms managing orders live.

Here is how to use demos for networking:

  • Arrive 5 minutes early and position yourself near other attendees. Introduce yourself while waiting for the demo to start.
  • During the demo, observe not just the technology but the questions other attendees ask. Someone asking detailed technical questions is likely a serious buyer or decision-maker — exactly the person you want to know.
  • After the demo, stay near the demonstration area. This is when the most productive conversations happen, because attendees naturally discuss what they just saw. "What did you think of the cycle time on that AS/RS?" is a conversation starter that works every time.
  • Ask the demo engineer to compare what you just saw to real-world deployments. Demo engineers are often the most knowledgeable people at the booth and can provide insights that sales reps cannot.

8. The StartUp Pavilion and Pitch Contest

Located in Hall A, the StartUp Pavilion is a dedicated area for early-stage companies that are bringing new ideas to the supply chain industry. This section deserves special attention for several reasons:

  • Access to founders: Unlike established exhibitors where you might speak with a regional rep, startup booths are typically staffed by the CEO or CTO. You get direct access to the person making strategic decisions.
  • Less competition for attention: While major exhibitors like Honeywell Intelligrated draw massive crowds, startup booths often have quieter moments where you can have extended, meaningful conversations.
  • Innovation scouting: If part of your job involves identifying emerging technologies, the StartUp Pavilion is the most efficient way to survey the landscape in a single afternoon.
  • Investment opportunities: VCs, corporate venture arms, and angel investors attend MODEX specifically to scout the StartUp Pavilion. If you are raising capital, this is your stage.

The Pitch Contest on Tuesday, April 14 from 3:00 to 4:00 PM is the centerpiece event, with a $10,000 prize for the winning startup. The judging panel typically includes industry executives and investors, and the audience is filled with potential customers and partners. Attend the full hour, take notes on every presentation, and approach the founders afterward — they will be energized and receptive to new connections.

9. Working the Conference Sessions

MODEX 2026 offers 180 free conference sessions across 13 theaters, organized into 10 tracks: automation/robotics, distribution/warehousing, manufacturing planning/sourcing, sustainability, and six additional specialized tracks. These sessions are networking goldmines if you approach them strategically.

Session Selection Strategy

Do not attend sessions just to learn — attend sessions to meet people. Choose sessions where your target audience will be. If you sell WMS software, attend sessions on warehouse operations where your potential customers will be in the audience. If you are evaluating robotics, attend automation sessions where you will sit beside peers facing the same challenges.

Before the Session

Arrive 10 minutes early. The seats fill up quickly in popular sessions, but early arrival also gives you time to introduce yourself to neighbors. A simple "What brings you to this session?" opens the door to learning about their company, their challenges, and whether there is a basis for a professional relationship.

During the Session

Ask a question during Q&A. A thoughtful, well-phrased question accomplishes two things: it signals your expertise to the entire room, and it creates a natural icebreaker for post-session conversations ("Great question you asked about labor optimization — we are dealing with the same issue").

After the Session

Do not rush out. The 5 minutes after a session ends are among the most productive networking minutes at the entire show. Approach the speaker to introduce yourself, exchange cards with people you chatted with during the session, and suggest continuing the conversation over coffee or at a specific booth.

10. Post-Show Follow-Up System

The most critical networking happens after MODEX ends. Research consistently shows that 80% of trade show leads are never followed up on. Your follow-up system is what separates you from the 80%.

Within 24 Hours (Thursday Evening or Friday)

  • Send LinkedIn connection requests to every meaningful contact with a personalized note: "Great meeting you at MODEX at the [specific booth/event]. I enjoyed our conversation about [specific topic]."
  • Organize your notes and business cards into three categories: Hot (immediate opportunity), Warm (future potential), and Archive (good to know).
  • Enter all contacts into your CRM or a spreadsheet with notes on what you discussed and proposed next steps.

Within 48 Hours

  • Send personalized follow-up emails to your Hot contacts. Reference specific details from your conversation to show that you were genuinely engaged. Propose a concrete next step: a 30-minute call, a demo, a site visit, or sending relevant case studies.
  • Share a post on LinkedIn about your MODEX experience, tagging companies and people you met. This extends your visibility and reminds contacts about you.

Within One Week

  • Follow up with Warm contacts. These are people who may not have an immediate need but could become important relationships over time.
  • Schedule follow-up calls for your Hot contacts. The goal is to have a conversation within 10 business days of the show while the memory is still fresh.
  • Send a brief recap email to your team or management summarizing key takeaways, promising vendors, and recommended next steps.
The 48-Hour Rule

Follow up within 48 hours or do not bother. After 48 hours, your new contacts have returned to their regular routines and your conversation at MODEX has faded. Speed in follow-up directly correlates with conversion rates.

11. Atlanta Insider Tips

MODEX takes place in the heart of downtown Atlanta, and knowing the area will make your experience significantly smoother.

Transportation

The Georgia World Congress Center is served by MARTA rail (Vine City or GWCC/CNN Center stations). If you are flying into Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, MARTA runs directly to downtown in about 20 minutes for $2.50. It is faster and cheaper than rideshare during rush hour. If you do use rideshare, set your pickup point to a specific building entrance to avoid confusion at the massive GWCC complex.

Hotels

Book a hotel within walking distance of the GWCC — the Omni Hotel at CNN Center, the Westin Peachtree Plaza, and the Hilton Atlanta are all popular choices. Staying nearby lets you return to your room between sessions to recharge (literally and figuratively) and change for evening events.

Dining for Networking

Avoid eating at the convention center every day. Some of the best networking at MODEX happens over dinner at nearby restaurants. Consider Der Biergarten (a 3-minute walk, great for groups), Alma Cocina (upscale Latin, good for client dinners), or STK Atlanta (downtown, good ambiance for deal-making). Make reservations early — 44,000+ attendees strain local restaurants.

Weather

Mid-April in Atlanta averages highs around 73°F (23°C) with a chance of afternoon rain showers. Dress in layers — the convention center can be cold, but the walks between buildings will be warm. Pack a compact umbrella.

Fitness & Recovery

Four days of trade show networking is physically demanding. Stay hydrated (carry a refillable water bottle), get 7+ hours of sleep each night even if the temptation to stay out late is strong, and stretch each morning. Your energy level on Day 4 determines whether you close strong or stumble to the finish.

Your MODEX 2026 Networking Checklist

To summarize this guide into an actionable checklist:

  1. Register now at modexshow.com (it is free).
  2. Build your tiered target list of exhibitors and set up pre-show meetings.
  3. Update your LinkedIn profile and connect with target contacts before the show.
  4. Pack your networking kit: 200+ business cards, portable charger, comfortable shoes, notebook.
  5. Attend all four key networking events: YPN Reception (Monday), Women in Supply Chain Forum (Tuesday), Pitch Contest (Tuesday), MHI Industry Night (Wednesday).
  6. Work the halls strategically: mornings for Tier 1 meetings, afternoons for sessions and demos, evenings for social events.
  7. Leverage live demos and conference sessions as conversation starters.
  8. Follow up within 48 hours with personalized LinkedIn messages and emails.
  9. Convert connections to conversations by scheduling calls within 10 business days.

MODEX 2026 runs April 13-16 at the Georgia World Congress Center in Atlanta. With 1,200+ exhibitors, 600,000 square feet of show floor, 180 free conference sessions, live technology demos, and the industry's best networking events, it is the single most important week on the supply chain calendar. Prepare well, execute your strategy, follow up fast, and you will leave Atlanta with relationships that drive your business forward for years to come.
