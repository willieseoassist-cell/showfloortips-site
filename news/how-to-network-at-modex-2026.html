How to Network at MODEX 2026: A Complete Guide

Published on February 11, 2026 • 10 min read

MODEX 2026 is the supply chain industry's flagship event, drawing more than 45,000 professionals and over 1,000 exhibitors to the Georgia World Congress Center in Atlanta from April 13 through 16. Spanning roughly 600,000 square feet across Halls A, B, and C, the show is a dense concentration of manufacturing executives, logistics managers, warehouse operators, automation engineers, and technology vendors all converging in one place. If you work anywhere along the supply chain, MODEX represents the single best opportunity of the year to build relationships that can reshape your operations, your career, or your business. This guide will help you make the most of every hour on the show floor.

Pre-Show Preparation: Lay the Groundwork Before You Arrive

With 45,000 attendees moving through a massive convention center, walking in without a plan is a recipe for exhaustion and missed opportunities. The professionals who get the most out of MODEX are the ones who start working weeks before the doors open.

  • Define your networking objectives. Are you sourcing a new warehouse management system? Evaluating robotic solutions for your distribution center? Looking for a career move into supply chain leadership? Seeking channel partners for your material handling product? Write down your top three goals and use them to prioritize every meeting, session, and booth visit.
  • Study the exhibitor list and floor plan. MODEX organizes its 600,000 square feet into distinct zones. Hall A houses the StartUp Pavilion, Sustainability Zone, and Last Mile solutions. Halls B and C concentrate on automation, robotics, autonomous mobile robots (AMRs), automated storage and retrieval systems (AS/RS), and conveyor systems. Knowing the layout in advance lets you map efficient routes between priority booths and avoid wasting time crisscrossing the venue.
  • Schedule meetings through the MODEX platform. The official MODEX website and app allow you to browse exhibitors and request appointments. Reach out to key contacts at least two to three weeks before the show. High-demand exhibitors fill their calendars early, and a pre-scheduled meeting guarantees face time that a walk-up visit cannot.
  • Register for ticketed events immediately. Several of MODEX's highest-value networking events require separate registration and sell out. Industry Night with comedian Jim Gaffigan costs $75 per ticket and includes a $30,000 door prize drawing. The Women in Supply Chain Forum is $125. The Young Professionals Event is just $10. These are not afterthoughts. They are some of the best networking environments at the entire show.
  • Plan your seminar schedule around networking potential. MODEX offers more than 200 free seminars across 10 educational tracks, including automation and robotics, distribution and warehousing, and manufacturing. Attending the same track sessions throughout the week means you will repeatedly encounter the same group of professionals who share your interests, which naturally accelerates relationship building.

Key Networking Venues and Events at MODEX 2026

MODEX is not just a trade show floor. It is a four-day ecosystem of events, sessions, and social gatherings, each offering a different kind of networking opportunity. Here is where to focus your energy.

The Exhibition Floor: Halls A, B, and C

The show floor is the beating heart of MODEX, and it is enormous. More than 1,000 exhibitors fill the Georgia World Congress Center with live demonstrations of robotics, conveyor systems, warehouse software, packaging equipment, and every other category of supply chain technology. The floor is busiest between 10 AM and 2 PM. For deeper conversations with exhibitors, visit in the first hour after opening or during the last hour before closing when booth staff are more available for substantive discussion rather than quick demos.

Hall A deserves special attention for its concentration of emerging technology. The StartUp Pavilion brings together early-stage companies that are building the next generation of supply chain solutions. The Sustainability Zone highlights companies addressing environmental impact in logistics and warehousing. The Last Mile area focuses on the final leg of delivery, a segment that has exploded in importance with the growth of e-commerce. These areas attract forward-thinking professionals and tend to foster more innovative conversations than the established vendor sections.

Halls B and C are where the heavy automation lives. If your focus is on robotics, AMRs, AS/RS, or large-scale conveyor and sortation systems, plan to spend significant time here. Live product demonstrations draw crowds, and the energy around a working robot cell or a fully automated picking system creates natural conversation starters with the engineers and operations leaders watching alongside you.

Keynote Sessions

The MODEX 2026 keynote lineup features three speakers who will draw large, senior-level audiences. Richard McPhail brings the perspective of a Fortune 500 executive navigating supply chain complexity at massive scale. Salim Ismail, known for his work on exponential organizations and disruptive technology, will attract the innovation-minded segment of the audience. Dale Earnhardt Jr. brings a unique angle on teamwork, performance, and logistics from the world of motorsports. Arrive early for keynotes to claim a good seat, and use the pre-session waiting time to introduce yourself to the people around you. Post-keynote hallway conversations are some of the most productive networking minutes at MODEX because the shared experience of a compelling talk gives everyone an instant conversation topic.

Industry Night with Jim Gaffigan

Industry Night is the signature social event of MODEX 2026, and it deserves its own strategy. The evening features comedian Jim Gaffigan performing for the MODEX community, with food, drinks, and the chance to win a $30,000 door prize. At $75 per ticket, it is one of the best values in trade show entertainment. More importantly, the relaxed, celebratory atmosphere breaks down the professional barriers that keep people in sales mode on the show floor. This is where you meet people as human beings first and business contacts second, and those relationships tend to be more durable. Buy your ticket early because this event sells out.

Women in Supply Chain Forum

The Women in Supply Chain Forum is a $125 ticketed event that brings together women leaders from across the supply chain industry for focused discussion, mentorship, and networking. Even in 2026, women remain underrepresented in supply chain leadership, which makes this forum both an important industry initiative and a concentrated networking opportunity. The attendees at this event tend to be highly engaged and committed to building meaningful professional relationships, making it one of the highest-quality networking environments at MODEX.

Young Professionals Event

At just $10, the Young Professionals Event is designed for early-career supply chain professionals and recent graduates. If you are within the first decade of your career, this event connects you with a peer group that will grow alongside you in the industry for years to come. The relationships formed among young professionals at industry events often become the most enduring and mutually beneficial connections of a career. If you are a more experienced professional, attending this event as a mentor or speaker can be equally valuable.

Free Seminars and Educational Sessions

MODEX offers more than 200 free seminars organized across 10 educational tracks. The automation and robotics track draws engineers and operations leaders evaluating new technology. The distribution and warehousing track attracts warehouse managers and logistics directors. The manufacturing track pulls in plant managers and production engineers. These sessions are 30 to 60 minutes each and take place in rooms adjacent to the show floor. The Q&A periods at the end of each session are excellent networking moments. Listen for attendees who ask particularly sharp questions, then introduce yourself afterward. Speakers are also highly approachable in the minutes following their presentations.

Supply Chain Industry Networking Tips

The supply chain community has its own professional culture. Understanding these norms will help you connect more effectively at MODEX.

  • Speak the language of operations. Supply chain professionals are pragmatic and results-oriented. They care about throughput, accuracy, cost per unit, labor efficiency, and return on investment. Abstract technology pitches fall flat. Frame every conversation around measurable operational outcomes.
  • Bring real numbers. If you are a vendor, come prepared with case studies that include specific metrics: how much you reduced pick times, how many labor hours you saved, what ROI your customers achieved and in what timeframe. Supply chain buyers are analytical. Data earns credibility faster than any marketing claim.
  • Respect the operator perspective. Many MODEX attendees are warehouse managers, distribution center supervisors, and plant operators who live with the daily reality of fulfilling orders and moving product. They are skeptical of solutions that look great in a demo but fail in a real-world environment with dust, temperature swings, and imperfect inventory data. Acknowledge the complexity of their environment.
  • Cross-pollinate across the supply chain. MODEX draws professionals from manufacturing, warehousing, transportation, and last-mile delivery. Some of the most valuable connections happen across these segments. A warehouse operator might find a game-changing solution at a manufacturing automation booth. A transportation executive might discover a loading dock technology they had never considered. Stay curious and venture outside your immediate focus area.
  • Engage with startups seriously. The StartUp Pavilion in Hall A is filled with companies that may be solving your most pressing problems in ways you have not imagined. Startups at MODEX tend to be more accessible and more eager for in-depth technical conversations than established vendors. An early partnership with a promising startup can give you a competitive advantage before their solution becomes mainstream.

  • LinkedIn. The supply chain community is highly active on LinkedIn. Send connection requests the same day you meet someone and include a specific detail about your conversation. Follow up on LinkedIn with relevant content in the weeks after the show.

On-Site Networking Strategies

  • Wear comfortable shoes and dress business casual. MODEX is a working trade show, not a formal conference. You will walk miles across 600,000 square feet of exhibition space. Comfortable footwear is not optional. Business casual is the standard dress code, and you will fit in with khakis, a button-down, and comfortable shoes.
  • Carry a portable charger and bring extra business cards. Your phone is your map, your meeting schedule, your badge scanner, and your note-taking tool. Keep it charged. And despite the digital age, business cards remain ubiquitous at supply chain trade shows. Bring more than you think you need.
  • Use demonstrations as conversation starters. When you see a crowd gathered around a live robot demo or an automated picking system, join it. Turn to the person next to you and ask what they think of the technology. Shared observation of a live demonstration is one of the most natural ways to start a conversation at a trade show.
  • Eat strategically. The food courts and dining areas at the Georgia World Congress Center are natural gathering points. Instead of rushing through lunch alone, look for an open seat at a table with other attendees and introduce yourself. Some of the best trade show conversations happen over a casual lunch.
  • Attend at least one evening event. The show floor closes in the late afternoon, but MODEX networking continues well into the evening. Whether it is Industry Night, a vendor dinner, or an informal gathering at a nearby Atlanta restaurant, the after-hours environment is where transactional conversations transform into genuine relationships.
  • Take notes immediately after every meaningful conversation. After you exchange information with a contact, step aside and spend 60 seconds recording key details: what you discussed, what they need, what you promised to follow up on. By the end of a four-day show, you will have met dozens of people. Without notes, the details blur together and your follow-up suffers.

Follow-Up Strategies After MODEX

The connections you make at MODEX only become valuable if you follow up effectively. The window is narrow: the first 48 to 72 hours after the show are critical.

  • Send personalized follow-ups within 48 hours. Reference something specific from your conversation. Mention the booth where you met, the demo you watched together, or the seminar topic you discussed. Generic follow-up emails get deleted. Specific ones get responses.
  • Segment your contacts by priority and action needed. Separate hot leads from warm contacts from general connections. Hot leads get a phone call or meeting request. Warm contacts get a thoughtful email with relevant content. General connections get a LinkedIn request with a personal note.
  • Share something of value before you ask for anything. Send a relevant article, a case study, a recording from a MODEX seminar, or an introduction to someone else they should know. Leading with generosity builds the kind of goodwill that turns a trade show acquaintance into a long-term professional relationship.
  • Schedule follow-up meetings within two weeks. For your highest-priority contacts, propose a 20 to 30 minute virtual meeting while the MODEX experience is still fresh. The longer you wait, the more the momentum fades and the harder it becomes to re-establish the connection.
  • Stay engaged with the MHI community year-round. MODEX is organized by MHI, the trade association for the material handling and supply chain industry. MHI offers webinars, industry reports, and regional events throughout the year. Staying active in the MHI ecosystem keeps you connected to the same professionals you met at MODEX and positions you well for the next show cycle.

Final Thoughts

MODEX 2026 brings together 45,000 supply chain professionals and 1,000 exhibitors in one of the most dynamic and fastest-changing industries in the world. From the StartUp Pavilion in Hall A to the massive automation demonstrations in Halls B and C, from the keynote stage to Industry Night with Jim Gaffigan, the show offers an extraordinary density of networking opportunities. But density alone does not produce results. Preparation, intentionality, and disciplined follow-up are what separate the professionals who leave MODEX with a stack of forgotten business cards from those who leave with relationships that drive real business value. Plan your week in Atlanta carefully, invest your energy in the events and conversations that align with your goals, and treat the 48 hours after you return home as the most important part of the entire trip.
