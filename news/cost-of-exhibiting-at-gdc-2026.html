How Much Does It Cost to Exhibit at GDC 2026?

ShowFloorTips Editorial February 11, 2026

The Game Developers Conference (GDC), now rebranded as the GDC Festival of Gaming for 2026, is the premier professional event for the video game industry. Held March 9-13 at San Francisco's Moscone Convention Center, GDC attracts over 30,000 game developers, publishers, platform holders, tool makers, and investors. The Festival Hall (formerly GDC Expo) is where exhibitors showcase game engines, middleware, development tools, publishing services, and playable games. Here is what it costs to exhibit.

Booth Space Costs

GDC uses a partner/sponsorship model for exhibit space rather than a simple per-square-foot rate. Pricing is negotiated through the GDC sales team and varies by location, size, and partnership level:

  • Indie/startup pod or tabletop: $5,000 - $8,000
  • 10x10 standard booth (100 sq ft): $15,000 - $25,000
  • 10x20 booth (200 sq ft): $25,000 - $40,000
  • 20x20 island booth (400 sq ft): $40,000 - $80,000+

GDC exhibitor packages typically include badges, exhibitor directory listings, and marketing benefits. The 2026 rebrand introduced new partnership tiers, so contact the GDC sales team directly at gdconf.com for current pricing.

Booth Design and Construction

  • Pop-up display with demo stations: $500 - $2,500
  • Custom modular 10x10: $8,000 - $25,000
  • Custom 20x20 with playable demo kiosks: $30,000 - $80,000
  • Rental booth (turnkey): $3,000 - $12,000 for 10x10

Game industry exhibitors typically need multiple demo stations with PCs, monitors, headphones, and controllers. Budget $1,000 - $3,000 per demo station for equipment rental.

Drayage and Material Handling

  • Standard drayage rate: $130 - $180 per CWT
  • Typical 10x10 booth (600-1,000 lbs): $780 - $1,800
  • Typical 20x20 booth (2,000-3,500 lbs): $2,600 - $6,300

San Francisco's Moscone Center uses Freeman as its general contractor. Ship early to avoid premium rates.

Electrical and Internet

  • Standard electrical (500W): $350 - $750
  • Higher-power service (for multiple PCs): $700 - $2,000
  • Dedicated Wi-Fi: $800 - $2,500
  • Hardwired internet (essential for online game demos): $1,200 - $3,000

If you are demoing multiplayer or cloud-streamed games, hardwired internet is essential. Budget accordingly.

Travel and Lodging

San Francisco is one of the most expensive convention cities in the United States:

  • Hotel (5 nights, SoMa/downtown): $1,500 - $3,000 per person
  • Flights: $200 - $600 per person (SFO or OAK)
  • Meals: $50 - $100 per person per day
  • Ground transportation: $150 - $300 per person
  • GDC passes (if not included in exhibitor package): $649 - $1,699 per person

For a team of three, travel costs range from $6,000 to $15,000. Booking through the GDC hotel block can provide modest savings on downtown San Francisco rates.

Marketing Materials

  • Pre-show outreach and PR: $500 - $3,000
  • Demo equipment rental (PCs, monitors): $1,000 - $5,000
  • Printed materials: $200 - $800
  • Branded swag: $500 - $2,000
  • GDC guide advertising: $3,000 - $15,000

Indie studios can exhibit at GDC for as little as $12,000 - $20,000 using a small pod or tabletop. Tool and middleware companies with standard 10x10 booths should budget $25,000 - $45,000. Major publishers and platform holders typically invest $100,000 - $300,000+.

Tips to Reduce Your GDC Costs

  1. Apply for indie showcase areas: GDC offers affordable exhibit options for independent developers.
  2. Bring your own equipment: Ship or carry personal laptops and monitors to avoid rental fees.
  3. Rent instead of build: Turnkey booth rentals save 40-60% over custom construction.
  4. Stay outside downtown: BART-accessible hotels in Oakland or the Mission District save 30-50% on lodging.
  5. Leverage earned media: GDC press coverage is extensive; a compelling demo can generate free exposure.
