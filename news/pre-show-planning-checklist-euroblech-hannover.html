Pre-Show Planning Checklist for EuroBLECH Hannover

ShowFloorTips · January 11, 2026

Every year, thousands of companies invest in exhibition space. The ones that plan ahead see the greatest returns. The pre-show planning phase is where the foundation for trade show success is built. This checklist for EuroBLECH Hannover (October 20-23, 2026 at , Hannover) covers every critical action item from the moment you commit to exhibiting through the day before the show floor opens.

12+ Weeks Before EuroBLECH Hannover

  • Register as an exhibitor and secure your booth space (pricing starts at approximately $3,000 - $15,000).
  • Review the exhibitor manual and note all deadlines for services, submissions, and payments.
  • Define your show objectives: number of leads, meetings scheduled, products to feature, and revenue targets.
  • Establish your total exhibition budget including booth, travel, marketing, and contingency funds.
  • Begin booth design process with your design team or vendor.
  • Book hotel rooms through the official show hotel block for best rates and shuttle access to .

8-12 Weeks Before the Show

  • Finalize booth design and approve production specifications.
  • Order all venue services: electrical, internet, plumbing, cleaning, and lead retrieval devices.
  • Book freight and shipping for booth materials to in Hannover.
  • Create pre-show marketing campaign: email invitations, social media announcements, and landing page.
  • Identify and reach out to top 20-30 prospects you want to meet at EuroBLECH Hannover.
  • Select and confirm your booth staff team.
  • Order promotional materials, giveaways, and printed collateral.

4-8 Weeks Before the Show

  • Launch pre-show email campaign to your prospect list and existing customers.
  • Schedule meetings with key contacts through the show's matchmaking platform or direct outreach.
  • Confirm all travel arrangements: flights, ground transportation, and accommodations.
  • Begin booth staff training on talking points, product demonstrations, and lead qualification criteria.
  • Ship booth materials to advance warehouse if the venue offers early receiving.
  • Test all technology: lead capture devices, presentations, demos, and display equipment.
  • Submit required booth drawings, electrical plans, and insurance certificates to show organizers.

1-2 Weeks Before the Show

  • Send reminder emails to scheduled meetings and priority contacts.
  • Print final copies of all marketing materials and presentation handouts.
  • Conduct final booth staff briefing covering daily schedules, roles, and objectives.
  • Confirm freight delivery status and track shipment to .
  • Pack personal toolkit: tape, zip ties, markers, power strips, chargers, and first-aid supplies.
  • Download and test the official show app on all team devices.
  • Verify all hotel reservations and ground transportation arrangements.
  • Prepare daily briefing templates and lead tracking spreadsheets or dashboards.
