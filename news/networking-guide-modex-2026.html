Networking Guide

The Complete Networking Guide for MODEX 2026

ShowFloorTips · February 10, 2026

MODEX is the premier supply chain and material handling trade show in North America. Produced by MHI, the nation's largest material handling and logistics industry association, MODEX brings together the full spectrum of the supply chain ecosystem every two years. From March 9 to 12, 2026, the Georgia World Congress Center in Atlanta will host approximately 45,000 attendees and over 1,000 exhibitors across more than 400,000 square feet of exhibit space. If your business involves warehousing, distribution, robotics, automation, conveyor systems, packaging, or supply chain software, MODEX is where the industry gathers to see the technology that will define the next wave of operational excellence.

What sets MODEX apart from other logistics events is its emphasis on live demonstrations. This is not a show where you look at static displays and read spec sheets. Exhibitors run conveyors, operate autonomous mobile robots, demonstrate pick-and-place systems, and showcase end-to-end warehouse automation in real time. That experiential element creates a networking dynamic unlike any other trade show: attendees gather around demonstrations, ask questions, and engage in technical discussions that would never happen in a traditional booth setting. These moments of shared curiosity are your best networking opportunities.

Expected attendees at MODEX 2026, including supply chain executives, warehouse operations managers, logistics directors, and manufacturing engineers from every major industry

Show Overview: Dates, Venue, and Key Industries

MODEX 2026 runs March 9 through 12 at the Georgia World Congress Center (GWCC) in downtown Atlanta. The GWCC is one of the largest convention centers in the United States, and MODEX utilizes Buildings B and C, which are connected by skywalks and interior corridors. The show floor is organized by product category, with zones for robotics and automation, conveyor and sortation, packaging, warehouse management systems, material handling equipment, and supply chain software. The adjacent education theaters host over 150 sessions across the four days.

Key industries represented include e-commerce fulfillment, third-party logistics (3PL), food and beverage distribution, pharmaceutical logistics, automotive manufacturing, aerospace, retail distribution, and the rapidly expanding cold chain sector. The attendee profile is heavily operational: VP of operations, directors of distribution, warehouse managers, supply chain engineers, and heads of procurement. These are the people who specify, evaluate, and purchase material handling equipment and software.

Top 5 Networking Strategies for MODEX 2026

1. Use Live Demonstrations as Conversation Starters

The live demos at MODEX are magnets for serious buyers. When you see a crowd gathered around an autonomous mobile robot demonstration or a high-speed sortation system, join the group and observe who is asking the most detailed questions. Those people are actively evaluating solutions. After the demo, introduce yourself with a comment that adds value: share a relevant experience with similar technology, ask a follow-up question that builds on what was demonstrated, or offer a perspective from your industry vertical. The shared experience of watching a demonstration creates an instant conversational bond that cold introductions on the open floor cannot replicate.

2. Prioritize the MHI Industry Night and Opening Reception

MHI hosts the MODEX Industry Night on the first evening of the show, and it is the single most productive networking event of the week. The entire MODEX community compresses into one venue, and the social atmosphere lowers professional barriers. Arrive early, because the first 30 minutes are when attendees are most receptive to new introductions. Have your name badge visible, lead with your name and company, and ask one question: "What are you hoping to find at MODEX this year?" That question immediately reveals whether someone is a potential customer, partner, competitor, or peer, and it gives you the information you need to continue the conversation productively.

3. Target the Education Sessions for C-Suite Access

MODEX's education program features more than 150 seminars led by industry experts, including keynotes from major supply chain executives. The sessions on warehouse automation ROI, labor optimization, and AI in logistics attract the most senior decision-makers. These are the people who approve capital expenditures for the equipment and software on the show floor. Attend the sessions that align with your target audience, sit near the front, and engage during Q&A. Ask a question that demonstrates expertise rather than seeking basic information. After the session, introduce yourself to the speaker and to the attendees who asked the most substantive questions.

4. Work the Startup Pavilion for Innovation Networking

MODEX features a dedicated area for emerging companies and startups that are bringing new technology to the supply chain industry. This area attracts venture capitalists, corporate innovation teams, and technology scouts from major 3PLs and retailers. If you are looking for cutting-edge solutions or investment opportunities, the startup pavilion offers a concentrated pool of founders who are eager to demonstrate their technology and explore partnerships. The conversations here tend to be more technical and more forward-looking than on the main show floor.

5. Leverage the GWCC Layout for Strategic Positioning

The Georgia World Congress Center's layout means that attendees funnel through specific corridors and crossover points between Buildings B and C. The food courts and seating areas near these chokepoints become informal networking zones, especially during lunch hours from 11:30 AM to 1:30 PM. Instead of leaving the venue for lunch, grab food from the on-site vendors and sit in the common areas where thousands of attendees are doing the same thing. The relaxed lunch environment is ideal for introducing yourself to the people sitting near you. A simple "What company are you with?" opens more doors than you might expect when asked in a shared space where everyone is in the same industry.

"At MODEX, the technology does the talking. I have closed more deals watching a customer react to a live robot demo than I have in a hundred PowerPoint presentations." -- VP of Sales, Material Handling Equipment Manufacturer

Key Events, Parties, and Receptions

Beyond the MHI Industry Night, MODEX features several high-value networking opportunities. The MHI Annual Conference sessions, which run concurrently with MODEX, attract C-suite executives and industry thought leaders. The MODEX Welcome Reception on Monday evening is your first chance to connect in a social setting. Throughout the week, major exhibitors host private dinners and receptions at restaurants and venues around downtown Atlanta. Companies like Dematic, Honeywell Intelligrated, and AutoStore typically organize customer appreciation events. If you have a relationship with these companies, request an invitation through your sales contact.

Atlanta's restaurant scene provides excellent options for dinner meetings. The restaurants along Peachtree Street, particularly in the Midtown district, are popular with MODEX attendees. For a more casual atmosphere, the bars and restaurants in the Centennial Park area, within walking distance of the GWCC, fill with supply chain professionals every evening. The food halls at Ponce City Market, a short ride from the convention center, attract groups of attendees looking for a diverse dining experience in a social setting.

VIP and Speaker Networking Tips

MODEX keynote speakers include CEOs of major logistics companies, heads of supply chain at Fortune 500 retailers, and technology leaders driving automation innovation. The supply chain industry is more accessible than many sectors, and senior executives at MODEX tend to be approachable and willing to engage in substantive conversations. Research their companies before the show, identify specific challenges or initiatives they have discussed publicly, and use that knowledge to craft a targeted introduction.

For VIP access to major exhibitors, the private meeting suites located behind the large booth structures are where the most significant conversations happen. These meetings are typically pre-scheduled through the exhibitor's sales team. If you are a qualified buyer or strategic partner, reach out to your contacts at target exhibitors now to secure a meeting slot. The best time slots are on day one and day two, before the pace of the show wears everyone down.

  • Scannly: QR-code-based contact exchange for instant lead capture. In MODEX's demo-heavy environment, you need a tool that lets you exchange information in seconds between demonstrations.
  • LinkedIn: Use #MODEX2026 and #SupplyChain for pre-show outreach and real-time engagement. The supply chain community is highly active on LinkedIn, making it the most effective platform for pre-show connection requests.
  • Voice Memo App: After every significant conversation, record a 30-second voice note with the person's name, company, what you discussed, and the agreed next step. This is faster than typing and more reliable than memory after a 10-hour day on the show floor.

Key Takeaway MODEX's live demonstration environment creates organic networking opportunities that no other supply chain event can match. Position yourself near the demonstrations, engage in the technical discussions, and follow up with the attendees who share your level of interest. The connections forged over a shared reaction to new technology are the most authentic and durable.

Follow-Up Strategy: The Operational Buyer's Timeline

Supply chain purchases are capital expenditure decisions with long evaluation cycles. Your follow-up strategy must reflect this reality. Within 48 hours of meeting a prospect, send a personalized email that references the specific technology or solution you discussed and attach any relevant case studies or ROI analyses. Within one week, enter all contacts into your CRM tagged with "MODEX 2026" and categorize them by buying timeline: immediate (next 90 days), mid-term (6 to 12 months), and long-term (12 to 24 months).

For immediate prospects, propose a site visit or virtual demo within two weeks. For mid-term prospects, add them to a nurture sequence with monthly touchpoints featuring industry content, case studies, and product updates. For long-term prospects, maintain quarterly contact and plan to reconnect at ProMat 2027 in Chicago, MODEX's sister show that alternates years.

The professionals who extract the most value from MODEX are the ones who recognize that the four days in Atlanta are the starting point of a sales cycle, not the endpoint. Supply chain decisions involve multiple stakeholders, site evaluations, pilot programs, and budget approvals. Build your follow-up plan to match the pace at which your prospects actually make decisions, and you will convert MODEX connections into signed contracts long after the Georgia World Congress Center goes quiet.
