Networking Guide

How to Network at KBIS 2026

February 11, 2026 • ShowFloorTips Editorial • 18 min read

Overview: The Kitchen and Bath Industry's Premier Networking Destination

KBIS 2026, the Kitchen & Bath Industry Show, returns to the Orange County Convention Center in Orlando, Florida, from February 17 through 19, anchoring the 13th annual Design & Construction Week alongside the NAHB International Builders' Show. This is the single largest annual gathering of kitchen and bath professionals in North America, and the combined scale of Design & Construction Week makes it one of the most significant events in the entire residential design and construction ecosystem. If you are in the kitchen and bath business, KBIS is where your year begins in earnest.

The numbers tell the story of why KBIS commands your attention. More than 650 KBIS exhibitors will fill approximately 500,000 square feet of the convention center's show floor, and when you factor in the co-located IBS exhibitors, the total climbs past 2,350 companies across 1.2 million square feet. KBIS alone draws more than 43,000 visitors, and the combined Design & Construction Week attendance exceeds 115,000 professionals. These are not casual attendees browsing for ideas. They are kitchen and bath dealers, designers, architects, remodelers, builders, distributors, manufacturers, showroom owners, and the sales professionals who serve them. They represent a $235 billion industry that continues to grow as homeowners invest more aggressively in their kitchens and bathrooms than in any other rooms of the house.

What makes KBIS uniquely valuable for networking is its position at the intersection of design, technology, and construction. You will find cabinetry manufacturers debuting new door styles next to smart appliance companies demonstrating AI-powered cooking systems, luxury faucet brands unveiling matte-finish collections alongside countertop fabricators showcasing new engineered stone colors. The diversity of the exhibitor base means the networking opportunities span every segment of the kitchen and bath value chain. Whether you are a one-person interior design studio looking for new product lines or a national distributor evaluating emerging brands, KBIS concentrates the people you need to meet into three intense, opportunity-rich days in Orlando.

Pre-Show Networking Preparation: Maximizing Your Time Before Orlando

Three days sounds like a lot until you are standing in 500,000 square feet of exhibit space surrounded by 650-plus companies and 43,000 fellow professionals. The kitchen and bath professionals who extract the most value from KBIS are the ones who arrive in Orlando with a plan that was finalized weeks before the convention center doors opened. Here is your comprehensive pre-show preparation strategy.

Define Clear Networking Objectives

The kitchen and bath industry is broad enough that showing up at KBIS with a vague intention to "see what's new" guarantees you will feel overwhelmed by noon on Day 1. Before you register, articulate three to five specific goals. Are you a designer looking for luxury cabinet lines to expand your specification library? A remodeler scouting smart home integration products? A distributor evaluating which emerging countertop brands deserve shelf space? A manufacturer seeking new rep groups or retail partners? A showroom owner researching experiential display concepts? Clear objectives let you filter the massive exhibitor directory into a manageable target list and ensure you allocate your limited floor time to the conversations that matter most.

Study the Floor Plan and Exhibitor Directory

KBIS 2026 is organized across multiple halls and zones, each with its own character and exhibitor mix. The North and South Halls in the convention center house the primary KBIS exhibition, with the NEXTStage presentation area located at booth S28201 in the South Hall. The West Hall features the LUXURY Lounge, which curates high-end brands in an intimate setting designed to facilitate premium conversations. The Discovery District at the Rosen Centre Hotel showcases emerging and innovative brands that may not yet have the booth budgets of the major manufacturers but often present the most exciting new products at the show. Understanding this layout before you arrive lets you plan efficient routes between your priority exhibitors and prevents you from missing entire sections of the show.

Register for the VFTI Conference Sessions

The Voices from the Industry Conference at KBIS 2026 has expanded to seven tracks with more than 75 sessions. The tracks cover Building & Remodeling, Leadership, Design, Kitchen & Bath Trends, Marketing, and two new tracks for 2026: Technology and Sustainability. At $775 for the full conference, VFTI represents a significant investment, but the networking value of these sessions extends far beyond the educational content. The conference rooms are where you will find the industry's most engaged professionals, the ones who invest in their professional development, stay current on trends, and are most likely to become valuable long-term contacts. Choose sessions aligned with your objectives, and treat every session break as a networking opportunity.

Consider the Exclusive Concierge Program

New for KBIS 2026, the Exclusive Concierge Program is available for $1,750 and provides a curated, white-glove experience that includes priority access, personalized show navigation, and exclusive networking opportunities. If your networking objectives involve connecting with senior executives, luxury brand leaders, or high-profile designers, the Concierge Program places you in smaller, more intimate settings where meaningful conversations are easier to initiate and sustain. The program is designed for professionals who value their time at a premium and want to ensure that every hour at KBIS delivers maximum return on investment.

Pre-Schedule Meetings with Priority Contacts

The KBIS event platform and the NKBA's networking tools allow you to identify and reach out to exhibitors and attendees before the show. For your top 10 to 15 targets, send personalized messages requesting meetings during specific time blocks. For contacts you cannot reach through the platform, use LinkedIn to connect directly with the sales managers, brand ambassadors, and executives you want to meet. Reference something specific about their company, a recent product launch, a project you admired, or a design trend you want to discuss. The kitchen and bath industry is relationship-driven, and people respond warmly to outreach that demonstrates genuine interest in their work.

Prepare Your Portfolio and Story

The kitchen and bath industry is intensely visual, and your ability to show your work will dramatically enhance your networking conversations. If you are a designer, load your phone or tablet with your best project photos. If you are a remodeler, have before-and-after images of your recent kitchen and bath projects ready to share. If you are a manufacturer, bring product samples or high-resolution images that demonstrate your quality and design sensibility. Your verbal introduction should be equally polished. Instead of "I'm a kitchen designer from Atlanta," try "I specialize in transitional kitchen design for the luxury custom home market in the Southeast, and I'm looking for European-style cabinetry lines that are not yet widely distributed in my region." Specificity signals professionalism and invites deeper conversation.

Key Takeaway

KBIS spans multiple halls, zones, and a satellite venue at the Rosen Centre Hotel. Study the floor plan before you arrive so you can build efficient routes between your priority targets and avoid missing the Discovery District or the LUXURY Lounge.

Key Networking Events and Opportunities at KBIS 2026

Keynote with Emmitt Smith: Monday, February 17, 8:00-9:00 AM

KBIS 2026 opens with a keynote address from NFL legend and successful businessman Emmitt Smith, who has built a significant career in real estate development and interior design after his Hall of Fame football career. The keynote takes place on Monday morning from 8:00 to 9:00 AM and draws one of the largest single-room gatherings of the entire show. Beyond the motivational content, the keynote is a prime networking opportunity because it concentrates thousands of attendees in one location at the same time, many of whom will linger afterward in the hallways and lobbies. Arrive early to secure a good seat and strike up conversations with the people around you before the session begins. The shared experience of the keynote gives everyone an instant conversation starter for the rest of the day.

Kitchen & Bath Awards and Opening Party: Monday, February 16

The Kitchen & Bath Awards ceremony and Opening Party takes place on Monday, February 16, the evening before the show floor opens. This event celebrates excellence in kitchen and bath design and product innovation, and it draws the industry's top designers, manufacturers, and media. The awards ceremony transitions into a social reception where winners are congratulated, projects are discussed, and the energy of the coming week begins to build. If you can attend only one evening event at KBIS, this should be your priority. The celebratory atmosphere lowers barriers to introduction, and the award-winning projects provide natural conversation topics that can organically lead to business discussions.

NEXTStage Presentations at S28201

The NEXTStage is KBIS's dedicated presentation theater located at booth S28201 in the South Hall. Throughout the three-day show, NEXTStage hosts a rotating schedule of product demonstrations, trend presentations, designer panels, and educational sessions. The audience tends to be smaller and more engaged than the main keynote, which creates an intimate environment where networking feels natural. After each presentation, the speakers typically remain on or near the stage for follow-up conversations. Position yourself near the front so you can approach speakers as they step down, and use the shared content of the presentation as your conversation opener.

KBIS Live Studio and Podcast Studio

KBIS 2026 features both a Live Studio and a Podcast Studio on the show floor where industry leaders are interviewed, trends are debated, and products are reviewed in real time. These studios draw crowds of attentive professionals who are genuinely interested in the topics being discussed, which makes them excellent networking environments. The live format creates energy and excitement, and the moments between tapings or recordings are prime times to approach both the hosts and the audience members who were most engaged. If you are a content creator, designer, or brand representative, inquire about appearing on the live broadcast or podcast to amplify your visibility and create additional networking touchpoints.

Innovation Hour: New for 2026

Innovation Hour is a new addition to KBIS 2026 that spotlights the most cutting-edge products, technologies, and ideas emerging in the kitchen and bath space. This curated experience gathers innovators, early adopters, and industry forward-thinkers into a focused session that rewards attendees who are looking for the next big thing. For networking purposes, Innovation Hour is particularly valuable because it self-selects for the most progressive and entrepreneurial professionals at the show. The people who attend Innovation Hour are typically the ones who are most open to new partnerships, new product lines, and new approaches to their business.

Wine & Design Panels (Sponsored by Fabuwood)

The Wine & Design panels, sponsored by Fabuwood, combine industry discussion with a relaxed social format that includes wine service. These panels feature prominent kitchen and bath designers discussing trends, project challenges, and the creative process in a conversational setting that encourages audience participation. The combination of wine and design conversation creates an atmosphere that is more social than educational, which makes it one of the easiest networking environments at KBIS. Attendees at these panels tend to be design-focused professionals, including interior designers, kitchen and bath designers, architects, and the product representatives who serve them.

Best of KBIS Awards: Wednesday, February 18

The Best of KBIS Awards ceremony on Wednesday, February 18, recognizes the most innovative and impressive products launched at the show. Winning a Best of KBIS award generates significant media attention and buyer interest, which means the winning exhibitors' booths will be buzzing with energy after the announcement. If you are evaluating products, attend the awards to see which companies earned recognition, then visit their booths while the excitement is fresh. If you are an exhibitor, a Best of KBIS award transforms your booth into a networking magnet for the remainder of the show.

The BASH at Mangos: Wednesday, February 18, 8:30 PM

The BASH is KBIS's signature evening social event, and in 2026 it takes place at Mangos Tropical Cafe on Wednesday, February 18, starting at 8:30 PM. This is the single most energetic and social networking event of the entire show. The venue's tropical atmosphere, live entertainment, and flowing drinks create an environment where business conversations happen naturally amid the celebration. The BASH draws exhibitors, designers, builders, media, and industry executives who have spent three days on the show floor and are ready to unwind together. Some of the most productive long-term business relationships in the kitchen and bath industry began with a conversation at The BASH.

SHARK Rep Networking: Thursday, February 19

SHARK Rep Networking on Thursday, February 19, is specifically designed for manufacturers' representatives and the companies they represent. If you are a rep looking for new lines or a manufacturer seeking representation in new territories, this event concentrates your target audience into a single, purpose-built networking session. The format facilitates structured introductions and targeted conversations, making it significantly more efficient than trying to find rep partners through random show floor encounters. Even if you are not directly in the rep business, attending SHARK can connect you with the distribution professionals who influence product placement in showrooms and dealer networks across the country.

Navigating the KBIS Show Floor: Zone-by-Zone Networking Strategy

North and South Halls: The Main Exhibition

The North and South Halls of the Orange County Convention Center house the primary KBIS exhibition, with the majority of the show's 650-plus exhibitors spread across these interconnected spaces. This is where you will find the major cabinetry manufacturers, appliance brands, plumbing fixture companies, countertop fabricators, lighting designers, and hardware suppliers that form the backbone of the kitchen and bath industry. The scale of these halls can feel overwhelming, but the key is to approach them methodically. Start with your priority list and work outward from there. The most productive booth conversations happen when you arrive with specific questions rather than a generic "tell me about your products" approach.

The NEXTStage at S28201 in the South Hall serves as a natural gathering point throughout the day. Use it as your basecamp. Attend presentations that align with your interests, then fan out to nearby exhibitors during breaks. The area around NEXTStage tends to attract attendees who are more engaged and information-hungry than the average badge holder, which makes it a fertile networking zone even when no presentation is in progress.

West Hall: The LUXURY Lounge

The West Hall features the LUXURY Lounge, which curates premium and luxury kitchen and bath brands in a more refined environment. The atmosphere in the LUXURY Lounge is deliberately calmer and more exclusive than the main halls, with wider aisles, more sophisticated booth designs, and a clientele that skews toward high-end designers, architects, and luxury homebuilders. If your business operates in the premium segment, the LUXURY Lounge is where your most valuable networking will happen. The exhibitors in this zone typically have dedicated meeting areas within their booths, and the conversations tend to be longer and more substantive than the rapid-fire exchanges in the busier main halls.

The LUXURY Lounge also attracts media and influencers who cover the design and luxury lifestyle space, making it a productive zone for brands seeking editorial coverage and social media exposure. If you are a manufacturer or designer looking to build your media relationships, spend dedicated time in the West Hall and introduce yourself to the writers, editors, and content creators you see moving through the space.

Discovery District at the Rosen Centre Hotel

The Discovery District is housed at the Rosen Centre Hotel, separate from the main convention center, and it is one of the most underutilized networking opportunities at KBIS. This satellite venue showcases emerging brands, startups, and innovative companies that are often too new or too small for the main exhibition floor. The Discovery District attracts buyers who are actively looking for differentiation, the showroom owners who want products their competitors do not carry, the designers who want to surprise their clients with something unexpected, and the early adopters who pride themselves on being ahead of the trend curve.

Because the Discovery District is physically separated from the main venue, the attendance is lower, which means the exhibitors have more time for you and the conversations tend to be deeper and more personal. Some of the kitchen and bath industry's most successful brands of the past decade made their KBIS debut in the Discovery District. Allocate at least two to three hours to visit this venue and give its exhibitors the same serious attention you give to the major brands in the main halls.

Leveraging Design & Construction Week: Cross-Pollinate Your Network at IBS

KBIS 2026 is co-located with the NAHB International Builders' Show for the 13th consecutive year, creating Design & Construction Week, the largest annual gathering of residential design and construction professionals in the world. This co-location is not just a logistical convenience. It is a strategic networking advantage that multiplies the value of your trip to Orlando by exposing you to the broader ecosystem of professionals who influence, specify, and purchase kitchen and bath products.

The IBS side of Design & Construction Week adds more than 1,700 additional exhibitors and 72,000 attendees to the event, bringing the combined total past 2,350 exhibitors and 115,000 professionals. Many of these IBS attendees, including custom home builders, production builders, remodeling contractors, and residential architects, are direct decision-makers for kitchen and bath product selection. They may not be walking the KBIS floor specifically, but they are in the building, and the shared badge access at Design & Construction Week means you can cross over to the IBS halls and engage with them directly.

If you are a kitchen and bath manufacturer or distributor, the builder and remodeler audiences at IBS represent untapped networking potential. Many of these professionals source their kitchen and bath products through a limited set of relationships, and a direct introduction at Design & Construction Week can open doors that cold calling never would. If you are a designer, connecting with builders at IBS can lead to project partnerships where you are brought in as the kitchen and bath specialist on new construction projects. The cross-pollination between KBIS and IBS is one of the most powerful, and most overlooked, networking advantages of Design & Construction Week.

IBS Closing Concert: Brothers Osborne

Design & Construction Week closes with a concert featuring Brothers Osborne, the Grammy-winning country duo, with tickets available for $65. Beyond the entertainment value, the closing concert creates one last opportunity to connect with the professionals you have been interacting with all week in a relaxed, celebratory setting. The concert draws attendees from both KBIS and IBS, which means it is your best chance for cross-show networking in an informal environment. If you have been meaning to approach someone from the IBS side of the event but have not found the right moment, the closing concert provides it.

Key Takeaway

Design & Construction Week's shared badge system means your KBIS registration gives you access to more than 2,350 exhibitors and 115,000 attendees. Do not limit yourself to the KBIS halls. Cross over to IBS to connect with builders, remodelers, and architects who specify kitchen and bath products.

Day-by-Day Networking Tactics for KBIS 2026

Pre-Show: Monday, February 16 — The Opening Night Advantage

KBIS 2026 officially opens its exhibit hall on February 17, but your networking should begin the evening before. The Kitchen & Bath Awards and Opening Party on February 16 is your first opportunity to connect with the industry's leaders, award winners, and most ambitious professionals who arrive a day early to maximize their KBIS experience. If you are already in Orlando, attend the Opening Party and use the relaxed evening atmosphere to make initial connections that you can deepen on the show floor over the following three days. First impressions made in a social setting are warmer and more memorable than booth introductions, and the people who attend the Opening Party tend to be the most serious and engaged professionals at the show.

Day 1: Tuesday, February 17 — Set Your Foundation

The first official day of KBIS 2026 starts with Emmitt Smith's keynote at 8:00 AM. Arrive early, take a seat near people you do not know, and use the pre-keynote minutes to introduce yourself. After the keynote, spend the first two hours walking the show floor to orient yourself. Locate your priority exhibitors, note which booths are drawing the biggest crowds, and identify the zones where you want to spend the most time. Execute your pre-scheduled meetings in the mid-morning and early afternoon when energy levels are highest. Attend one or two VFTI sessions in the afternoon, choosing topics that align with your networking objectives. Visit the NEXTStage for a presentation that interests you and linger afterward to meet fellow attendees. End the day by reviewing your contacts and notes over dinner.

Day 2: Wednesday, February 18 — Go Deep and Celebrate

Wednesday is the highest-energy day at KBIS because it combines full show floor activity with the Best of KBIS Awards and The BASH in the evening. Use the morning for your most important booth conversations, the ones where you need 20 to 30 minutes of uninterrupted attention from a brand representative or executive. Attend the Innovation Hour to discover the most forward-thinking products and meet the entrepreneurs behind them. Visit the Wine & Design panels for networking in a relaxed, design-focused setting. Check the Best of KBIS Awards results and visit the winning booths while they are celebrating. In the evening, attend The BASH at Mangos. Arrive on time, do not cluster with people you already know, and commit to meeting at least five new people during the event. The BASH is where formal business relationships often evolve into genuine professional friendships.

Day 3: Thursday, February 19 — Close, Connect, and Follow Up

The final day of KBIS typically has lower attendance, and the experienced networkers know this is an advantage. Exhibitors who were too busy for extended conversations on Tuesday and Wednesday now have time to sit down and talk in depth. Use Thursday morning for your most important follow-up conversations. Return to the booths where you had promising exchanges earlier in the week and advance those conversations toward concrete next steps, whether that means a sample order, a showroom visit, a rep agreement, or a design collaboration. Attend the SHARK Rep Networking session if you are in the manufacturer's rep business. Visit the Discovery District if you have not yet, as the lower-traffic environment on Thursday makes it ideal for extended conversations with emerging brands. Before you leave the convention center, spend 20 minutes organizing your contacts by priority and drafting quick follow-up notes while the conversations are still fresh.

Best Networking Spots Inside and Outside the Venue

The LUXURY Lounge in the West Hall

The LUXURY Lounge is designed for longer, more substantive conversations than the main exhibit halls. The premium environment attracts high-end designers, architects, and luxury brand representatives who have the time and inclination for deeper discussions. If you are in the luxury segment of the kitchen and bath market, the LUXURY Lounge should be your primary networking base. The lower foot traffic compared to the North and South Halls means you can have unhurried conversations without competing with the noise and energy of the main show floor.

The NEXTStage Area at S28201

The NEXTStage functions as a natural gathering point in the South Hall. Between presentations, the seating area attracts attendees who are taking a break from the floor and are open to conversation. After presentations, both the speakers and the audience members tend to linger, creating a concentrated networking environment around shared content. Position yourself in the NEXTStage area during transitions between sessions to catch the incoming and outgoing audiences simultaneously.

VFTI Conference Hallways and Breakout Spaces

The corridors and breakout areas adjacent to the VFTI Conference rooms are among the most productive networking locations at KBIS. Conference attendees have paid $775 for the educational content, which means they are invested, serious, and engaged professionals. The hallway conversations between sessions, during coffee breaks, and over lunch are often where the most candid industry discussions happen. Topics covered inside the sessions, from sustainability strategies to technology adoption to kitchen and bath design trends, provide instant conversation starters that make approaching strangers feel natural.

The Discovery District at the Rosen Centre Hotel

Because the Discovery District is separated from the convention center, it creates its own micro-community of innovative brands and adventurous buyers. The atmosphere is more entrepreneurial and experimental than the main show floor, and the conversations tend to be more intimate. The Rosen Centre's lobby and common areas become informal networking lounges where Discovery District exhibitors and attendees gather between booth visits, creating spontaneous connection opportunities that rarely happen in the cavernous convention center halls.

The Orange County Convention Center Concourse and Dining Areas

The OCCC's main concourses and food court areas serve as transit zones where thousands of attendees pass through multiple times per day. The seating areas near food vendors become impromptu meeting spots, especially during the lunch hours. Many experienced KBIS networkers schedule informal catch-ups in these central locations because they are easy to find and provide a neutral space away from any exhibitor's booth. The coffee stations scattered throughout the convention center are also reliable conversation starters. The simple act of waiting in a coffee line next to someone wearing a KBIS badge creates a natural opening for introduction.

International Drive and Orlando Dining

The Orange County Convention Center is located on International Drive, one of Orlando's most active restaurant and entertainment corridors. Restaurants along I-Drive and in the surrounding area attract the KBIS crowd after the show floor closes. The hotel bars at the Rosen Centre, the Rosen Plaza, the Hilton Orlando, and other nearby properties become unofficial KBIS networking venues each evening. The warm Orlando weather in February means outdoor dining and poolside receptions are common, creating relaxed environments where business conversations flow naturally into the evening hours. Make dinner reservations in advance at popular spots and invite contacts you met during the day to join you.

Key Takeaway

Do not underestimate the Discovery District at the Rosen Centre Hotel. The satellite venue's intimate atmosphere and emerging brand focus make it one of the highest-value networking environments at KBIS for professionals seeking differentiation and early access to innovation.

The attendees in the Leadership track tend to think strategically and long-term, which makes them ideal partners for meaningful business relationships.

The Design track is the creative heart of VFTI, attracting interior designers, kitchen and bath designers, architects, and the product representatives who serve them. Sessions cover design trends, color and material forecasting, client management, and portfolio development. This track attracts the industry's early adopters and technology leaders, which makes it a fertile networking environment for technology companies seeking kitchen and bath industry partnerships and for traditional industry professionals who want to connect with the innovators who are reshaping how kitchens and bathrooms are designed, sold, and installed.

Sustainability Track (New for 2026)

Also new for 2026, the Sustainability track addresses the growing demand for environmentally responsible kitchen and bath products, practices, and business models. Sessions cover sustainable materials, water conservation, energy-efficient appliances, and green certification programs. Sustainability is increasingly a purchase driver for homeowners, and the professionals who attend this track are positioning their businesses to serve that demand. Conversation Starters That Work at KBIS

The kitchen and bath industry runs on relationships, and relationships begin with conversations. Here are approaches tailored to the specific environments you will encounter at KBIS 2026.

At an exhibitor booth: "I have a client project coming up that calls for a European-style frameless cabinet with integrated lighting. Can you walk me through how your line handles the electrical integration? I want to understand what my installer needs to know before I specify it." This signals that you are a real buyer with a real project, which earns immediate attention and a detailed response from any exhibitor.

During a VFTI session break: "That session on sustainable countertop materials was eye-opening. Are you seeing client demand for recycled content in your market, or is it still mostly designer-driven? I am trying to figure out when sustainability becomes a selling point rather than a cost premium." This is specific enough to attract a substantive response and open enough to spark a genuine exchange of market intelligence.

At the Wine & Design panel: "What is the one design trend from this year's show that you think has the most staying power? I am always skeptical about trend predictions, but I am curious what other designers are actually seeing in their project pipelines." Inviting a design opinion is the universal language of the kitchen and bath design community and almost always leads to an extended conversation.

In the LUXURY Lounge: "The finish quality on this hardware collection is outstanding. Are you working with any designers in the Southeast who are specifying this line? I have been looking for a premium hardware partner that is not yet oversaturated in my market." This approach combines product knowledge with a clear business need, which positions you as a serious professional worth investing time in.

At The BASH: "What was the highlight of your KBIS this year? Mine was discovering a countertop brand in the Discovery District that nobody in my region is carrying yet. I love finding the hidden gems at this show." Sharing your own enthusiasm invites reciprocity and creates a positive, energetic conversation that matches the atmosphere of the event.

At the Discovery District: "How did you hear about the Discovery District? I make a point of coming here every year because some of the best finds are the brands that have not hit the mainstream yet. What has caught your eye so far?" This approach positions you as an experienced KBIS attendee and creates an opportunity to share recommendations back and forth.

Post-KBIS Follow-Up Strategy

The kitchen and bath industry is built on long-term relationships, and the contacts you make at KBIS have value that extends far beyond any single transaction. Your follow-up strategy should reflect both the immediate opportunities and the long-term relationship potential of this deeply interconnected industry.

During the show (every evening): Spend 15 to 20 minutes each evening reviewing the contacts you made that day. For each significant connection, record what you discussed, what products or projects were referenced, what their specific needs or challenges are, and any next step you agreed to. If you are using Scannly, your contacts are already organized with tags and notes. Review them, add any details you forgot to capture in the moment, and prioritize your follow-up list. This nightly discipline is what separates the professionals who convert KBIS contacts into business from those who return home with a box of business cards and good intentions.

Within 48 hours of the show closing: Send personalized follow-up messages to your highest-priority contacts. The kitchen and bath industry is small enough that generic mass emails are immediately recognized and discarded. Reference something specific from your conversation: "It was great discussing your new matte black faucet collection at the LUXURY Lounge on Wednesday. The wall-mount option you showed me would be perfect for a master bath project I am starting next month. Can we schedule a call to discuss dealer pricing and lead times?" This level of specificity demonstrates that the conversation mattered to you and invites a genuine continuation of the relationship.

Within two weeks: For high-priority contacts, propose a concrete next step. Request product samples, schedule a showroom visit, arrange a video call to review project specifications, send a portfolio of relevant projects, or make an introduction to a colleague in your network who would benefit from their product or service. The kitchen and bath community values generosity and follow-through. People who connect others and share opportunities become the trusted nodes in the industry's professional network.

Ongoing quarterly engagement: The kitchen and bath industry is cyclical, with spring and fall being the peak seasons for project starts and product orders. Maintain visibility with your KBIS contacts by sharing relevant content quarterly: a new project completion, a trend article, an NKBA chapter event, or simply a check-in message asking how their spring season is shaping up. Comment on their LinkedIn posts when they share project photos or product launches. The kitchen and bath industry has a long memory and a strong culture of reciprocity. The relationships you nurture consistently are the ones that produce referrals, partnerships, and opportunities year after year.
