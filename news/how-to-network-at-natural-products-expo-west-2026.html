How to Network at Natural Products Expo West 2026

ShowFloorTips Editorial February 11, 2026

Overview: The Natural Products Industry's Biggest Week

Natural Products Expo West returns to the Anaheim Convention Center from March 3 through 7, 2026, once again claiming its title as the largest natural, organic, and healthy products trade show in the world. With more than 65,000 attendees and over 3,000 exhibiting companies expected across the main halls, the Hilton Anaheim, and the Marriott, this five-day event is the beating heart of the natural products industry.

Expo West is where retailers discover the brands that will fill their shelves next year, where founders meet the investors who will fuel their growth, and where ingredient suppliers connect with the product developers who need them. But the sheer scale of the show can be overwhelming. Without a networking plan, you will spend five days sampling snacks and collecting tote bags without building a single meaningful relationship. This guide will help you cut through the noise and leave Anaheim with connections that actually move your business forward.

Key Networking Events at Expo West 2026

Education Sessions and Keynotes (March 3-4). The first two days of Expo West are dedicated to education before the main exhibit halls open. These sessions, covering topics from regenerative agriculture to retail trends, draw a more focused crowd of industry leaders. The smaller room sizes make it far easier to introduce yourself to speakers and fellow attendees. Arrive early, sit near the front, and stay after the session ends when speakers are most accessible.

NEXT Pavilion and Pitch Slam. Located in Hall E, the NEXT area showcases emerging brands and innovative products. The NEXT Pitch Slam competition draws a crowd of investors, brokers, and retail buyers specifically looking for the next breakout brand. Whether you are pitching or watching, this is one of the densest concentrations of decision-makers at the entire show and a prime networking environment.

New Hope Network Receptions. New Hope Network, the organizer of Expo West, hosts several official receptions throughout the week. The All-Industry Welcome Reception on Tuesday evening is the best early networking opportunity, setting the tone before the exhibit halls open on Wednesday. These events tend to be crowded, so arrive within the first 30 minutes while the energy is high and the bar lines are short.

Brand-Hosted Dinners and Parties. Larger brands like Organic Valley, Whole Foods Market, UNFI, and KeHE host private dinners and events across Anaheim during the week. These are often the most productive networking settings because the guest lists are curated and the pace is relaxed. To get invited, start engaging with contacts at these companies on LinkedIn and through email at least three weeks before the show. A specific, personalized note explaining what you would like to discuss goes a long way.

Sunrise Yoga and Wellness Events. Expo West leans into the wellness culture of its industry. Morning yoga sessions, group runs, and wellness meetups are organized throughout the week, often in the outdoor spaces around the convention center. These might sound informal, but they attract health-focused founders and executives, and the shared experience creates an immediate bond that translates naturally into business conversations over breakfast afterward.

Best Networking Spots Inside and Outside the Venue

The Bridge Between the Convention Center and Hilton Anaheim. This enclosed walkway is one of the most heavily trafficked paths at Expo West, and people naturally slow down as they cross. It is an excellent place to reconnect with someone you recognize or strike up a conversation with a fellow attendee. The foot traffic creates a natural flow that prevents the awkwardness of lingering.

Hot Products Area in Hall E. The curated Hot Products section draws a stream of buyers and media specifically seeking new innovations. The exhibitors here tend to be smaller and more approachable, and the visitors are in discovery mode, making conversations more open and organic than in the packed main halls.

Outdoor Areas Around the Convention Center. The Southern California weather in early March is typically mild, and the outdoor courtyards and plazas around the Anaheim Convention Center fill with attendees taking breaks. The relaxed vibe of sitting on a bench in the sunshine makes it easy to start a conversation with whoever sits down nearby.

The Anaheim Packing District. Located about ten minutes from the convention center, this food hall and entertainment complex has become a popular after-hours destination for Expo West attendees. The craft cocktail bars and artisanal food stalls attract an industry crowd without feeling like an extension of the trade show. It is an excellent place for more personal, less transactional networking.

Hotel Lobbies at the Hilton and Marriott. Both hotels are connected to the convention center and serve as unofficial meeting grounds. The lobby of the Hilton Anaheim, in particular, becomes a secondary trade show in its own right, with exhibitors and buyers meeting in every available chair. Grab a seat with a clear view of the entrance and you will spot familiar faces all day.

Conversation Starters That Work in the Natural Products World

The natural products industry has a unique culture. People are passionate about their missions, their ingredients, and their impact. Tap into that passion with openers that go beyond the transactional.

At a booth: "What is the origin story behind this product? I am always interested in what drives founders in this space." People in natural products are almost always mission-driven, and asking about their story signals that you value more than just the transaction.

In a session: "I am curious how you are seeing [trend from the session] play out on actual store shelves. Are retailers really moving on this yet?" This positions you as someone who thinks practically, which is refreshing in an industry that can lean heavily toward aspiration.

At a party or dinner: "What has been the most surprising product or brand you have discovered at the show so far?" This is easy to answer, sparks enthusiasm, and often leads to mutual recommendations that can anchor a longer conversation.

With fellow attendees in line: "Is this your first Expo West? Any must-see booths you would recommend?" Simple, friendly, and it opens the door for them to share what excites them, which tells you a lot about their priorities and role.

Your Post-Expo West Follow-Up Strategy

Expo West is sensory overload. Five days of tastings, demos, and conversations blend together fast. A structured follow-up plan is the difference between a productive week and a pile of forgotten business cards.

Each evening during the show: Spend 15 minutes reviewing the contacts you made that day. Categorize them into three tiers: high-priority (schedule a call within two weeks), medium-priority (send a personalized email), and low-priority (connect on LinkedIn with a brief note). Within 48 hours of the show closing: Send personalized follow-up messages to your high-priority contacts. Reference your specific conversation: "It was great talking about your expansion into the club channel at the UNFI dinner on Thursday. I have some data on club-format packaging that might be useful. Can we set up a 15-minute call next week?"

Within one week: Email your medium-priority contacts with a brief, personal note. Share a relevant resource or article to add value rather than simply saying "nice to meet you."

Within one month: Review your full contact list and identify any connections that could benefit from an introduction to someone else in your network. Making introductions is one of the most effective ways to deepen new relationships because it demonstrates generosity and positions you as a connector in the industry.

Expo West 2026 is the natural products industry's most important week of the year. Come prepared with a plan, focus on quality conversations over quantity, and follow up with intention. The relationships you build in Anaheim this March could define your next product launch, retail placement, or strategic partnership.
