PACK EXPO International vs ProMat 2026: Packaging Meets Material Handling in Chicago's McCormick Place

Chicago's McCormick Place plays host to two of the most significant industrial trade shows in North America: PACK EXPO International and ProMat. Both are massive. Both attract tens of thousands of professionals. Both occupy the same convention center on the same lakefront. And yet these two shows serve fundamentally different industries, draw different buyer profiles, and operate on different business models. If your company touches packaging, material handling, automation, or supply chain operations, understanding how PACK EXPO International and ProMat compare is essential for allocating your trade show budget wisely.

This comparison breaks down every meaningful dimension of both shows, from audience and technology focus to registration costs and ROI profiles, so you can make an informed decision about where to invest your time and money in the coming trade show cycle.

Industry Focus: Packaging vs Material Handling

The core distinction between PACK EXPO International and ProMat is the point in the supply chain where each show concentrates its attention. PACK EXPO is about what goes into the package, how the package gets made, and how the product is processed before it reaches the package. ProMat is about what happens after the package is sealed: how it gets stored, moved, sorted, shipped, and delivered.

PACK EXPO International is the largest packaging and processing trade show in the world. Organized by PMMI, the show covers the entire packaging value chain, from raw materials and container manufacturing to filling, sealing, labeling, coding, inspection, and end-of-line palletizing. Walk the show floor and you will see shrink-wrapping machines running at full speed, robotic case packers assembling corrugated shippers, pharmaceutical blister pack lines operating under clean-room conditions, and flexible packaging converting equipment producing finished pouches. The show is visceral in a way that few trade shows can match because so much of the equipment is running live, producing real output at production speeds.

ProMat covers the material handling and supply chain ecosystem. Organized by MHI, the show brings together manufacturers of conveyors, automated storage and retrieval systems, autonomous mobile robots, warehouse management software, sortation systems, dock equipment, and everything else required to move goods through distribution networks. The perspective is broader in terms of supply chain scope but narrower in terms of the specific operation: ProMat is fundamentally about moving things from point A to point B, whether that means across a warehouse floor or across the country.

The Overlap Zone

The two shows share significant overlap in automation, robotics, and conveyor technology. Companies like FANUC, ABB, KUKA, and Yaskawa exhibit at both shows, demonstrating robotic palletizing and case packing at PACK EXPO and robotic picking and goods-to-person systems at ProMat. If your primary interest is industrial robotics or automation platforms, you will find relevant content at either event, though the application context differs substantially.

Size and Scale at McCormick Place

Both shows occupy McCormick Place, the largest convention center in North America, but they use it differently. PACK EXPO International is the larger show by exhibitor count, with more than 2,500 exhibitors compared to ProMat's 1,100-plus. This reflects the fragmented nature of the packaging industry, where dozens of companies compete in each equipment category and the range of technologies spans from simple hand-held heat sealers to multi-million-dollar integrated packaging lines.

ProMat, meanwhile, draws more attendees, with over 50,000 visitors compared to PACK EXPO's 44,000-plus. The free registration model is a significant factor here, removing the cost barrier that keeps some professionals from attending paid shows. ProMat 2025 expanded into the new Lakeside Hall at McCormick Place to accommodate growing demand, a signal that the show continues to gain momentum.

For exhibitors evaluating both shows, the math is straightforward: PACK EXPO puts you in a larger competitive field where differentiation matters more, while ProMat gives you access to a larger audience with a lower barrier to entry. Both shows fill McCormick Place to a scale that requires multiple days to walk thoroughly.

Registration and Cost: A Major Differentiator

This is where the two shows diverge most sharply from an attendee perspective. ProMat offers completely free registration, including access to all 200-plus education sessions, keynote presentations, and the full show floor. This is one of the most generous attendee value propositions in the trade show industry. MHI funds the event primarily through exhibitor fees, which means the attendee experience is essentially subsidized by the companies on the show floor.

PACK EXPO International charges registration fees that vary by badge type and timing. Early registration discounts are available, and PMMI members receive preferential pricing, but the bottom line is that attending PACK EXPO requires a financial commitment beyond travel and lodging. For individual attendees, this cost is modest. For companies sending teams of five or ten, it adds up and must be factored into the ROI calculation.

Budget Planning Tip

If you are evaluating both shows and budget is a constraint, consider sending a larger team to ProMat (where registration is free and the only costs are travel and lodging) and a smaller, more targeted team to PACK EXPO International (where registration fees make it more economical to send fewer people with specific objectives). This approach maximizes coverage across both events without doubling your per-person costs.

Audience: Who Attends Each Show

The audience profiles of PACK EXPO International and ProMat reflect their respective industries, and understanding who walks each show floor is critical for exhibitors evaluating lead quality.

PACK EXPO International Attendees

The PACK EXPO audience is dominated by professionals from consumer packaged goods companies, food and beverage manufacturers, pharmaceutical and healthcare companies, cosmetics and personal care brands, and contract packaging organizations. These are the people who specify, evaluate, and purchase packaging machinery, processing equipment, packaging materials, and related services. You will find packaging engineers, plant managers, operations directors, quality assurance professionals, R&D teams evaluating new packaging formats, and procurement specialists comparing capital equipment options. The Healthcare Packaging Pavilion, which occupies 70,000 square feet of dedicated floor space, draws a concentrated audience of pharmaceutical and medical device packaging professionals who may not attend general material handling shows.

ProMat Attendees

ProMat draws from the warehousing, distribution, logistics, and manufacturing operations community. The audience includes warehouse managers, supply chain directors, logistics coordinators, 3PL operators, e-commerce fulfillment specialists, distribution center planners, and manufacturing operations teams evaluating material flow solutions. ProMat 2025 featured keynotes from Paul Zikopoulos of IBM on AI and data strategy, Azeem Azhar of Bloomberg on exponential technology trends, and Deion Sanders on leadership and performance, reflecting the show's ambition to connect supply chain professionals with broader business and technology themes.

The key distinction for exhibitors: if you sell to people who put products into packages, exhibit at PACK EXPO. If you sell to people who move packages through warehouses and distribution networks, exhibit at ProMat. If you sell automation technology that serves both functions, you need to evaluate which audience generates higher-value leads for your specific solution.

Education and Content

Both shows invest heavily in educational programming, recognizing that attendees increasingly value learning opportunities alongside the exhibition floor.

PACK EXPO International features the Innovation Stage, a free-access presentation area on the show floor where exhibitors and industry experts deliver focused sessions on emerging packaging technologies, sustainability trends, regulatory changes, and operational best practices. The format emphasizes short, practical presentations that attendees can fit between booth visits. PACK EXPO also integrates educational content through the PMMI Media Group's publications and digital channels, extending the learning experience beyond the show dates.

ProMat's education program is one of its strongest differentiators. With more than 200 free seminars organized across multiple tracks covering automation, robotics, software, sustainability, workforce development, and supply chain strategy, ProMat offers a conference-caliber education program at no additional cost. The keynote program attracts high-profile speakers who draw large audiences and set the intellectual tone for the entire event. The fact that all of this education is included in the free registration makes ProMat one of the highest-value educational experiences in the industrial trade show calendar.

Technology Overlap: Robotics, Automation, and Conveyors

Despite their different industry orientations, PACK EXPO International and ProMat share a substantial technology overlap zone centered on robotics, automation, and conveyor systems. This overlap has grown significantly in recent years as the lines between packaging operations and distribution operations blur.

Robotic palletizing, for example, is a core technology at PACK EXPO, where it appears at the end of packaging lines. The same robotic platforms appear at ProMat, but configured for depalletizing, order picking, or goods-to-person applications in warehouse environments. Conveyor manufacturers exhibit at both shows: at PACK EXPO they demonstrate product handling and accumulation conveyors that feed packaging machines, while at ProMat they showcase sortation conveyors, merge systems, and high-speed distribution conveyors.

Vision systems, sensors, controls platforms, and software integration tools are equally relevant at both events. A company selling machine vision inspection systems might demonstrate quality control applications at PACK EXPO and barcode scanning or dimensioning applications at ProMat. The underlying technology is identical; the application context shifts.

For technology companies that sell into both packaging and material handling markets, exhibiting at both shows provides access to complementary audiences. The packaging engineer evaluating a robotic case packer at PACK EXPO is a different buyer than the warehouse operations manager evaluating a robotic each-pick system at ProMat, even though both may purchase from the same robotics integrator.

ROI Comparison: Where Your Dollar Goes Further

The ROI equation for each show depends on your industry, your sales cycle, and your definition of a qualified lead.

PACK EXPO International ROI

PACK EXPO delivers ROI through high-intent buyer interactions. The registration fee, while modest, acts as a natural filter: the people who pay to attend PACK EXPO are serious about evaluating packaging and processing solutions. The live equipment demonstrations create a selling environment where prospects can see, hear, and evaluate machinery in a way that no catalog or video can replicate. For capital equipment manufacturers, a single qualified lead at PACK EXPO can represent a deal worth $250,000 to several million dollars, with the show serving as the starting point for an evaluation process that may take 6 to 18 months.

The concentration of CPG, food and beverage, and pharmaceutical buyers is unmatched at any other event. If your solution serves these industries, the cost of exhibiting at PACK EXPO is justified by the quality and specificity of the audience.

ProMat ROI

ProMat's free registration model creates a different ROI dynamic. The larger attendee base means more foot traffic and more lead volume, but the absence of a registration fee also means the audience includes a broader spectrum of professionals, from senior decision-makers to junior staff members attending for education. Lead qualification on the show floor becomes more important at ProMat because the funnel is wider at the top.

That said, the supply chain technology market is enormous and growing rapidly, driven by e-commerce growth, labor shortages, and the push toward warehouse automation. Deals generated from ProMat leads in areas like automated storage and retrieval, warehouse management systems, and autonomous mobile robots can easily reach seven figures. The free education sessions also provide exhibitors with thought leadership opportunities that build credibility and shorten future sales cycles.

Companies That Should Attend Both

For a specific set of companies, attending both PACK EXPO International and ProMat is not a luxury but a strategic necessity. If your business falls into any of the following categories, you should seriously evaluate a dual-show strategy:

  • Robotics and automation integrators who sell into both packaging lines and warehouse operations. Your technology serves both audiences, and the application stories differ enough to require separate booth messaging at each show.
  • Conveyor and material handling equipment manufacturers whose products appear in both packaging facilities and distribution centers. The buyer profiles are different, the application requirements are different, and the competitive landscape shifts between shows.
  • Controls, vision, and sensor companies whose platforms serve inspection, quality control, sorting, and identification applications across both packaging and logistics environments.
  • Software companies offering solutions that span production, packaging, and warehouse management. ERP vendors, MES providers, and IIoT platforms are increasingly relevant at both events as manufacturers seek end-to-end visibility.
  • Contract packaging and 3PL organizations that must stay current on both packaging technology innovations and distribution infrastructure developments to serve their clients effectively.
Timing Advantage

Because PACK EXPO International (October 2026) and ProMat (April 2027) are separated by approximately six months, companies attending both can spread their trade show investment across two fiscal periods. This scheduling gap also provides time to refine messaging and booth strategy based on lessons learned at the first show before executing at the second.

Decision Guide: Which Show Is Right for Your Company

Choose PACK EXPO International If...

  • Your primary customers are in CPG, food and beverage, pharmaceutical, or personal care manufacturing.
  • You sell packaging machinery, processing equipment, packaging materials, or related services.
  • You need to demonstrate equipment running at production speeds to close deals.
  • The Healthcare Packaging Pavilion is relevant to your product line.
  • Your sales cycle depends on buyers seeing and evaluating physical equipment in person.
  • You want access to the largest concentration of packaging decision-makers in the world.

Choose ProMat If...

  • Your primary customers are in warehousing, distribution, logistics, e-commerce fulfillment, or manufacturing operations.
  • You sell material handling equipment, warehouse automation, supply chain software, or logistics technology.
  • Free attendee registration is important for driving booth traffic and maximizing lead volume.
  • You want to leverage the 200-plus free education sessions for thought leadership positioning.
  • Your solution addresses the supply chain challenges of labor shortages, e-commerce growth, or distribution optimization.
  • You want access to 50,000-plus supply chain professionals under one roof.

Attend Both If...

  • Your technology serves both packaging and material handling applications.
  • You are a robotics, automation, conveyor, or controls company with cross-industry relevance.
  • Your total addressable market spans from production floor to distribution center.
  • You can justify the investment with distinct lead profiles from each show.

Verdict

PACK EXPO International and ProMat are not competitors. They are complementary shows that serve adjacent industries from the same world-class venue. PACK EXPO is the definitive event for packaging and processing, period. If you sell to the people who package products, there is no substitute. ProMat is the definitive event for material handling and supply chain, period. If you sell to the people who move, store, and ship those packaged products, there is no substitute there either.

The real strategic question is not which show is better. It is whether your business operates in the overlap zone where both shows deliver unique value. The growth of automation, robotics, and integrated supply chain software is pulling these two worlds closer together every year. The companies that recognize this convergence and position themselves at both events will capture opportunities that their single-show competitors miss.

If budget forces a choice, let your customer profile decide. Follow the buyers. If your pipeline is filled with packaging engineers and plant managers at food companies, PACK EXPO International in October 2026 is where you need to be. If your pipeline depends on warehouse operations managers and supply chain directors at 3PLs and distribution companies, plan for ProMat in April 2027. And if your best customers exist in both worlds, start planning for both shows now, because the best booth positions at McCormick Place go to the companies that commit early.
