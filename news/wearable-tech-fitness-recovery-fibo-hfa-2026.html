Sports & Fitness

Wearables Hold the No. 1 Spot for the 10th Year as $278B Fitness Industry Pivots to Recovery and Community — FIBO, HFA Show, and Olympia 2026

ShowFloorTips Editorial · February 11, 2026

For the 20th year, the American College of Sports Medicine has published its annual Worldwide Fitness Trends forecast, and the results confirm what the fitness technology industry has known for a decade: wearable technology is the undisputed number one trend. Fitness trackers, smartwatches, heart rate monitors, and GPS devices have held the top spot since 2016, and 2026's edition underscores how advanced biosensors — capturing fall detection, heart rhythm, blood pressure, blood glucose, and skin temperature — are expanding the category far beyond step counting. With the global fitness industry projected to reach $278 billion in 2026 at a 7.9 percent annual growth rate, the stakes for exhibitors at FIBO (April 16–19, Cologne), The HFA Show (March 16–18, San Diego), and the Olympia Expo have never been higher.

$278B
Projected global fitness industry value in 2026, growing at 7.9% annually

Wearables: From Step Counters to Health Platforms

The continued dominance of wearable technology in the ACSM rankings masks a fundamental transformation in what "wearable" means. A decade ago, fitness trackers counted steps and estimated calories. In 2026, the latest devices function as comprehensive health monitoring platforms: continuous glucose monitors integrate with fitness apps, advanced ECG sensors detect atrial fibrillation during workouts, and skin temperature tracking provides early illness detection. The data these devices generate is increasingly being used by gyms and studios to personalize training programs, adjust class intensity in real time, and provide members with medical-grade health insights.

For exhibitors at fitness trade shows, the wearable trend creates opportunities across the entire ecosystem: device manufacturers, software platforms that integrate wearable data into gym management systems, content providers building personalized workout experiences, and health professionals developing clinical programs based on wearable insights.

Adult Recreation and Sport Clubs: A New Entry

In a notable development, Adult Recreation and Sport Clubs made their first appearance on the ACSM trends list in 2026. The category reflects the explosive growth of pickleball, adult sports leagues, and social fitness communities. This is not a niche trend — it represents a fundamental shift in how people think about exercise. The desire for social connection while exercising is driving membership growth in sport club formats that traditional gym operators are racing to incorporate.

For exhibitors selling court equipment, league management software, social fitness platforms, or facility design services, this new entry on the ACSM list is a validation that the market is real and growing. Trade shows in 2026 will be the primary venue for reaching facility operators who are investing in recreation and sport amenities.

Key InsightAdults 65 and older now visit gyms and studios more often than any other age group, according to IHRSA data. This demographic shift has enormous implications for equipment design, programming, facility accessibility, and marketing. Exhibitors who address the active aging market will find a receptive audience at every fitness trade show in 2026.

The Recovery Revolution

The wellness trends for 2026 make one thing unmistakably clear: recovery is no longer a luxury — it is an essential part of an active lifestyle. Active regeneration has taken center stage, with saunas, ice baths, cryotherapy, massage guns, lymphatic drainage devices, and compression therapy becoming standard offerings at premium fitness facilities. The recovery market is creating entirely new revenue streams for gym operators and entirely new product categories for exhibitors.

FIBO 2026, which attracted over 154,000 visitors from 129 countries in its 2025 edition, will feature recovery prominently in its exhibition and education programming. For exhibitors selling recovery equipment, wellness technology, or facility design services, FIBO's scale and international audience provide unmatched exposure. The first two days (April 16–17) are trade-only, creating a focused environment for B2B conversations before the venue opens to the public on the weekend.

"The wellness trends for 2026 clearly show that it is less about pure performance and more about experiences, community, and sustainable well-being. Recovery is not a luxury but an essential part of an active lifestyle."

-- Fitnessmarkt, Wellness 2026 Trends Report

The 2026 Fitness Trade Show Calendar

  • The HFA Show (March 16–18, San Diego): Formerly IHRSA, The HFA Show is the primary U.S. trade event for health club and studio professionals. With 10,000+ attendees expected from over 80 countries, it serves as the industry's annual business meeting. Best for equipment manufacturers, technology providers, facility design firms, and service companies targeting the U.S. and international club market.
  • FIBO (April 16–19, Cologne): The world's largest fitness expo. Over 1,200 exhibitors and 145,000+ visitors from 133 countries. The first two trade-only days create a focused B2B environment, while the weekend public days generate consumer awareness and media coverage. FIBO's Future Forum specifically addresses AI in wellness, making it relevant for technology exhibitors.
  • Olympia Expo: Held alongside the iconic Mr. Olympia competition, the Olympia Expo attracts the sports nutrition, supplements, and bodybuilding equipment market. The audience skews toward dedicated fitness enthusiasts, supplement brands, and athletic apparel companies.

The Retention Challenge

Despite industry growth, the retention challenge persists. The average annual retention rate for health clubs is approximately 71.4 percent, meaning about 28.6 percent of members leave each year. For exhibitors selling member engagement platforms, retention analytics, or communication tools, this statistic is both a problem statement and a market opportunity. Every gym operator at The HFA Show and FIBO is looking for solutions that reduce churn and increase lifetime member value.

Exhibitor Strategies for Fitness Trade Shows in 2026

  1. Integrate wearable data into your story. If your product or service can receive, analyze, or act on data from fitness wearables, lead with that capability. Gym operators want to see how wearable integration enhances the member experience and creates data-driven programming opportunities.
  2. Showcase recovery solutions prominently. Recovery is the fastest-growing category in fitness facility investment. If you sell recovery equipment, wellness technology, or facility design services, give this category premium positioning in your booth layout and marketing materials.
  3. Address the social fitness demand. Adult recreation clubs, pickleball courts, and social fitness programming are driving new membership growth. Exhibitors should demonstrate how their products support community-building, league management, and social engagement within fitness facilities.
  4. Prepare for the active aging conversation. With adults 65+ now the most frequent gym visitors, exhibitors should show how their products serve this demographic. Equipment accessibility, programming flexibility, health monitoring integration, and fall prevention features are all purchase drivers for operators investing in the senior fitness market.
  5. Maximize international reach at FIBO. With visitors from 133 countries, FIBO is a genuinely global event. Prepare multilingual materials, international pricing structures, and distribution partnership frameworks. The fitness industry in 2026 is defined by a simple insight: people want to feel good, not just look good. Wearable technology provides the data to personalize that journey. Recovery solutions address the body's need for restoration. And community-driven fitness formats satisfy the universal human desire for connection. Exhibitors who build their trade show presence around these three pillars — data, recovery, and community — will find a $278 billion industry that is ready to invest.
