KBIS 2026 Exhibitor FAQ: Everything You Need to Know

ShowFloorTips Editorial February 11, 2026

KBIS (Kitchen & Bath Industry Show) is North America's largest trade show dedicated to kitchen and bath design. Held February 17-19, 2026 at the Orange County Convention Center in Orlando, Florida, KBIS attracts over 30,000 design professionals and 600+ exhibitors. Here are answers to the most frequently asked exhibitor questions.

How much does booth space cost at KBIS 2026?

NKBA (National Kitchen & Bath Association) members pay approximately $48 per square foot, while non-members pay about $51 per square foot. A standard 10x10 booth costs $4,800 - $5,100, a 10x20 is $9,600 - $10,200, and a 20x20 island is $19,200 - $20,400. NKBA membership provides both booth discounts and access to the designer community that attends KBIS.

When and where is KBIS 2026?

KBIS 2026 takes place February 17-19, 2026 at the Orange County Convention Center (OCCC) in Orlando, Florida. KBIS co-locates with the International Builders' Show (IBS), creating Design & Construction Week with combined attendance exceeding 80,000 professionals.

What is the exhibitor registration deadline?

KBIS opens exhibitor registration approximately 8-12 months before the show. NKBA members receive priority booth selection. Returning exhibitors can rebook during the current show for the following year. First-time exhibitors should apply early as desirable locations fill quickly. Contact the KBIS sales team through kbis.com for specific deadlines and space availability.

How do I ship materials to KBIS in Orlando?

KBIS uses an official general services contractor for freight handling at the OCCC. Ship to the designated advance warehouse 2-3 weeks before the show, or arrange direct-to-show delivery during the move-in window. Drayage rates typically run $120 - $175 per CWT (per 100 lbs). The OCCC's loading docks are well-organized, but plan for the shared move-in schedule with IBS exhibitors.

What hotels are near the Orange County Convention Center?

The Rosen Centre, Rosen Plaza, and Hilton Orlando are within walking distance of the OCCC. The International Drive corridor offers dozens of hotel options from $120 - $250 per night. KBIS negotiates hotel blocks with discounted rates available through the official housing portal. Walt Disney World resort hotels are 15-20 minutes away and sometimes offer competitive rates. Book early as Design & Construction Week draws large crowds.

How many exhibitor badges are included?

KBIS provides approximately 10 booth staff badges per 100 square feet of booth space. A 10x10 booth receives about 10 badges. Additional badges can be purchased through the exhibitor portal. All staff must be registered and badged to access the show floor during setup, show hours, and teardown.

What are the setup and teardown schedules?

Move-in begins 2-3 days before the show opens. Standard hours are 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM during move-in, with overtime hours available. Large island booths get earlier move-in access. Move-out begins immediately after the show closes on the final day and must be completed within 24 hours. Early teardown is strictly prohibited and may result in penalties.

Does KBIS offer lead retrieval systems?

Yes. KBIS offers official lead retrieval scanners through its technology partner at $300 - $500 per unit. The system captures attendee badge data including contact information, company, title, and product interests. Is there parking at the OCCC?

The Orange County Convention Center has extensive on-site parking at $15 - $20 per day. The parking garages are large and rarely fill completely. The I-Drive Trolley runs along International Drive and stops at the OCCC. Rideshare drop-off zones are located at the main entrances.

What types of companies exhibit at KBIS?

KBIS showcases the full spectrum of kitchen and bath products including cabinetry, countertops, appliances, plumbing fixtures, tile, flooring, lighting, smart home technology, and design software. The show attracts kitchen designers, bath designers, architects, builders, remodelers, interior designers, and retail showroom owners from across North America.
