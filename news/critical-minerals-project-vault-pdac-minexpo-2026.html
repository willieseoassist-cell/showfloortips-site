Project Vault, $14K Copper, and the Scramble for Critical Minerals — What PDAC 2026 Means for Mining Exhibitors

ShowFloorTips Editorial · February 11, 2026

On February 3, 2026, the Trump administration launched "Project Vault," a $12 billion initiative combining $1.67 billion in private capital with a $10 billion loan from the U.S. Export-Import Bank to create a strategic critical minerals stockpile. Copper hit an all-time high above $14,000 per tonne in late January. And PDAC 2026, the world's premier mineral exploration and mining convention, opens in Toronto on March 1 with copper as the most discussed commodity on the agenda. For mining sector exhibitors, the convergence of government policy, record commodity prices, and heightened geopolitical urgency creates a show floor environment unlike anything the industry has seen in decades.

Project Vault critical minerals stockpile initiative (Feb 2026)

Project Vault Reshapes the Investment Landscape

Project Vault is designed to reduce U.S. dependence on China for critical minerals, covering everything identified as "critical" by the U.S. Geological Survey, including rare earths, lithium, uranium, and copper. The initiative has already won support from metals industry players, with mining stocks gaining on the announcement.

For exhibitors at PDAC and MINExpo, Project Vault creates a specific, actionable conversation. Companies with U.S.-based or allied-nation mining and processing assets can now point to a direct government buyer. Junior explorers with projects targeting critical minerals have a clearer path to financing. Equipment and technology providers can frame their solutions around the accelerated development timelines that government procurement demands.

"2025 put mining and metals at the heart of geopolitical competition. In 2026, the sector faces a decision: be a pawn or a player in a business cycle structurally driven by a geopolitical race for mineral security and national power."

-- White & Case, Mining & Metals 2026 Outlook

Copper: The Commodity Everyone Is Watching

Copper remains the most talked-about commodity heading into PDAC 2026. With CME futures prices near $5.98 per pound as of mid-January, analysts at ICSG and Wood Mackenzie forecast sustained supply deficits of 150,000 to 330,000 tonnes in 2026. The U.S. Geological Survey added copper to its critical minerals list in November 2025, enabling funding incentives and streamlined permitting for exploration and processing.

The supply tightness is not going away. Clean energy transitions are projected to dramatically increase demand for copper across electric vehicles, grid infrastructure, and renewable energy installations. For exhibitors offering copper exploration technology, extraction equipment, or processing solutions, every major mining show in 2026 will provide a receptive audience.

Beyond Copper: Lithium, Rare Earths, and Battery Metals

The IEA projects that battery material demand for lithium could grow over 40 times under aggressive clean energy transition scenarios. BMI forecasts robust M&A activity in 2026 as miners and metals producers prioritize deals that increase exposure to copper, lithium, and rare earth elements. Countries are wielding trade restrictions on critical minerals as part of broader geopolitical tensions, further incentivizing domestic production.

Key InsightExhibitors at PDAC's Core Shack and Investors Exchange (March 2-3) should prepare materials specifically for the investment community. Junior explorers with critical mineral projects in allied jurisdictions will find a particularly warm reception.

What PDAC 2026 Offers Exhibitors

PDAC 2026 runs March 1-4 at the Metro Toronto Convention Centre, with the Core Shack and Investors Exchange running March 2-3. The event brings together exploration companies, investors, mining executives, government officials, and technology providers from around the world. Here is what to expect:

  • Government participation will be elevated. The Critical Minerals Ministerial hosted by the U.S. State Department in February underscores the level of government attention on the sector. Expect ministerial-level delegations, policy announcements, and procurement discussions on the PDAC floor.
  • M&A conversations will accelerate. With commodity prices supporting project economics and government incentives reducing risk, PDAC will be a active venue for deal-making. Exhibitors should prepare materials for both commercial customers and potential acquirers.
  • ESG and permitting will be contentious topics. Fast-track permitting initiatives are welcomed by industry but face environmental scrutiny. Exhibitors offering environmental monitoring, reclamation technology, or ESG reporting platforms will find these themes woven into every conversation.
  • Technology adoption is accelerating. Autonomous haulage, AI-powered exploration, and digital mine management are moving from pilot programs to standard operations. Technology exhibitors should bring case studies demonstrating measurable productivity improvements.

The Price Outlook Supports Capital Investment

BMI, a unit of Fitch Solutions, holds a cautiously optimistic outlook for 2026, with most mineral and metal prices expected to edge higher, supported by declining tariff uncertainties, robust demand from energy transition sectors, and tighter supply. Control Risks identifies ten global issues that will shape mining markets, including the geopolitical scramble for resources, supply chain regionalization, and the intersection of mining with climate policy.

For equipment manufacturers and mining service providers exhibiting at MINExpo and PDAC, this price environment translates to active capital expenditure budgets among mining companies. The conversation shifts from "Can we afford this?" to "How quickly can you deliver?"

Exhibitor Playbook for Mining Shows in 2026

  1. Lead with the geopolitical narrative. Your products and services exist within a context of mineral security competition. Frame your value proposition around national supply chain resilience, not just operational efficiency.
  2. Prepare for investor conversations. PDAC uniquely combines commercial exhibiting with investor relations. Have your capital requirements, project timelines, and return projections ready for sophisticated resource sector investors.
  3. Demonstrate permitting acceleration. Any technology or service that reduces time-to-production will be in high demand. Show how your solution helps projects move from exploration to extraction faster while maintaining regulatory compliance.
  4. Address the talent gap. The mining sector faces a well-documented workforce challenge. Solutions that enable remote operations, automate routine tasks, or support training and upskilling programs address a pain point shared by virtually every mining company.

The mining and metals sector in 2026 sits at the intersection of record commodity prices, unprecedented government support, and existential geopolitical urgency. PDAC and MINExpo are where the deals that shape this industry's next decade will begin. Exhibitors who arrive prepared for the scale of the opportunity will be the ones who capitalize on it.
