Global Pet Expo vs SuperZoo 2026: Which Pet Industry Show Should You Exhibit At First?

13 min read February 14, 2026 ShowFloorTips Research Team

The pet industry has two anchor trade shows in North America: Global Pet Expo in Orlando and SuperZoo in Las Vegas. Global Pet Expo 2026 runs March 25 through 27 at the Orange County Convention Center with 20,000+ attendees and 1,300+ exhibitors. SuperZoo 2026 runs August 18 through 20 at the Mandalay Bay Convention Center with 30,000+ attendees. Both are essential for anyone serious about the pet products business. But if you are exhibiting for the first time, or if your budget forces you to choose one, the differences between these shows matter enormously for your ROI.

The core question is timing. Global Pet Expo is the spring buying show — where retailers make purchasing decisions for the back half of the year and where new products get their first major exposure. SuperZoo is the late-summer preview — where the industry reconvenes to evaluate what worked, discover products for the following year’s pipeline, and build relationships in a more educational and social atmosphere. Same industry, very different rhythms.

Timing and Buying Cycle

This is the single most important difference between the two shows, and it should drive your decision if you can only exhibit at one.

Global Pet Expo is the spring buying show. Retailers attend Orlando in March to make purchasing decisions for the back half of the year — the critical summer-through-holiday selling season. The New Products Showcase, featuring over 3,000 product launches, is the industry’s definitive product discovery event. Buyers walk the showcase with open purchase orders. If your product makes an impression in Orlando, it can be on retail shelves within three to six months. The buying intent at Global Pet Expo is among the highest of any trade show in any industry.

SuperZoo runs in August, which positions it as a late-summer check-in and a preview of the following year’s product pipeline. Retailers attend SuperZoo to evaluate how their spring purchases are performing, discover products for their Q1 and Q2 plans, attend education sessions, and strengthen relationships with existing vendors. The buying intent at SuperZoo is real but less urgent than at Global Pet Expo. SuperZoo is where the industry builds the relationships that turn into orders over the following months.

Buyer Demographics

Global Pet Expo: The Buying Machine

Global Pet Expo’s strict qualification process ensures that every attendee is a verified pet industry professional. The buyer base includes national retail chain category managers, regional distributor purchasing directors, independent pet boutique owners, international buyers sourcing American products for global markets, and the e-commerce buyers who are reshaping pet product distribution. The international buyer presence at Global Pet Expo is particularly strong — distributors from Europe, Asia, and Latin America attend specifically to source products for their home markets. If your growth strategy includes international distribution, Global Pet Expo’s international buyer base is unmatched in the pet industry.

SuperZoo: The Community Hub

SuperZoo draws a broader attendee base that includes the buying professionals who attend Global Pet Expo plus a significant population of groomers, pet service providers, veterinary professionals, and pet industry entrepreneurs who are attracted by SuperZoo’s extensive education programme and its Las Vegas location. The grooming competitions and education tracks at SuperZoo are world-class and attract a passionate community that does not attend Global Pet Expo in the same numbers. For brands that sell grooming products, grooming equipment, or pet services technology, SuperZoo’s attendee demographics may be more precisely aligned with their target customer.

New Product Showcase

Global Pet Expo’s New Products Showcase is the pet industry’s equivalent of a new car reveal at an auto show. With over 3,000 product launches, it is the largest concentrated product discovery event in the pet industry and draws the most innovation-focused buyers, media, and influencers. The showcase is organized by category, judged for innovation awards, and covered extensively by trade media. A strong showing in the Global Pet Expo New Products Showcase can generate press coverage, retail interest, and social media buzz that carries a brand for months.

SuperZoo features new products as well, but the showcase is smaller in scale and the media coverage is less concentrated. The timing matters here too: a product launched at Global Pet Expo in March has the entire spring-through-holiday selling season ahead of it. A product launched at SuperZoo in August has a shorter runway before the critical holiday season, though it is well-positioned for Q1 of the following year.

Cost Comparison

Both shows offer comparable booth pricing, with Global Pet Expo running slightly higher due to the larger venue and higher buyer density. The real cost difference is in the destination logistics.

Orlando in March offers moderate hotel rates ($150 to $250 per night near the convention center), reasonable dining costs, and the convenience of Orlando International Airport’s extensive domestic and international connections. The Orange County Convention Center’s location on International Drive provides a range of accommodation and dining options within walking distance.

Las Vegas in August means Mandalay Bay hotel rates during a major convention ($200 to $350 per night), higher dining costs on the Strip, and the August desert heat that makes outdoor walking between venues uncomfortable. However, Las Vegas offers more entertainment and hospitality options for client entertainment, and the concentration of hotels, restaurants, and event venues at Mandalay Bay creates a compact, convenient show experience.

International Buyers

If international distribution is part of your strategy, Global Pet Expo is the clear choice. The APPA’s international outreach programme and the show’s reputation as the premier American pet products sourcing event draw buyers and distributors from dozens of countries. The international buyer delegations at Global Pet Expo are organized, purposeful, and actively seeking products to bring to their home markets.

SuperZoo’s international attendance is growing but remains primarily North American. The WPA is investing in international outreach, and SuperZoo 2026 will feature expanded international buyer programmes. But for now, if your top priority is connecting with international distribution partners, Global Pet Expo delivers significantly more international buyer traffic.

The Verdict

Exhibit at Global Pet Expo first if: You are launching a new product and want maximum buyer attention during the spring buying season. You are targeting international distribution partners. You want the industry’s largest New Products Showcase as your launch platform. Your product is ready for retail now and needs immediate buyer exposure.

Exhibit at SuperZoo first if: You sell grooming products, grooming equipment, or pet services technology. You prioritize education and community-building over immediate sales. You want to test market response and refine your pitch before the higher-stakes Global Pet Expo. Your product is better positioned for a Q1 launch timeline.

Do both if: You can afford it. The pet industry’s most successful brands exhibit at both shows and use Global Pet Expo for product launches and buying season activation, and SuperZoo for relationship maintenance, education, and next-year pipeline development. The two shows complement each other perfectly across the annual calendar.

Which Show Is Right for You?

The honest answer for most pet industry exhibitors is: you need both. Global Pet Expo and SuperZoo serve different purposes at different points in the annual business cycle, and the pet industry’s most successful brands treat both as essential. But if you are a startup, a first-time exhibitor, or a company with a limited trade show budget, the choice comes down to timing and buyer intent.

If your product is ready and you want orders, exhibit at Global Pet Expo. The spring buying season timing, the massive New Products Showcase, the international buyer base, and the concentrated purchasing intent make Orlando in March the most commercially productive three days on the pet industry calendar.

If you are building a brand and prioritize education, community, and relationship development, exhibit at SuperZoo. The Las Vegas atmosphere, the extensive education programme, the grooming community, and the more relaxed networking dynamic make SuperZoo the show where long-term industry relationships are built and deepened.

Whichever show you choose, arrive with a plan, prepare your target list, pre-schedule your priority meetings, and follow up within 48 hours of the show closing. The pet industry rewards passion, preparation, and professionalism at both shows equally.

Key Takeaway

Global Pet Expo is the buying show — where orders are placed and new products launch. SuperZoo is the community show — where relationships deepen and brands grow. If forced to choose one, follow the buying cycle: exhibit at Global Pet Expo if your product is ready for retail, SuperZoo if you are building for the long term.
