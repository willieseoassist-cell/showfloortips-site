Luxury & Jewelry

Lab-Grown Diamonds Capture 20% of Sales as the $33.5B Market Reshapes JCK Las Vegas 2026

ShowFloorTips Editorial · February 11, 2026

The luxury jewelry industry has never been more divided — or more dynamic. Lab-grown diamonds now represent approximately 20 percent of global diamond jewelry sales, with prices up to 85 percent lower than natural stones. In the United States, half of engagement ring buyers are choosing lab-grown diamonds, a milestone that would have been unthinkable a decade ago. The global lab-grown diamond market is projected at $33.54 billion in 2026, growing at a CAGR of 13.42 percent toward $91.85 billion by 2034, according to Fortune Business Insights. Meanwhile, the natural diamond sector and high jewelry houses are doubling down on rarity, provenance, and storytelling as their competitive differentiators.

This tension between accessibility and exclusivity will be on full display at JCK Las Vegas 2026, returning to the Venetian Expo May 29 through June 1, with the invite-only Luxury event opening May 27–28. Registration is already open for what remains the North American jewelry industry's definitive commercial event. For exhibitors — whether you sell lab-grown stones, natural diamonds, colored gemstones, finished jewelry, or retail technology — understanding the market dynamics at play is essential to a successful show.

Le Vian's 2026 Trend Forecast: Emotion, Symbolism, and Rarity

Le Vian, a fixture of JCK Las Vegas for decades, unveiled its 2026 jewelry trend forecast at JCK Luxury, organized around four pillars that reflect the industry's current mood and direction.

Patriotism and Americana

Bold red, white, and blue gemstones lead this trend category, including Le Vian's Montana Sapphire Ombre in denim-inspired gradients and American Robin's Egg Blue Turquoise. The emphasis on domestically sourced gemstones resonates with consumers increasingly interested in supply chain transparency and American craftsmanship. For exhibitors offering American-origin gemstones or domestically manufactured jewelry, this trend validates a sourcing story that connects emotionally with buyers.

Exclusivity and High Jewelry

Le Vian's most prestigious collections feature platinum and 18K gold settings with rare gems including Neon Blue Paraiba Tourmaline and natural color pink and yellow diamonds, with pieces ranging from $20,000 to over $1 million. This category represents the natural diamond industry's strongest counter-narrative to lab-grown accessibility: for the ultra-luxury buyer, rarity is the product, and nothing lab-made can replicate geological uniqueness.

Indulgence and Chocolate Diamonds

The 25th Jubilee of Le Vian's Chocolate Diamonds celebrates rich brown tones, with Chocolate Ombre leading the trend through gradients of Chocolate and Nude Diamonds set in flavored golds. This category demonstrates how effective branding can transform an overlooked diamond category into a cultural phenomenon — a lesson relevant to any exhibitor seeking to differentiate commoditized products.

Serenity and Nature-Inspired Design

Blue-green tranquility expressed through Australian boulder opals, Montana Sapphire Ombre, and Neon Blue Paraiba Tourmaline in airy, fluid designs inspired by the natural world. This trend reflects the broader wellness-driven consumer preference for jewelry that communicates calm, balance, and connection to nature.

Key InsightLe Vian's trend forecast reveals that the high end of the jewelry market is competing on emotion and narrative, not price. For exhibitors at JCK Luxury, your booth experience should tell a story. Invest in visual merchandising, brand narrative materials, and experiential elements that make buyers feel the emotional resonance of your collection.

The Lab-Grown Diamond Reality

The numbers are unambiguous. The U.S. lab-grown diamond market alone reached $24.23 billion in 2026, with projections pointing toward $64.12 billion by 2035. The up-to-2-carat segment holds 70.49 percent of market share, driven by lower prices compared to larger stones. Colorless lab-grown diamonds lead with 69.34 percent of global market share, reflecting strong demand for traditional-looking stones at dramatically lower price points.

Major brands have responded. Pandora and other major houses have launched dedicated lab-grown lines, signaling that lab-grown is no longer a niche category but a mainstream commercial segment. For retailers, lab-grown diamonds offer significantly higher margins on a per-carat basis and appeal to younger consumers who prioritize value and ethical sourcing over geological rarity.

But the lab-grown segment faces its own challenges. Falling production costs have driven per-carat prices down dramatically, creating margin pressure for producers and raising questions about long-term resale value. The value proposition that makes lab-grown diamonds attractive to consumers — they are dramatically cheaper — also creates a perception challenge around whether they are "real" luxury goods. Exhibitors in the lab-grown space should be prepared for pointed questions about pricing trajectories, certification standards, and how retailers should position lab-grown stones alongside natural diamonds.

Colored Gemstones Gain Momentum

Beyond the diamond debate, colored gemstones are experiencing a renaissance. Montana sapphires, Paraiba tourmalines, Australian opals, and other distinctive stones offer what lab-grown diamonds cannot: natural geological rarity combined with unique color profiles that cannot be standardized. Le Vian's 2026 trends heavily feature colored gemstones, and the broader market reflects growing consumer interest in jewelry that stands apart from the diamond-dominated mainstream.

For colored gemstone exhibitors at JCK, the lab-grown diamond trend is actually an ally. As lab-grown diamonds commoditize the diamond market, consumers seeking unique, rare, and distinctive jewelry are discovering colored gemstones as an alternative that offers both natural rarity and aesthetic differentiation.

JCK Las Vegas 2026 Event Structure

  • Luxury by JCK (May 27–28): The exclusive, invitation-only event for high jewelry and luxury brands. Access is limited to vetted buyers and elite brands, creating an intimate environment for high-value transactions. If your products are in the $20,000-plus range, this is where your most qualified buyers will be.
  • JCK Las Vegas (May 29 – June 1): The main event at the Venetian Expo, open to the full spectrum of the jewelry industry. From independent retailers and buying groups to chain stores and e-commerce platforms, JCK Las Vegas provides the broadest buyer audience in North American jewelry.

Exhibitor Strategies for JCK Las Vegas 2026

  1. Take a clear position in the lab-grown conversation. Whether you are a lab-grown producer, a natural diamond advocate, or a retailer who sells both, you need a clear, confident position on how your products fit into the current market. Ambiguity does not sell in a divided market. Buyers want partners who understand their positioning and can help them communicate it to consumers.
  2. Invest in storytelling and visual experience. The Le Vian trend forecast makes clear that emotion and narrative are driving the high end of the market. Your booth should not just display products — it should tell the story of your brand, your sourcing, and the emotional experience your jewelry creates for consumers. Consider video content, interactive displays, and curated presentation environments.
  3. Prepare for serious buyer conversations about margins. In a market where lab-grown diamonds are 85 percent cheaper than natural equivalents, margin conversations are inevitable. Arm your sales team with detailed margin analysis, pricing comparisons, and sell-through data that helps retailers understand the financial case for your product category.
  4. Leverage the colored gemstone opportunity. If your collection includes colored gemstones, position them as the anti-commodity play. In a market where diamonds — natural and lab-grown — are competing on price, colored gemstones compete on uniqueness, personality, and design freedom.

JCK Las Vegas 2026 arrives at a moment of genuine transformation in the jewelry industry. The lab-grown diamond market has reached critical mass, forcing every participant in the industry — from miners to retailers — to reconsider their value proposition. The natural diamond and high jewelry segments are responding with intensified emphasis on rarity, emotion, and storytelling. And colored gemstones are emerging as a third path that offers both natural rarity and aesthetic distinctiveness. For exhibitors who understand these dynamics and position accordingly, the Venetian Expo floor in late May offers the industry's most concentrated commercial opportunity.
