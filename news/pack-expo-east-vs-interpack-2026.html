PACK EXPO East vs interpack 2026: Should You Go Regional in Philly or Global in Dusseldorf?

The packaging industry's two spring shows could not be more different. PACK EXPO East offers concentrated East Coast access. interpack offers the entire world. Here is how to decide which one -- or both -- belongs on your 2026 calendar.

Photo via Unsplash

The packaging industry's 2026 spring calendar presents a strategic dilemma that hits every exhibitor and attendee in the sector. PACK EXPO East opens March 3-5 at the Pennsylvania Convention Center in Philadelphia, a focused regional show drawing approximately 7,000 attendees and 400 exhibitors. Just two months later, interpack -- the undisputed heavyweight of global packaging exhibitions -- runs May 7-13 at the Messe Dusseldorf in Germany, pulling in 170,000 attendees and 2,700 exhibitors from every continent. One is a precision tool. The other is a sledgehammer. Both have their purpose, and choosing wrong can waste tens of thousands of dollars.

This comparison breaks down both shows across every factor that affects your return on investment: audience composition, exhibitor costs, networking dynamics, logistical complexity, and the type of business each show is designed to generate. If you sell packaging machinery, materials, automation systems, or services to the companies that package consumer goods, this analysis will tell you where your money works hardest.

Show Profiles: Two Completely Different Animals

PACK EXPO East 2026

PACK EXPO East is produced by PMMI, the Association for Packaging and Processing Technologies, as a regional complement to the massive PACK EXPO International held in Chicago on even-numbered years. The East edition targets the Northeast and Mid-Atlantic packaging market -- an area that includes some of the densest concentrations of food, beverage, pharmaceutical, and consumer goods manufacturers in the United States. Campbell Soup in Camden, Johnson & Johnson across New Jersey, Hershey two hours west in Pennsylvania, and hundreds of contract packagers and co-manufacturers scattered across the I-95 corridor all consider PACK EXPO East their local show.

The event occupies roughly 100,000 net square feet at the Pennsylvania Convention Center, making it navigable in a single day but worth two for thorough exploration. With approximately 400 exhibitors and 7,000 attendees, the ratio is intimate. Exhibitors report that conversations are longer, more detailed, and more likely to advance to a follow-up meeting compared to the sensory overload of larger shows. The three-day format keeps logistics simple and costs manageable.

interpack 2026

interpack is the global packaging industry's definitive event, held every three years in Dusseldorf, Germany. The 2026 edition marks its return after a three-year gap, and the anticipation is enormous. The show fills all 19 halls of the Messe Dusseldorf -- approximately 1.8 million square feet of exhibition space -- with 2,700 exhibitors from over 60 countries. Attendance regularly exceeds 170,000, with roughly two-thirds of visitors traveling from outside Germany. It is not just a trade show; it is the packaging industry's Olympics.

interpack's scope is staggering. The show covers packaging machinery, packaging materials, confectionery and bakery equipment, labeling and decoration, and the entire supply chain of related processes. Major exhibitors bring full production lines, running live on the show floor, producing actual packaged products in real time. The seven-day duration reflects the reality that no human being can see everything in less than a week. Most serious visitors plan three to four days and still leave having missed entire halls.

"PACK EXPO East is where I find the machine that solves my problem this quarter. interpack is where I discover the machine I did not know existed that transforms my operation next year. They serve completely different strategic purposes." -- VP of Operations, mid-size food manufacturer

Audience: Who Shows Up and Why

PACK EXPO East's audience is overwhelmingly domestic, predominantly from the Eastern United States, and heavily weighted toward operational buyers. These are plant managers, packaging engineers, and procurement directors who are actively evaluating equipment for near-term purchases. PMMI's data shows that over 70 percent of PACK EXPO East attendees have direct purchasing authority or direct influence over purchasing decisions. The audience is small but qualified to an extraordinary degree.

interpack's audience is global, diverse, and stratified. You will find C-suite executives conducting strategic vendor assessments alongside line engineers evaluating specific components. The international composition means conversations span dozens of languages, regulatory frameworks, and market expectations. A German pharma manufacturer has different requirements than a Brazilian beverage company or a Nigerian food processor, and all three are walking the same halls. For exhibitors with global ambitions, this diversity is the point. For exhibitors focused exclusively on the US market, much of the audience is irrelevant to immediate revenue goals.

Exhibitor Experience: Costs, Logistics, and ROI

PACK EXPO East Costs

PACK EXPO East is designed to be accessible. Booth space starts at approximately $40 per square foot, making a 10x10 configuration roughly $4,000 for the space alone. All-in costs for a mid-size exhibitor -- including build-out, graphics, equipment shipping, hotel for a 4-person team, and meals -- typically run $25,000-$45,000. Philadelphia's hotel market is competitive, with rooms near the convention center averaging $200-$300 per night during the show. Flights from most East Coast cities are domestic, short, and cheap. You can drive to Philly from New York, Baltimore, or Washington, D.C. in under three hours.

interpack Costs

interpack is a major investment. Booth space at the Messe Dusseldorf runs approximately EUR 200-250 per square meter (roughly $25-30 per square foot), but the real cost is in the build-out. European trade show culture expects custom-built, architect-designed booth structures that make American pop-up displays look like card tables. A respectable 30-square-meter booth at interpack, fully built out with custom walls, lighting, product displays, and meeting areas, typically costs $80,000-$150,000. Add transatlantic flights for a team of 6-8 people, seven nights of Dusseldorf hotel rates ($250-$400/night during interpack, when the city is at capacity), per diems, equipment shipping from the US, and AV rentals, and total investment easily reaches $150,000-$250,000 for a mid-size exhibitor. Larger companies regularly spend $500,000 or more.

Cost-Per-Lead Reality Check

PACK EXPO East: All-in cost of $35,000 divided by an expected 80-120 qualified leads = approximately $290-$440 per lead. These leads are predominantly US-based, operationally focused, and often on an active purchase timeline.

interpack: All-in cost of $180,000 divided by an expected 300-500 qualified leads = approximately $360-$600 per lead. These leads are internationally diverse, strategically oriented, and may require longer sales cycles across multiple time zones and currencies.

The math: PACK EXPO East delivers a lower cost per domestic lead with faster conversion timelines. interpack delivers global reach and strategic relationships that can open entirely new markets. The right choice depends on whether your growth strategy is domestic expansion or international diversification.

Networking and Relationship Building

PACK EXPO East's compact format creates natural networking density. With 7,000 attendees in a manageable venue, you will run into the same people at sessions, on the show floor, and at after-hours events. Philadelphia's restaurant scene -- from the Reading Terminal Market adjacent to the convention center to the Italian restaurants on East Passyunk Avenue -- provides excellent options for client dinners that do not require a taxi. The PMMI-organized networking events, including the opening night reception, are small enough that conversations happen naturally rather than being lost in crowd noise.

interpack's networking operates on a completely different scale. The sheer volume of attendees makes chance encounters unlikely, so pre-scheduled meetings are essential. Most serious exhibitors book their meeting calendars two to three months before the show and use interpack's matchmaking platform to identify and invite specific prospects. The Dusseldorf Altstadt (old town) -- a strip of over 250 bars and restaurants along the Rhine -- becomes the packaging industry's unofficial global clubhouse every evening during the show. The networking is extraordinary, but it requires advance planning that PACK EXPO East's more casual format does not demand.

Language and Cultural Considerations at interpack

If your team speaks only English, you can still navigate interpack effectively -- English is the show's lingua franca, and most exhibitors staff their booths with English speakers. However, having a German speaker on your team provides a meaningful advantage in the DACH market, and Spanish and Portuguese speakers will find abundant opportunities among Latin American attendees. Business card exchange protocols, meeting formality, and follow-up expectations vary significantly across cultures, and the companies that invest in cultural preparation outperform those that bring a purely American approach to a global event.

What Each Show Does Best

  • PACK EXPO East excels at near-term US business. If you need leads that convert within one to two quarters, from buyers located within a day's drive of your service territory, this show delivers with surgical precision at a fraction of interpack's cost.
  • interpack excels at global positioning and long-term strategy. If you are launching a new product for the global market, seeking international distributors, evaluating overseas manufacturing partners, or establishing your brand as a serious global player, there is no substitute. The three-year cycle makes every edition a must-attend milestone.
  • PACK EXPO East favors smaller companies. A $30,000 exhibit budget buys a credible, professional presence. At interpack, the same budget barely covers booth construction and gets lost among neighbors spending five times more.
  • interpack favors companies with global supply chains. If you source materials from Europe, sell equipment into Asia, or are evaluating production partnerships across multiple continents, interpack compresses six months of international travel into one week.

The Verdict: Who Should Go Where in 2026

Go to PACK EXPO East if: you are a US-based packaging machinery or materials company focused on the Eastern Seaboard market, you need qualified domestic leads on an active buying timeline, your exhibit budget is under $50,000, or you are a smaller company that would be visually overwhelmed at interpack. Also go if you already exhibit at PACK EXPO International in Chicago and want to extend your PMMI presence into the spring calendar without the cost and complexity of a transatlantic show.

Go to interpack if: you have global ambitions, need international distribution partners, are launching a product for the worldwide market, or your competitors will be there and your absence would signal retreat. If your company competes at the top tier of packaging technology, interpack is non-negotiable. The three-year cycle means missing 2026 means waiting until 2029 for the next opportunity.

Go to both if: your company serves both the US domestic market and international customers. The March/May timing is tight but workable. Use PACK EXPO East as a warm-up -- test your messaging, refine your demo, collect US leads -- then deploy the polished version at interpack two months later. The sequential scheduling is actually an advantage for companies disciplined enough to iterate between shows.

Our pick for most companies in 2026: Both, but with different objectives. PACK EXPO East is your lead generation engine. interpack is your global brand statement. Companies that treat them as complementary rather than competing investments will outperform those who choose only one. But if the budget forces a choice, and your revenue is predominantly North American, PACK EXPO East delivers faster, cheaper, and more measurable returns.
