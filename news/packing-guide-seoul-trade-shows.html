What to Pack for Seoul Trade Shows

ShowFloorTips · February 01, 2026

The difference between a forgettable booth and a lead-generating machine comes down to strategy. Packing for a trade show in Seoul requires balancing professional appearance, practical comfort, and the unique demands of exhibition work. Whether you are a veteran exhibitor or heading to Seoul for the first time, this guide ensures you arrive prepared for everything the show floor and the city have to offer.

Climate and Weather in Seoul

Seoul's climate is variable depending on the season. Research the specific weather forecast for your travel dates and pack accordingly. Business attire is standard for trade shows worldwide. Layers are always a safe strategy for adapting to indoor and outdoor temperature differences.

Regardless of the season, trade show venues themselves maintain controlled indoor environments. The show floor can be cooler than expected due to industrial air conditioning, so always pack at least one light layer you can add or remove as needed throughout the day.

Business Attire Essentials

Your wardrobe for a Seoul trade show should project professionalism while keeping you comfortable during long days on your feet. Here is what to pack:

  • Two to three professional outfits for show days (one per day plus a backup), matching your company's dress code and the show's formality level.
  • One elevated outfit for client dinners, networking receptions, or award ceremonies that often accompany major trade shows in Seoul.
  • Comfortable, professional shoes that can handle standing and walking for 8-10 hours per day on hard convention center floors. Break them in before the trip.
  • Compression socks or insoles to reduce fatigue during long days of standing at your booth.
  • A light blazer or cardigan that works across multiple outfits and adapts to temperature changes between indoor and outdoor environments.
  • A branded polo or company shirt if your booth team wears matching attire for brand consistency.

Booth Toolkit and Emergency Kit

Experienced exhibitors never travel without a personal toolkit for last-minute booth adjustments and unexpected situations. Pack these items in your carry-on luggage:

  • Multi-tool or basic toolkit with screwdrivers, pliers, and an adjustable wrench.
  • Gaffer tape, double-sided tape, and clear packing tape for quick fixes.
  • Cable ties, Velcro strips, and binder clips for securing displays and wiring.
  • Extension cords and a power strip (check voltage compatibility for international travel to South Korea).
  • Portable phone charger (at least 10,000 mAh capacity) and charging cables for all team devices.
  • Business cards (bring at least 200) and a professional cardholder.
  • Markers, pens, notepads, and sticky notes for on-the-spot needs.
  • Basic first-aid supplies: pain relievers, blister treatment, throat lozenges, and hand sanitizer.
  • Breath mints or gum for all those close-conversation booth interactions.

Technology and Digital Essentials

Your tech setup can make or break your trade show experience. Ensure you pack the following:

  • Laptop or tablet with all presentation files, demo software, and backup copies loaded locally (do not rely on venue Wi-Fi).
  • Lead capture device or app fully configured and tested before departure.
  • Universal power adapter and voltage converter if traveling internationally to South Korea.
  • Portable Wi-Fi hotspot as a backup for unreliable venue connectivity.
  • USB drives with presentation backups and product materials for quick sharing.
  • HDMI cable and any necessary display adapters for connecting to monitors or screens.

Travel Documents and Logistics

Organize these essential documents before departing for Seoul:

  • Passport (valid for at least six months beyond your travel dates for international trips).
  • Visa documentation if required for entry to South Korea.
  • Printed and digital copies of show registration confirmations, booth assignments, and exhibitor badges.
  • Hotel reservations, flight itineraries, and ground transportation arrangements.
  • Shipping and drayage receipts for booth materials sent ahead to the venue.
  • Travel insurance documentation covering trip cancellation, medical emergencies, and equipment.
  • Corporate credit card and local currency (or a card with no foreign transaction fees for international travel).

Shipping and Logistics Reminders

Before you pack your personal luggage, ensure your booth materials are handled:

  • Coordinate with your booth builder for installation and dismantle labor well ahead of the show dates.
  • Pack a separate essentials kit with tools, tape, zip ties, markers, and first-aid supplies for last-minute adjustments.
  • Keep digital copies of all shipping documents, insurance certificates, and venue contracts accessible on your phone.

Final Packing Tips for Seoul

The key to packing for a trade show in Seoul is preparation and redundancy. Pack essentials in your carry-on in case checked luggage is delayed. Bring backup copies of critical digital files. And always leave room in your suitcase for materials, samples, and branded items you will inevitably collect on the show floor.
