How Much Does It Cost to Exhibit at World of Concrete 2026?

ShowFloorTips Editorial February 11, 2026

World of Concrete (WOC) is the premier annual event for the commercial concrete and masonry construction industries. Held every January at the Las Vegas Convention Center, WOC 2026 (January 19-22) attracts approximately 60,000 attendees and 1,500+ exhibiting companies across over 700,000 square feet of indoor and outdoor exhibit space. Here is what it costs to exhibit.

Booth Space Costs

WOC offers both indoor and outdoor exhibit space with distinct pricing structures:

Indoor Space

  • 10x10 inline booth (100 sq ft): $3,500 - $4,800 ($35-$48/sq ft)
  • 10x20 inline booth (200 sq ft): $7,000 - $9,600
  • 20x20 island booth (400 sq ft): $14,000 - $19,200

Outdoor Space

  • Outdoor demo area (per sq ft): $20 - $35/sq ft
  • Typical outdoor space (400-1,000 sq ft): $8,000 - $35,000

Outdoor space is popular for heavy equipment demonstrations, concrete mixing, and masonry competitions but requires additional costs for ground preparation.

Booth Design and Construction

  • Pop-up display: $500 - $2,000
  • Custom 10x10 with product displays: $8,000 - $25,000
  • Custom 20x20 island: $30,000 - $80,000
  • Rental booth: $3,000 - $12,000 for 10x10

Concrete-Specific Demo Costs

WOC is unique in offering live demonstration features that add significant cost:

  • Sandbox (4" deep): $15.00 per sq ft
  • Sandbox (6" deep): $15.50 per sq ft
  • Slab on grade (4" deep): $20.00 per sq ft
  • Demo wall construction: $425 per linear foot
  • Concrete/material disposal and cleanup: $1,000 - $5,000
  • Water supply and drainage: $500 - $1,500

Drayage and Material Handling

WOC exhibitors often ship extremely heavy equipment, making drayage a major cost:

  • Standard drayage rate: $140 - $195 per CWT
  • Light booth (10x10, 800-1,200 lbs): $1,120 - $2,340
  • Heavy equipment (5,000-20,000 lbs): $7,000 - $39,000
  • Crane and rigging (for heavy equipment): $2,000 - $15,000
  • Forklift service: $500 - $2,000

Electrical and Internet

  • Standard electrical (500W): $300 - $700
  • High-power service (for equipment demos): $800 - $3,000
  • Compressed air supply: $500 - $1,500
  • Dedicated Wi-Fi: $600 - $1,800

Travel and Lodging

  • Hotel (4 nights): $800 - $2,000 per person
  • Flights: $250 - $700 per person
  • Meals: $60 - $120 per person per day
  • Ground transportation: $150 - $350 per person

WOC occurs in late January, which is not peak Las Vegas convention season, so hotel rates are more reasonable than during CES.

Marketing Materials

  • Pre-show email outreach: $500 - $1,500
  • Printed materials: $300 - $1,000
  • Product demos and samples: $500 - $5,000
  • Giveaways: $500 - $2,000
  • Lead retrieval: $300 - $500 per scanner

A basic 10x10 indoor exhibitor at WOC should budget $15,000 - $30,000. Companies with outdoor demo areas and heavy equipment typically spend $50,000 - $200,000+. Equipment manufacturers with large outdoor areas and live demos can exceed $300,000.

Tips to Reduce Your WOC Costs

  1. Start indoors: Test the show with an indoor booth before investing in outdoor demo space.
  2. Share demo space: Partner with complementary exhibitors to split outdoor demo costs.
  3. Drive equipment in: If feasible, driving equipment to Las Vegas avoids rigging and crane fees.
  4. Ship early: Late drayage surcharges at WOC can double your material handling costs.
  5. Book mid-Strip hotels: January rates in Las Vegas are moderate compared to CES or SEMA weeks.
