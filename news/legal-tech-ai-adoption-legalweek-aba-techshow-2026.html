Legal Tech Spending Surges 9.7% as AI Adoption Doubles — What Exhibitors Need for Legalweek and ABA TECHSHOW 2026

ShowFloorTips Editorial · February 11, 2026

The legal profession's relationship with technology has never moved faster than it is right now. Law firms dramatically accelerated their technology investments in 2025, with spending on tech and knowledge management tools growing 9.7 percent and 10.5 percent respectively — the fastest real growth likely ever experienced in the legal industry, according to the Thomson Reuters State of Legal Market report. Corporate legal AI adoption more than doubled in a single year, jumping from 23 percent to 52 percent. And the market for legal AI tools specifically has risen from $1.5 billion in 2024 to over $3 billion in 2025, with projections reaching $10.8 billion by 2030.

These numbers set the stage for the two most important legal technology trade shows of the year: Legalweek New York (March 9–12, North Javits Center) and ABA TECHSHOW (March 25–28, Hyatt Regency McCormick Place, Chicago). Together, these events will attract more than 10,000 legal professionals — Legalweek alone draws 6,000-plus attendees, 400-plus speakers across 100-plus sessions, and representation from 51 countries. For legal tech exhibitors, these back-to-back March events represent the most commercially concentrated window in the legal technology calendar.

From Experimentation to Execution

The defining shift in legal AI for 2026 is the move from experimentation to execution. Generative AI has moved beyond hype and is now a daily fixture in legal workflows, from drafting first-pass contracts to summarizing deposition records and extracting insights from document review. In the personal injury sector specifically, more than 50 percent of firms are already using AI, signaling mainstream adoption in even the most traditionally conservative practice areas.

For legal tech exhibitors, this transition fundamentally changes the sales conversation. In 2024 and early 2025, the question was "should we use AI?" In 2026, the question is "which AI tools should we use, how do we integrate them into our workflows, and how do we manage the risks?" This maturation of the buyer means your booth conversations at Legalweek and ABA TECHSHOW will be more technical, more procurement-focused, and more demanding of concrete evidence.

The Adoption Gap Between Large and Small Firms

A significant adoption gap persists between large and small firms. Firms with 51 or more lawyers reported a 39 percent generative AI adoption rate, while firms with 50 or fewer lawyers showed adoption at approximately 20 percent. This gap creates distinct market opportunities for exhibitors:

  • For large firms: The conversation is about platform consolidation, enterprise governance, and integration with existing practice management and document management systems. These buyers want solutions that scale across hundreds of lawyers and integrate with their existing technology stack.
  • For small firms: The conversation is about accessibility, affordability, and ease of implementation. These buyers want AI tools that work out of the box, do not require dedicated IT support, and deliver immediate productivity gains without a lengthy deployment process.
Key InsightIf you exhibit at both Legalweek (which skews toward larger firms and corporate legal departments) and ABA TECHSHOW (which has strong representation from solo practitioners and small firms), consider tailoring your booth messaging and demo flow for each audience. A single-message approach risks alienating one of these distinct buyer segments.

Key Legal AI Trends for 2026

AI Governance and Policy Frameworks

In-house legal departments are establishing concrete expectations for how outside counsel uses AI and reports on its impact in 2026. This governance trend creates demand for compliance tools, audit trails, AI usage policies, and reporting dashboards that help firms demonstrate responsible AI use to their corporate clients. Exhibitors selling AI governance platforms, compliance monitoring tools, or consulting services around AI policy have a timely and receptive audience.

AI in Dispute Resolution

More corporations will experiment with AI for resolving disputes in 2026. Solutions range from the American Arbitration Association's AI Arbitrator concept to case assessment tools using generative AI in eDiscovery platforms. This emerging category represents a frontier for legal tech exhibitors who can demonstrate AI capabilities in litigation support, arbitration, and alternative dispute resolution.

Technology Consolidation

Many law firms currently operate with a fragmented technology stack — separate tools for document management, practice management, billing, eDiscovery, contract review, and client communication. In 2026, consolidation is accelerating toward a vision of one cloud, one identity, one data model. Exhibitors who can demonstrate how their product integrates with or replaces multiple point solutions have a compelling pitch for firms seeking to reduce complexity.

Generative AI in Contract Work

Contract drafting, review, and management represent the largest commercial application of generative AI in legal practice. AI tools that can generate first drafts from templates, identify non-standard clauses, compare terms across multiple agreements, and flag risk provisions are moving from pilot programs to standard workflows. For exhibitors in the contract lifecycle management space, Legalweek and ABA TECHSHOW are where procurement decisions are influenced.

The 2026 Legal Tech Show Calendar

  • Legalweek New York (March 9–12, North Javits Center, New York City): One of the largest and most commercially significant legal technology conferences in the U.S., drawing 6,000+ attendees, 400+ speakers across 100+ sessions, and representation from 51 countries. The expansive exhibit hall and dynamic sessions make Legalweek the premier venue for enterprise-scale legal technology sales. The audience skews toward large law firms, Am Law 200 firms, corporate legal departments, and litigation support organizations.
  • ABA TECHSHOW (March 25–28, Hyatt Regency McCormick Place, Chicago): Where legal professionals and technology converge for four days of interactive workshops, expert-led sessions, and live demos. ABA TECHSHOW has traditionally been the more practitioner-focused event, with strong representation from solo practitioners, small firms, and lawyers who are actively hands-on with their technology decisions. The workshop format allows for deeper product demonstrations than the typical trade show booth encounter.

Exhibitor Strategies for Legal Tech Shows in 2026

  1. Lead with ROI and workflow integration, not AI for AI's sake. The legal profession has moved past the novelty phase of AI. Your booth messaging should focus on measurable outcomes: time saved per document review, reduction in contract turnaround time, cost per matter reduction, or billable hour recovery. Lawyers are trained to demand evidence, so bring case studies with specific metrics.
  2. Address the governance and ethics conversation. Corporate legal departments are increasingly requiring outside counsel to disclose AI usage and demonstrate responsible implementation. If your product includes audit trails, permission controls, data privacy safeguards, or compliance reporting, feature these capabilities prominently. Governance is moving from a "nice to have" to a procurement requirement.
  3. Demonstrate, do not just describe. Both Legalweek and ABA TECHSHOW audiences are sophisticated technology evaluators. Static booth displays and slide decks will not suffice. Build live, interactive demonstrations that let attendees use your product on real-world legal scenarios. The more your demo resembles actual legal work, the more credible your product becomes.
  4. Prepare for the consolidation conversation. Buyers at both events are actively looking to reduce the number of vendors in their technology stack. If your product can replace multiple point solutions, make that case explicitly. If your product integrates seamlessly with dominant platforms like iManage, NetDocuments, or Microsoft 365, demonstrate those integrations live.
  5. Build your pipeline across back-to-back events. With Legalweek and ABA TECHSHOW occurring within two weeks of each other, many exhibitors attend both.
