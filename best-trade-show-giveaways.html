What Makes a Great Trade Show Giveaway

Not all swag is created equal. The most effective giveaways share a few key traits that separate memorable promotional items from the ones that end up in the hotel room trash can.

Usefulness Above All

The single biggest predictor of whether a giveaway will be kept is whether the recipient finds it useful. Items people can use immediately at the show -- tote bags, phone chargers, pens -- outperform novelty items every time. Ask yourself: would you keep this if someone handed it to you?

Brand Visibility

A great giveaway puts your logo where people will see it repeatedly. Tote bags carried around the show floor, water bottles on office desks, and phone accessories on devices all deliver ongoing impressions. Items that end up in drawers -- like keychains or generic pens -- deliver far fewer brand touches per dollar.

Relevance to Your Audience

Match your giveaway to your audience. Tech companies do well with power banks and USB drives. Healthcare companies can offer hand sanitizer and wellness items. Construction firms might give away tape measures or flashlights. The more relevant the item, the stronger the brand association.

Quality Over Quantity

It is better to give away 200 premium items to qualified prospects than 2,000 cheap trinkets to everyone who walks by. Reserve your best giveaways for people who engage in a real conversation or provide their contact information. Use an inexpensive candy bowl to attract foot traffic, and a higher-value item to reward engagement.

  • Tier 1 (everyone): Candy, stickers, basic pens -- items under $0.50
  • Tier 2 (engaged visitors): Tote bags, notebooks, stress balls -- items at $1-5
  • Tier 3 (qualified leads): Water bottles, power banks, USB drives -- items at $5+

Order Early

Custom promotional items typically require 2-4 weeks for production plus shipping time. Rush orders are available but cost 30-50% more. Place your order at least 6 weeks before the show to avoid expedited fees and ensure you receive exactly what you ordered.

Pro Tips

Strategies from experienced exhibitors on getting the most out of your giveaway budget.

Create a Giveaway Funnel

Display cheap items openly to attract foot traffic. Keep premium giveaways behind the counter and offer them only after a qualifying conversation. This turns your giveaway budget into a lead qualification tool rather than a cost center.

Add a Call to Action

Print more than your logo on giveaway items. Include your website URL, a QR code to your landing page, or a short tagline that explains what you do. The goal is to make it easy for someone to take the next step when they see your item weeks later.

Track Your ROI

Use unique URLs or promo codes on your giveaway items so you can track which shows and which items generate actual leads. Over time, this data tells you exactly which giveaways are worth reordering and which ones you should drop.

Ship Extras to the Hotel

Convention centers charge steep drayage fees for receiving freight. Ship a box of lightweight giveaways to your hotel instead and bring them in each morning. This can save $100-300 in handling fees for smaller quantities.
